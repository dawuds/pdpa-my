# GDPR Articles 37 / 38 / 39 — Data Protection Officer

> **Deep-dive reference.** Source: Regulation (EU) 2016/679 + EDPB Guidelines on DPOs (WP243rev.01 as adopted).

## When DPO appointment is mandatory — Article 37(1)

Three triggering categories:

| Trigger | Article |
|---|---|
| Public body or authority (except courts in judicial capacity) | 37(1)(a) |
| Core activities consist of large-scale regular and systematic monitoring | 37(1)(b) |
| Core activities consist of large-scale processing of special category (Article 9) or criminal data (Article 10) | 37(1)(c) |

Beyond mandatory triggers, voluntary appointment is permitted and increasingly common.

### "Core activities"

Per EDPB Guidelines: not "ancillary". IT and HR processing are usually ancillary to a manufacturer; data analytics is usually core to an AdTech firm.

### "Large-scale"

Not defined numerically. EDPB factors:

- Number of subjects
- Volume of data
- Range of data items
- Duration / permanence
- Geographic extent

Hospitals are typically large-scale by EDPB example; individual lawyers are not.

### "Regular and systematic monitoring"

Includes all forms of tracking and profiling, online or offline. Behavioural advertising, location tracking, fitness/wellness apps, smart-home devices, CCTV monitoring all qualify where at scale.

## Article 38 — position of the DPO

| Article | Requirement |
|---|---|
| 38(1) | Involved properly and in a timely manner in all issues related to personal data protection |
| 38(2) | Provided necessary resources to carry out tasks + maintain expertise |
| 38(3) | No instructions on exercise of tasks; cannot be dismissed or penalised for performing tasks; reports directly to highest management |
| 38(4) | Subjects may contact the DPO regarding processing and rights |
| 38(5) | Bound by secrecy / confidentiality |
| 38(6) | May fulfil other tasks; controller ensures no conflict of interest |

The 38(6) conflict of interest position is critical. EDPB Guidelines explicitly identify roles that create conflict:

- CEO
- COO / CFO
- Head of Marketing
- Head of HR
- Head of IT
- General Counsel (where advising on commercial decisions involving personal data)

A DPO who is also the senior decision-maker whose decisions the DPO must review cannot perform the role.

## Article 39 — tasks

The DPO's tasks:

1. Inform and advise the controller / processor + employees of their GDPR obligations
2. Monitor compliance with GDPR + other data protection laws + internal policies (assignment of responsibilities + training + audits)
3. Provide advice on DPIA upon request and monitor its performance (Article 35)
4. Cooperate with the SA
5. Act as the SA's contact point for processing-related matters, including Article 36 consultations

## Article 37(7) — publication and notification

The controller / processor must publish the DPO contact details (typically in the privacy notice) AND notify them to the SA. The notification is informational; the SA does not approve the appointment.

## DPO independence — practical implications

1. The DPO cannot be **instructed** on how to exercise tasks
2. The DPO cannot be **dismissed or penalised** for performing tasks
3. Performance evaluation must not create conflict (DPO compensation not tied to "no findings")
4. The DPO must have direct access to **highest management**

For audit work, these create specific test points: review the DPO appointment letter for independence language; review the DPO's role description for conflict-of-interest exclusions; review the DPO's reporting line; review the performance evaluation framework.

## Outsourced DPO

Article 37(6) permits external DPO appointment via service provider. The contract must specify the natural person responsible. The independence and conflict-of-interest tests apply to the named individual.

## Group DPO

Article 37(2) allows a single DPO for a group of undertakings, provided easily accessible from each. For multinational groups with substantial cross-border processing, a group DPO with regional deputies is common.

## Common implementation gaps

1. **Article 37 trigger not formally assessed** — DPO appointed or not appointed without documented assessment
2. **DPO is GC / CISO / CFO / Head of Marketing** — conflict of interest not assessed
3. **DPO reports to middle manager** — fails Article 38(3)
4. **External DPO without named individual**
5. **No Article 37(7) SA notification**
6. **No publication of DPO contact in privacy notice**
7. **DPO tasked with operational decisions** — conflict with monitoring role
8. **Performance KPI tied to no-findings** — creates conflict

## PDPA parallel

PDPA s12A (introduced by A1727) makes DPO appointment broader — mandatory for ALL controllers AND processors, not activity-triggered.

| GDPR | PDPA |
|---|---|
| Article 37(1) — activity-based mandatory trigger | s12A — universal for controllers and processors |
| Article 37(7) — published + SA notification | s12A — SPDP Commissioner notification |
| Article 38 independence + conflict | DPO Guideline 2025 |
| Article 39 tasks | DPO Guideline 2025 |
| Article 38(3) dismissal protection | DPO Guideline 2025 |

For dual-regime clients, PDPA's universal mandate means a DPO appointed for PDPA also satisfies GDPR where the GDPR trigger fires. The reverse — GDPR-triggered DPO not appointed where PDPA s12A applies — is a PDPA breach.
