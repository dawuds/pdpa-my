# Checkpoint — GDPR orientation self-test

> **Tier 1 / Lesson 5 — 10 minutes.** Ten questions. If you cannot answer eight, re-read the relevant lesson before moving to T2.

## Questions

1. **Scope — establishment arm.** State the test under Article 3(1) and identify the key CJEU case that broadened "establishment".

2. **Scope — targeting arm.** A Malaysian SaaS company has no EU establishment but has French customers. What test under Article 3(2) determines whether it is in scope?

3. **Penalty tiers.** Article 83 has two tiers. State the maxima for each and identify which tier applies to (a) Article 5 principle violation, (b) Article 33 breach notification failure.

4. **The six principles.** Name all six Article 5(1) principles plus the over-principle in Article 5(2).

5. **Lawful basis.** A controller wants to use customer data for fraud-prevention purposes. Which Article 6(1) basis is most likely to apply, and what is the three-part test the controller must pass?

6. **Conditions of consent.** State four of the six conditions under Article 7 for valid consent.

7. **Data subject rights.** Name the eight enumerated data subject rights and the GDPR Article for each.

8. **Article 22.** State the rule in Article 22(1) and the three exceptions in Article 22(2). What additional safeguards must the controller implement under Article 22(3) even when an exception applies?

9. **Citation hierarchy.** Rank the following from highest to lowest authority: (a) law firm client briefing, (b) CJEU decision, (c) Regulation text, (d) EDPB Guideline, (e) national SA guidance.

10. **PDPA parallel.** PDPA does not codify the GDPR accountability over-principle. Name three A1727 features that together create de facto accountability infrastructure even though the principle is not formally codified.

## Answer key

1. **Article 3(1)**: A controller or processor with an establishment in the Union is bound for processing in the context of that establishment, whether or not the processing takes place in the Union. Key CJEU case: **Weltimmo (C-230/14)** — interpreted "establishment" broadly; even a single representative with stable arrangements can constitute an establishment.

2. **Article 3(2)** — the targeting arm applies if the controller (a) offers goods or services to data subjects in the Union (regardless of payment) or (b) monitors data subjects' behaviour in the Union. Factors per EDPB Guidelines 3/2018: EU-language content, EU currency, EU country-specific TLD, references to EU users, EU advertising spend.

3. **Tier 1** (Article 83(4)): up to **10M EUR or 2% of worldwide annual turnover**. **Tier 2** (Article 83(5)): up to **20M EUR or 4% of worldwide annual turnover**. Article 5 principle violation → Tier 2. Article 33 breach notification failure → Tier 1.

4. **Six principles**: (1) Lawfulness, fairness, transparency; (2) Purpose limitation; (3) Data minimisation; (4) Accuracy; (5) Storage limitation; (6) Integrity and confidentiality. **Over-principle**: Accountability (Article 5(2)).

5. **Article 6(1)(f) legitimate interests**. Three-part test: (a) purpose test (is there a legitimate interest?); (b) necessity test (is the processing necessary, not just useful, for that interest?); (c) balancing test (do the data subject's interests, rights, and freedoms override the legitimate interest, considering reasonable expectations, age, context, data nature, and consequences?). Documentation requirement: a Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA).

6. Any four of: freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous, withdrawable, demonstrable. (Additional condition for special category: explicit.)

7. **Eight rights**: Information (Articles 13, 14); Access (Article 15); Rectification (Article 16); Erasure (Article 17); Restriction of processing (Article 18); Notification (Article 19); Portability (Article 20); Object (Article 21); plus the right relating to solely automated decisions (Article 22).

8. **Article 22(1)**: The data subject has the right not to be subject to a decision based **solely** on automated processing, including profiling, which produces **legal effects** concerning him/her or **similarly significantly affects** him/her. **Exceptions (Article 22(2))**: (a) necessary for entering into / performing a contract; (b) authorised by Union or Member State law; (c) based on explicit consent. **Safeguards (Article 22(3))**: even under an exception, the controller must implement the right to obtain human intervention, express a point of view, and contest the decision.

9. From highest to lowest: **(c) Regulation text > (b) CJEU decision > (d) EDPB Guideline > (e) national SA guidance > (a) law firm client briefing**. Note: CJEU can override EDPB and SA positions; commentary is persuasive only.

10. Three A1727 features creating de facto accountability: (a) **RoPA under s138** — documentation of processing activities; (b) **DPIA under s137** — risk-based assessment for high-risk processing; (c) **DPO under s12A** — designated accountable individual. Together these create demonstrable evidence even without GDPR Article 5(2)'s explicit obligation.
