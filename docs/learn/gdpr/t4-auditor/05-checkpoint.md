# T4 GDPR Checkpoint — produce a GDPR finding

> **Tier 4 / Lesson 5 — 120 minutes.** Parallel to PDPA T4 Checkpoint. Deliverable: a sample GDPR finding on the equivalent obligation + side-by-side PDPA finding.

## The exercise

Pick an article. Walk to a fully-formed finding. Produce the parallel PDPA finding on the equivalent obligation. Self-grade.

## Side-by-side parallel finding

The deliverable is a single sample finding produced in BOTH PDPA and GDPR formats — same substantive observation; different citation framing.

Example: a controller fails to disclose automated decision-making in the privacy notice (the worked example from T4 Lesson 4).

### PDPA version

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Identifier | F-DAC-NT-001-PDPA |
| Citation | JPDP ADMP Guideline §9.3 (mandatory FACT-of-ADM disclosure) + Act 709 s7(1)(b) Notice obligation |
| Severity | High |
| Penalty exposure | s5(3) Principle violation: RM1M / 3y individual; RM3M or 10% Malaysian turnover (s130); officer (s133A) |
| Status flag | ISSUED REQUIREMENT |
| Module trail | pdpa-my/automated-decisions/data-subject-rights.json + pdpa-my/principles/notice-and-choice.json |

### GDPR version

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Identifier | F-DAC-NT-001-GDPR |
| Citation | Article 13(2)(f) + Article 22(1) + EDPB Guidelines on Automated Individual Decision-Making (WP251rev.01, adopted 6 February 2018) + CJEU C-26/22 + C-64/22 SCHUFA Holding (2023) for Article 22 broader trigger |
| Severity | High |
| Penalty exposure | Article 83(5)(b) — 20M EUR or 4% global turnover |
| Article 82 civil exposure | Yes — recent class actions emerging |
| Status flag | ISSUED REQUIREMENT |

The PDPA finding cites local infrastructure (module trail, BM PDF). The GDPR finding cites EUR-Lex, EDPB Guidelines (version-pinned), and CJEU decisions (date-pinned). The substantive observation — privacy notice fails to disclose automated decision-making — is identical.

## The transition out of GDPR T4

Same as PDPA T4 — graduates execute procedures, draft findings, apply citation discipline, self-grade against the rubric.

The pre-T4 finding citation error rate is similar between regimes (around 35-50% have at least one major citation error). The post-T4 target is <10%.

## Continuing professional development

For GDPR specifically:

| Item | Cadence |
|---|---|
| Read new CJEU decisions on GDPR | Continuous — set a CJEU search |
| Review new EDPB Guidelines / Opinions | As issued |
| Monitor national SA enforcement actions | Quarterly minimum (CNIL, ICO, DPC, Garante, AEPD, DSK) |
| Refresh on the Member State implementation variation | Annual minimum |
| Review NIS2 transposition in scoped jurisdictions | As issued |
| Cross-jurisdictional updates (PDPA, UK GDPR, US state laws, China PIPL, Japan APPI) | Continuous |

The post-T4 GDPR consultant should be a daily reader of GDPR developments. The regime evolves continuously and findings against last-month's position can be outdated.

## What's next

T4 GDPR is the final structured tier. Beyond T4, GDPR consultants operate under engagement-manager and partner supervision on real engagements.

For dual-regime consultants, completing both PDPA T4 and GDPR T4 unlocks the side-by-side finding capability — producing findings in both flavors on a single audit. This is the operational target for cross-border privacy consultants.
