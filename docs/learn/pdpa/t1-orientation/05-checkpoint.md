# Checkpoint — orientation self-test

> **Tier 1 / Lesson 5 — 10 minutes.** Ten questions. If you cannot answer eight, re-read the relevant lesson before moving to T2.

## Questions

1. **Scope.** Act 709 binds a person processing personal data "in respect of a [...]". Fill in the blank, and state the major carve-out.

2. **Penalty.** A controller commits a Principle violation (s5(3)) post-A1727. What are the maximum penalty exposures for (a) the individual, (b) the corporate, (c) the officer personally?

3. **Principles.** Name all 7 PDP Principles and their statutory section. (Hint: s6 through s12.)

4. **A1727 — new rights.** A1727 expanded data subject rights beyond access + correction. Name the three new rights and their section numbers.

5. **Breach notification.** s12B mandates breach notification. State the timing to (a) the Commissioner and (b) data subjects, and the source for those timings.

6. **Sectoral CoP.** A bank licensed under FSA 2013 has a privacy programme. Which Code of Practice is the primary citation source for the finding — General CoP 2022 or Banking & Financial Sector CoP? Why?

7. **Issued vs PCP — DPIA.** The PCP proposed three quantitative thresholds for mandatory DPIA. The issued April 2026 guideline has two. Which one was dropped, and where did it go?

8. **Issued vs PCP — DPbD.** The PCP proposed seven foundational principles. The issued guideline lists how many elements, and what are they (in BM or EN)?

9. **Issued vs PCP — ADMP.** The PCP proposed three named ADM Restrictions. What did the issued guideline replace them with? Specifically, what does §9.3 mandate and what does §9.4 make permissive?

10. **NACSA clash.** Your client is an NCII bank. The same incident is notifiable under both PDPA s12B and NACSA Act 854 s26. What are the two clocks, and which expires first?

## Answer key

1. **"Commercial transaction"** (Act 709 s2). Major carve-out: **federal and state government** acting in their non-commercial capacity.

2. (a) **RM1,000,000 / 3 years** (s5(3) post-A1727). (b) **RM3,000,000 or 10% of annual Malaysian turnover, whichever is higher** (s130). (c) **Same as corporate** (s133A officer personal liability).

3. General (s6), Notice and Choice (s7), Disclosure (s8), Security (s9), Retention (s10), Data Integrity (s11), Access (s12).

4. **Erasure (s43)**, **Data Portability (s43A)**, **Restriction of Processing (s44)**. All introduced by A1727 Phase 2 (1 April 2025).

5. (a) **72 hours** to the Commissioner. (b) **7 days** to data subjects where significant harm likely. (c) Source: **JPDP Data Breach Notification Guideline 2025** (the Act itself says "as soon as practicable"; the guideline defines the clocks).

6. **The Banking & Financial Sector CoP.** The bank is in the Class of Data Users for which a sectoral CoP is registered under s23. Where General CoP and sectoral CoP both address the same matter, the sectoral CoP prevails on inconsistency. Cite the Banking CoP as primary; cite General CoP only for matters the sector-specific code does not address.

7. **Automated decision-making >10,000 subjects** (PCP §2.10(b)) was dropped from the quantitative thresholds. It became **Qualitative Factor Q7** ("automated decision-making and profiling that may give rise to high risk to the data subject" — issued §7.6(g)).

8. **Four elements**: Sifat Proaktif (Proactiveness — folds in privacy-as-default), Perlindungan Hujung-ke-hujung (End-to-end Protection), Ketelusan (Transparency), Berpusatkan Pengguna (User-centricity). The Cavoukian "Privacy Embedded into Design" and "Full Functionality / Positive-Sum" principles are not named.

9. The issued guideline replaced the three named ADM Restrictions (Refuse / Information / Human Review) with **Section 7 notice and Section 38 withdrawal of consent**. **§9.3** mandates the FACT of ADM disclosure (that ADM/profiling is involved). **§9.4** makes permissive (`boleh`) the explanation of logic, reasons, and likely impact — subject to carve-outs for confidential information, trade secrets, IP, and proprietary rights.

10. **NACSA 6 hours** to NC4 (Act 854 s26) and **PDPA 72 hours** to the Commissioner (A1727 s12B + JPDP DBN Guideline). **NACSA expires first** — by a wide margin. Programme design must reconcile these in advance.
