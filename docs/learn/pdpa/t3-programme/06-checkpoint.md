# T3 PDPA Checkpoint — programme readiness review

> **Tier 3 / Lesson 6 — 90 minutes.** Deliverable: a defensible 24-hour JPDP inspection pack containing DPO appointment, RoPA, DPIA register, breach response plan, privacy notice, training records, and ADM register.

## What a JPDP inspection looks for in the first 24 hours

When a JPDP inspector arrives or sends a written request under s101 / s103, the documents requested first are typically:

| # | Document | Required by |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Certificate of registration as data controller | s13-s16 |
| 2 | DPO appointment letter + Commissioner notification record | s12A + Aug 2025 DPO Guideline |
| 3 | Senior accountability assignment (DPbD §13) | DPbD Guideline §13 |
| 4 | Records of Processing Activities (RoPA) | s138 |
| 5 | Privacy notice(s) — all current and recent versions | s7 |
| 6 | DPIA register + sample DPIA records | s137 + DPIA Guideline |
| 7 | Breach response plan + breach register | s12B + DBN Guideline 2025 |
| 8 | Data subject rights request log (DSARs / correction / erasure / portability / restriction / withdrawal) | s30, s34, s38, s43, s43A, s44 |
| 9 | Cross-border transfer register + TIA records + SCC / BCR documents | s129 / s129A + CBPDT Guideline 2025 |
| 10 | Processor / DPA inventory | s9 + s12A |
| 11 | Staff training records | s9 + Aug 2025 DPO Guideline |
| 12 | ADM register (if any ADMP processing) | ADMP Guideline §5, §6, §7 |
| 13 | Sectoral CoP self-assessment (if applicable) | s23 |

A controller that produces these 13 documents within 24 hours has passed the inspection-readiness bar. A controller that cannot is exposed under multiple statutory duties.

## The deliverable

For a client where the consulting team has stood up the programme through T1-T3, the deliverable is the **inspection-ready pack** — these 13 documents, organised, version-controlled, and accessible.

## Rubric — by document

| # | Document | Pass criterion |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Registration certificate | Current and not expired; covers the right Class of Data Users |
| 2 | DPO appointment | s12A-compliant; Commissioner notification filed; reporting line independent |
| 3 | Senior accountability assignment | Named Head of Department with documented authority under DPbD §13 |
| 4 | RoPA | All processing activities covered; all 8 s138 elements per activity; sensitive PD flagged; cross-border flows identified |
| 5 | Privacy notice | s7(1) elements all present; BM + EN; layered structure; current version + recent prior versions |
| 6 | DPIA register | Sample DPIA executed using DEICA + Lampiran A; 2-year validity tracked; HIGH residual escalated to senior management |
| 7 | Breach response plan | Section 1 / 2 / 3 phases mapped; DPO + IR + Legal + Communications + Customer Service roles defined; tabletop AAR within last 12 months |
| 8 | DSR log | Each right tracked separately; response times within statutory windows; refusals justified |
| 9 | Cross-border register | Per-vendor entry; TIA documented; mechanism selected per s129 / s129A |
| 10 | DPA inventory | DPA in place for every processor; s9 obligations cascaded contractually; sub-processor consent procedures |
| 11 | Training records | Annual training minimum; completion rate by team; DPO-led refresher within last 12 months |
| 12 | ADM register | Per ADM operation; Ambang ADMP assessment; DPIA cross-reference; §9.3 disclosure flowed through to notice |
| 13 | Sectoral CoP self-assessment | Where applicable; current CoP version cited; gap analysis against CoP requirements |

## Scoring

| Score | Action |
|---|---|
| 12-13 of 13 documents pass | **Ready for inspection.** Move to T4 (Auditor) |
| 9-11 | Programme is mostly complete with isolated gaps; close the specific gaps before T4 |
| 6-8 | Programme has material gaps; return to T3 lessons for the weakest documents |
| ≤5 | Programme is not yet operational; return to T2 |

## What "defensible" means in practice

A document is defensible if:

1. **It exists** — basic test; many gaps fail here
2. **It is current** — within the appropriate update cycle (annual minimum for most; per-change for some)
3. **It is referenced** — other documents cite it where the obligation requires (DPO appointment referenced in the privacy notice; RoPA referenced in the DPIA register; etc.)
4. **It is locatable** — the team can produce it within 1-2 hours, not 24
5. **It cites authority** — references the specific section, regulation, or guideline that requires it
6. **It is signed off** — DPO sign-off where required; senior management sign-off where DPbD §13 applies

A document that exists but is uncurrent, unreferenced, unlocatable, or unsigned-off is partially defensible at best.

## Common gaps in inspection-ready packs

The patterns the audit team catches across client engagements:

1. **DPO appointment letter exists; Commissioner notification not filed** — separate s12A breach. Common.
2. **RoPA partial** — top processing activities documented; long-tail activities (employee health screening, vendor onboarding, marketing analytics) missing
3. **Privacy notice covers s7(1) but not §9.3 ADMP disclosure** — post-April 2026 notice update gap
4. **DPIA register has DPIAs but no 2-year validity tracking** — DPIA may have been done in 2024 but is now stale
5. **Breach response plan exists but no tabletop in last 12 months** — plan is theoretical
6. **Cross-border register exists for major SaaS but not for sub-processors** — flow analysis incomplete
7. **Training records show 100% completion** — but the completion is rote click-through; competence is not evidenced
8. **ADM register missing** — controller has automated decisions in production but no register exists
9. **Sectoral CoP self-assessment cites 2017 General CoP** — stale; should be 2022 + relevant sectoral

## The post-T3 transition to T4 (Auditor)

Completing T3 means the consultant has produced the artefacts. T4 is about **auditing** the artefacts — testing whether they survive scrutiny.

The transition: a T3 graduate can build the programme. A T4 graduate can audit it. Many consultants stop at T3 and rely on the engagement partner for audit work. The team's expectation is that all consultants work through T4 even if their primary work is build-side — auditing forces a higher standard of construction.

## What's next

If 12-13 documents pass, move to [T4 Lesson 1 — Anatomy of a PDPA audit pack](../t4-auditor/01-audit-pack-anatomy.md).

If 9-11 pass, close the gaps then move to T4.

If below 9, identify the weakest lessons in T3 and re-do those programmes before moving to T4.

The GDPR T3 checkpoint at [GDPR T3 Lesson 6](../../gdpr/t3-programme/06-checkpoint.md) is the parallel; many consultants run both checkpoints in parallel for dual-regime clients.
