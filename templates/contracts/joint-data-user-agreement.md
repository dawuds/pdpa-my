- The respective compliance responsibilities of each Party under the PDPA;
- The allocation of obligations with respect to data subject rights, breach notification, cross-border transfers, and data security;
- The designated lead Party for specified compliance functions; and
- The liability and indemnification framework between the Parties arising from joint processing activities.

## 6. Breach Notification and Response Coordination

*A coordinated breach response is essential to comply with the PDPA, meet any BNM reporting requirements, and minimise reputational and legal exposure. This section must be read alongside each Party's internal Incident Response Plan.*

### 6.1 Breach Identification and Initial Notification

Upon discovering or suspecting a personal data breach affecting jointly processed data, the discovering Party shall:

1. immediately activate its internal incident response procedures;
2. notify the other Party's DPO within **[4] hours** of discovery via the contacts in Schedule 1;
3. provide an initial incident report containing, at minimum:
   - date and time of discovery;
   - nature and scope of the breach (data categories, estimated number of data subjects affected);
   - likely cause and systems affected;
   - immediate containment actions taken; and
   - name and contact of incident lead.

### 6.2 Joint Incident Response

Upon notification of a breach, the Parties shall convene a joint incident response call within **[8] hours** to:

- agree on a joint incident response team and designated incident coordinator (default: **[Party A / Party B]**);
- assess the severity and likely regulatory notification threshold;
- coordinate technical containment and forensic investigation activities;
- agree a communications strategy for affected data subjects; and
- document all response actions in a joint incident log.

### 6.3 Regulatory Notification

| Regulatory Body | Threshold | Responsible Party | Notification Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Personal Data Protection Department (PDPD) | [As prescribed or upon material breach] | [Lead Party / Jointly] | [Prescribed timeline / as agreed] |
| Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) — if applicable | As per BNM Risk Management guidelines | [Party A / Party B] | As per BNM requirements |
| [Other Regulator] | [Threshold] | [Responsible Party] | [Timeline] |

The Parties shall not make any unilateral regulatory notification relating to jointly processed data without prior written agreement of the other Party, except where immediate notification is required by law and prior consultation is not practicable.

### 6.4 Data Subject Notification

Where notification to affected data subjects is required or warranted:

- the **Lead Party** (as designated in Section 4.1) shall coordinate and issue data subject notifications;
- the non-lead Party shall provide all information reasonably required to ensure accurate, complete notification within **[4] hours** of request;
- both Parties shall agree on notification content prior to issue; and
- costs of data subject notification shall be borne as specified in Section 9.

### 6.5 Post-Incident Review

Within **[30] calendar days** of resolution of any material breach, the Parties shall conduct a joint post-incident review and prepare a written lessons-learned report addressing root cause, remediation actions, and any revisions required to this Agreement or either Party's security controls.

## 7. Data Security Responsibilities and Minimum Standards

*Both Parties share accountability for the security of jointly processed data. This section defines the minimum baseline controls each Party must maintain and the process for verifying compliance.*

### 7.1 Security Principle Obligations (PDPA s8)

Each Party shall implement and maintain **practical steps** to protect personal data from loss, misuse, modification, unauthorised or accidental access or disclosure, alteration, or destruction, consistent with the PDPA Security Principle and the PDPA (Personal Data Protection Standards) Regulations 2012.

### 7.2 Minimum Security Standards

Both Parties agree to maintain, as a minimum, the following technical and organisational security controls in respect of jointly processed personal data:

| Control Domain | Minimum Requirement | Responsible Party |
|---|---|---|
| **Access Control** | Role-based access; least privilege; MFA for privileged accounts | [Both Parties] |
| **Encryption** | Encryption at rest (AES-256 or equivalent) and in transit (TLS 1.2+) | [Both Parties] |
| **Audit Logging** | System access and data processing logs retained for minimum [12] months | [Both Parties] |
| **Vulnerability Management** | Patching critical vulnerabilities within [30] days; penetration testing [annually] | [Both Parties] |
| **Network Security** | Firewalls, IDS/IPS, network segmentation for shared systems | [Specify by Party] |
| **Data Minimisation** | Processing only data necessary for the specified purpose | [Both Parties] |
| **Physical Security** | ISO 27001-aligned physical access controls for data processing facilities | [Both Parties] |
| **Endpoint Security** | Anti-malware, device encryption, mobile device management | [Both Parties] |
| **Third-party Risk** | Due diligence on sub-processors; contractual security obligations | [Engaging Party] |
| **Staff Awareness** | Annual PDPA and data security training for all staff with access | [Both Parties] |

### 7.3 Security Assessments and Audits

- Each Party shall, upon **[30] calendar days** written notice, permit the other Party (or its authorised third-party auditor) to conduct a security audit of systems and processes used to process shared personal data, no more than **[once per calendar year]** unless a breach has occurred.
- Each Party shall promptly remediate any material security gap identified during such an audit and provide written confirmation of remediation within **[60] calendar days**.
- The Parties may agree to accept each other's ISO 27001 certification, SOC 2 Type II reports, or equivalent third-party audit reports in lieu of a direct audit.

## 10. Transparency Requirements to Data Subjects

*Data subjects have a right to know who is processing their data and on what basis. Where personal data is jointly processed, both Parties must ensure their privacy notices are accurate and that data subjects are not misled about who is accountable.*

### 10.1 Privacy Notice Obligations (PDPA s6)

The Parties shall ensure that data subjects whose personal data is jointly processed receive a clear, accessible, and accurate privacy notice that:

- identifies **both** Parties as data users in relation to the jointly processed data, or identifies the Lead Party and discloses the existence of the joint processing arrangement;
- describes the purposes for which the personal data is jointly processed;
- discloses the categories of third parties (including sub-processors) to whom personal data may be disclosed;
- explains how data subjects may exercise their rights, including the Lead Party contact details; and
- is made available **prior to or at the time of** collection of personal data from data subjects.

### 10.2 Privacy Notice Review and Approval

### 10.3 Changes to Processing and Notice Updates

## 11. Term, Termination, and Data Handling on Exit

### 11.1 Term

This Agreement shall commence on the **Effective Date** and shall remain in force until **[End Date / indefinitely until terminated]**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Section.

### 11.2 Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement:

- by providing **[30] calendar days** written notice to the other Party;
- immediately, upon written notice, if the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement or the PDPA that is not remedied within **[14] calendar days** of written notice; or
- immediately, if the other Party is subject to insolvency proceedings, dissolution, or ceases to carry on business.

### 11.3 Effect of Termination — Data Handling

Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement, the Parties shall, within **[30] calendar days**:

| Action | Responsible Party | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Cease all joint processing activities | Both Parties | Immediately upon termination |
