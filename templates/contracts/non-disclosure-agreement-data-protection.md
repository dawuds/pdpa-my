## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*Describe the overall purpose of this agreement, including the engagement context and the regulatory basis under which data protection obligations are imposed.*

This Non-Disclosure Agreement (Data Protection) ("Agreement") establishes the confidentiality and data protection obligations applicable to [Contractor/Consultant/Professional Adviser] who, in the course of their engagement with [Organization Name], may access, process, handle, or otherwise come into contact with confidential information, including Personal Data as defined under the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA), Malaysia** and related subsidiary legislation.

This Agreement is executed in compliance with:

- **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)** — in particular **Section 9 (Security Principle)**, which requires that a data processor shall not process personal data unless the data controller has taken practical steps to ensure the personal data will be protected from any loss, misuse, modification, unauthorised or accidental access or disclosure, alteration or destruction
- **Personal Data Protection Regulations 2013**
- [Organization Name]'s **Data Protection Policy** [Document ID Reference]
- [Organization Name]'s **Information Security Policy** [Document ID Reference]

### 1.2 Scope

This Agreement applies to:

- All contractors, consultants, freelancers, and professional advisers (including legal, audit, and financial advisers) engaged by [Organization Name]
- Any personnel, employees, sub-contractors, or agents of the Receiving Party who are granted access to Confidential Information under this engagement
- All forms of Confidential Information, whether disclosed orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, before or after the date of this Agreement

This Agreement does **not** supersede or replace any existing confidentiality obligations in the Principal Service Agreement or engagement letter between the Parties; rather, it supplements those obligations with specific data protection requirements.

## 4. Permitted Disclosure Purposes

*Specify the narrow and limited purposes for which the Receiving Party may use or disclose Confidential Information. This must be strictly scoped to the engagement. Add further restrictions as required by the nature of the engagement.*

### 4.1 Limited Use Obligation

The Receiving Party shall use Confidential Information, including Personal Data, **solely and exclusively** for the Permitted Purpose as defined in this Agreement and as described in Schedule 1. Any use beyond the Permitted Purpose is strictly prohibited without prior written consent from the Disclosing Party.

### 4.2 Permitted Disclosures

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to:

| Recipient | Condition | Approval Required |
|---|---|---|
| Receiving Party's own employees or personnel | Only those who have a strict need-to-know for the Permitted Purpose; must be bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations | Internal authorisation by Receiving Party; notification to Disclosing Party |
| Receiving Party's professional advisers | Bound by professional duty of confidentiality | Prior written consent from Disclosing Party |
| Regulatory or law enforcement authorities | Only as required by applicable law or court order | Written notice to Disclosing Party prior to disclosure where legally permissible |
| Sub-contractors or sub-processors | Bound by written agreement with equivalent obligations | Prior written consent from Disclosing Party |

### 4.3 Prohibition on Secondary Use

The Receiving Party shall **not**:

- (a) Use Personal Data for any commercial purpose, marketing, profiling, or analytics outside the scope of the Engagement;
- (b) Combine Personal Data received under this Agreement with any other data set held by the Receiving Party;
- (c) Transfer or disclose Personal Data to any third party not listed in Clause 4.2 above;
- (d) Transfer Personal Data outside of Malaysia without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party and in compliance with the PDPA transfer restrictions.

## 5. Prohibition on Copying, Retaining, or Disclosing Personal Data

*Specify restrictions on reproduction and retention. These restrictions should be operationally practical and enforceable. Where specific systems or processes are used, reference them.*

### 5.1 Copying Restrictions

The Receiving Party shall **not** copy, reproduce, extract, download, or duplicate any Personal Data or Confidential Information beyond what is strictly necessary for the Permitted Purpose. All copies made in the course of the Engagement shall:

- (a) Be subject to the same obligations as the original;
- (b) Be tracked and inventoried by the Receiving Party;
- (c) Be disclosed to the Disclosing Party upon request.

### 5.2 Retention Restrictions

The Receiving Party shall not retain any Personal Data beyond the duration required to fulfil the Permitted Purpose and shall in any event not retain Personal Data beyond the Engagement End Date, except where:

- (a) Retention is required by applicable Malaysian law; or
- (b) The Disclosing Party has given express written consent for a specified extended period.

### 5.3 Prohibition on Unauthorised Disclosure

The Receiving Party shall maintain strict confidentiality over all Personal Data and shall not, directly or indirectly:

- Disclose, publish, communicate, or transmit Personal Data to any unauthorised person;
- Allow unauthorised persons to access systems, premises, or media containing Personal Data;
- Use Personal Data in any manner that could harm, embarrass, or prejudice any Data Subject;
- Disclose the existence or terms of this Agreement to any third party without prior written consent.

## 6. Security Obligations

*Detail the technical and organisational security measures the Receiving Party must implement. Align these with the Organization's own security standards and any applicable frameworks (e.g., ISO 27001, BNM RMiT). Reference the Organization's Information Security Policy.*

### 6.1 Minimum Security Standards

The Receiving Party shall implement and maintain, at a minimum, the following security measures when handling Personal Data or Confidential Information under this Agreement:

**6.1.1 Technical Controls**

| Control | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Encryption | All Personal Data stored electronically must be encrypted using [AES-256 or equivalent]; data in transit must use TLS 1.2 or higher |
| Access Control | Access to Personal Data restricted to named, authorised individuals on a need-to-know basis; multi-factor authentication required for remote access |
| Device Security | Devices used to process Personal Data must have full-disk encryption, up-to-date antivirus, and automatic screen lock |
| Network Security | Personal Data shall not be processed over unsecured public Wi-Fi without VPN; firewall and intrusion detection controls must be in place |
| Logging & Monitoring | Access to Personal Data systems must be logged and audit trails retained for a minimum of [12 months] |
| Data Loss Prevention | Controls to prevent unauthorised exfiltration of Personal Data (e.g., email DLP, USB restrictions) |

**6.1.2 Organisational Controls**

- Security awareness training for all personnel accessing Personal Data
- Background screening completed for personnel with access to sensitive Personal Data
- Clear desk and clear screen policy in effect at all times
- Physical access controls where Personal Data is handled in physical form
- Formal onboarding and offboarding procedures for personnel under the Engagement

## 7. Obligation to Report Data Incidents

*Define clear incident reporting obligations, timelines, and escalation procedures. Timelines should reflect internal SLAs and any applicable regulatory notification requirements under PDPA or BNM Guidelines.*

### 7.1 Immediate Notification

The Receiving Party **must** notify [Organization Name]'s designated contact (see Clause 7.4) **immediately and without undue delay**, and in any event **within [4 hours]** of becoming aware of a confirmed or suspected Data Incident involving Personal Data received under this Agreement.

### 7.2 Incident Report Contents

Initial notification must include, to the extent known:

- (a) Date and time the incident was discovered;
- (b) Nature of the incident (e.g., unauthorised access, data loss, ransomware, accidental disclosure);
- (c) Categories and approximate volume of Personal Data affected;
- (d) Data Subject categories affected;
- (e) Likely consequences of the breach;
- (f) Measures taken or proposed to address and mitigate the incident.

A full written incident report must be provided to [Organization Name] within **[24 hours]** of initial notification, using the form in **Appendix B** (Data Incident Report Form).

### 7.3 Ongoing Obligations

Following notification, the Receiving Party shall:

- (a) Cooperate fully with [Organization Name]'s investigation and response team;
- (b) Preserve all evidence and logs related to the incident;
- (c) Not make any public statement, press release, or regulatory notification relating to the incident without prior written consent from [Organization Name];
- (d) Implement corrective measures as directed by [Organization Name];
- (e) Provide regular updates until the incident is fully resolved and closed.

## 8. Return or Destruction of Personal Data on Engagement End

*Specify the process for returning or securely destroying all Personal Data and Confidential Information at the end of the engagement. Include verification requirements.*

### 8.1 Obligations on Engagement End

Upon the Engagement End Date, or upon written request from [Organization Name] at any time, the Receiving Party shall, within **[14 calendar days]**:

- (a) **Return** all Personal Data and Confidential Information to [Organization Name] in a format and medium specified by [Organization Name]; **AND/OR**
- (b) **Permanently destroy** all Personal Data and Confidential Information, including all copies, extracts, and reproductions, in a manner that renders recovery impossible, in accordance with the standards set out in Clause 8.2.

### 8.2 Approved Destruction Methods

| Data Form | Approved Destruction Method |
|---|---|
| Electronic data (files, databases) | Secure overwriting (DoD 5220.22-M standard or equivalent) or cryptographic erasure |
| Portable media (USB, external drives) | Physical destruction by certified media destruction service |
| Paper / printed documents | Cross-cut shredding (DIN 66399 Security Level P-4 or higher) or certified destruction service |
| Email and messaging data | Permanent deletion from all mail servers, archives, and backup systems |
| Cloud-hosted data | Verified deletion from cloud provider with written confirmation |

### 8.3 Certificate of Destruction

Upon completion of return or destruction, the Receiving Party shall provide [Organization Name] with a signed **Certificate of Destruction / Return** (using the form in **Appendix C**) confirming:

- (a) All Personal Data and Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed;
- (b) All copies, backups, and reproductions have been included;
- (c) The method of destruction used; and
- (d) The date of completion.

### 8.4 Retention Exceptions

Where the Receiving Party is legally required to retain certain records containing Personal Data beyond the Engagement End Date, it shall:

- (a) Notify [Organization Name] in writing prior to the Engagement End Date, specifying the legal basis and retention period;
- (b) Restrict access to such retained data to the minimum necessary;
- (c) Destroy such data as soon as the legal retention period expires;
- (d) Continue to apply all obligations under this Agreement to such retained data.

## 9. Personal Liability of Individual Signatories

*This section addresses the personal accountability of individuals who sign this Agreement, particularly for sole proprietors, consultants, or named representatives of corporate entities. Legal counsel should review jurisdiction-specific enforceability.*

### 9.1 Personal Accountability — Individual Contractors

Where the Receiving Party is an **individual** (including a sole proprietor, freelance consultant, or independent professional adviser), that individual acknowledges that:

- (a) They are personally bound by all obligations under this Agreement;
- (b) Personal liability attaches to them directly for any breach of this Agreement or violation of the PDPA 2010;
- (c) Obligations under this Agreement survive the termination of the Engagement and continue for a period of **[3 years]** following the Engagement End Date, or indefinitely with respect to Personal Data obligations.

### 9.2 Personal Accountability — Named Representatives of Corporate Entities

Where the Receiving Party is a **corporate entity**, the individual executing this Agreement on its behalf:

- (a) Warrants that they have full authority to bind the corporate entity to this Agreement;
- (b) Acknowledges that execution creates binding obligations on the corporate entity and its personnel;
- (c) Undertakes to ensure that all employees, sub-contractors, and agents of the corporate entity who access Confidential Information are made aware of and bound by equivalent obligations.

### 9.3 Personnel Obligations

The Receiving Party shall ensure that all personnel with access to Confidential Information:

- (a) Have read and understood this Agreement prior to being granted access;
- (b) Have signed an individual acknowledgement (see **Appendix D** — Personnel Acknowledgement Form);
- (c) Are made aware that breaches may result in disciplinary action and personal legal liability under the PDPA 2010 and applicable Malaysian law.

### 9.4 Post-Engagement Obligations

The obligations under this Agreement, including confidentiality and data protection obligations, shall **survive** the termination or expiry of this Agreement and the Engagement for:

- (a) **Personal Data obligations** — Indefinitely, or for so long as the Personal Data is retained by [Organization Name];
- (b) **General confidentiality obligations** — A period of **[3 years]** from the Engagement End Date;
- (c) **Return/Destruction obligations** — Until the Certificate of Destruction has been accepted by [Organization Name].
