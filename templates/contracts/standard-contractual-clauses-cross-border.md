## 1. Purpose and Scope

*This section explains the legal basis and applicability of these Standard Contractual Clauses. It should identify the regulatory framework, the transfer mechanism being relied upon, and the scope of personal data covered by this agreement.*

These Standard Contractual Clauses ("**SCCs**" or "**Clauses**") are entered into pursuant to **Section 129A of the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)** (Act 709) of Malaysia and the Personal Data Protection Commissioner's guidance on cross-border data transfer mechanisms.

These Clauses apply where the Data Exporter, as a data user operating in Malaysia, transfers personal data to a Data Importer located in **[Recipient Country]**, which has not been designated by the Minister as having adequate personal data protection laws equivalent to the PDPA.

These Clauses constitute appropriate safeguards for the cross-border transfer of personal data and are legally binding on both Parties. In the event of any conflict between these Clauses and any commercial agreement between the Parties, these Clauses shall prevail with respect to data protection obligations.

**Applicable Regulatory Framework:**

| Instrument | Provision | Subject Matter |
|---|---|---|
| Personal Data Protection Act 2010 | Section 129A | Cross-border transfer mechanism |
| Personal Data Protection Act 2010 | Section 6 | Security principle |
| Personal Data Protection Act 2010 | Section 8 | Access principle |
| Personal Data Protection Act 2010 | Section 9 | Correction principle |
| PDPC Transfer Guidance | [Reference] | Commissioner's approved clauses |

## 5. Obligations of the Data Exporter

*This section sets out the Data Exporter's binding obligations to ensure the transfer is lawful prior to and during the transfer period. The Data Exporter remains accountable under the PDPA for transfers made to the Data Importer.*

The Data Exporter warrants and undertakes that:

5.1 The transfer of personal data to the Data Importer is permitted under the PDPA 2010 and these Clauses constitute appropriate safeguards under Section 129A.

5.2 The data subjects have been notified of the cross-border transfer in accordance with the PDPA's Notice and Choice Principle, including the identity of the recipient country.

5.3 The personal data transferred is accurate, complete, and limited to what is necessary for the stated transfer purposes (data minimisation).

5.4 The Data Exporter has conducted reasonable due diligence on the Data Importer's ability to comply with these Clauses prior to transfer commencement.

5.5 The Data Exporter shall promptly inform the Data Importer of any changes to applicable Malaysian data protection law that may affect the Data Importer's obligations under these Clauses.

5.6 The Data Exporter shall respond to data subject requests, including access and correction requests, within the timeframes prescribed by the PDPA, and may rely on the Data Importer's cooperation as specified in Section 7.

5.7 The Data Exporter shall maintain records of all cross-border transfers made pursuant to these Clauses and make such records available to the Personal Data Protection Commissioner upon request.

## 7. Data Subject Rights and Third-Party Beneficiary Rights

*These Clauses must be enforceable by data subjects as third-party beneficiaries. This section specifies the rights data subjects can invoke directly against the Data Importer and the mechanism for doing so.*

### 7.1 Third-Party Beneficiary Status

These Clauses are intended to confer enforceable rights upon data subjects as third-party beneficiaries. A data subject may invoke and enforce the following Clauses directly against the Data Importer where the Data Exporter has ceased to exist, has become insolvent, or has failed to take appropriate action within **[30] calendar days** of a data subject complaint:

- The right to access their personal data (Section 7.2)
- The right to correct inaccurate personal data (Section 7.3)
- The right to information about recipients of their personal data (Section 7.4)
- Remedies for breach of these Clauses (Section 13)

### 7.2 Access Requests

Upon a data subject's access request, the Data Importer shall:

1. Verify the identity of the requesting data subject.
2. Provide a copy of the personal data being processed within **[21] calendar days** of a verified request, or notify the Data Exporter to respond where the Data Exporter holds primary accountability.
3. Document all access requests and responses in an access request log.

### 7.3 Correction Requests

Upon receiving a data subject's request to correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data:

1. The Data Importer shall correct, complete, or update the personal data within **[21] calendar days** of a verified request.
2. Where correction is not technically possible within the Data Importer's systems, the Data Importer shall immediately notify the Data Exporter to effect the correction at source.
3. The Data Importer shall notify any sub-processors of the correction where the personal data has been onward transferred.

### 7.4 Data Subject Complaints

Data subjects who believe their rights have been infringed may:

1. Submit a complaint to the Data Exporter at: **[Data Exporter's Data Subject Complaint Contact]**
2. Submit a complaint directly to the Personal Data Protection Commissioner of Malaysia at: **www.pdp.gov.my**
3. Invoke third-party beneficiary rights against the Data Importer via the dispute resolution mechanism in Section 14.

## 9. Suspension of Transfers on Law Enforcement Demand

*This section governs the Data Importer's obligations when it receives a legally binding demand from a public authority in the recipient country to disclose personal data. Transparency and proportionality are essential safeguards.*

### 9.1 Notification of Demands

Where the Data Importer receives a legally binding demand from a public authority in [Recipient Country] to disclose personal data transferred under these Clauses, the Data Importer shall:

1. **Assess legality:** Immediately assess whether the demand is legally binding and whether it is proportionate and necessary under the laws of [Recipient Country].
2. **Notify the Data Exporter** as soon as permissible under applicable law, providing details of the demand and the legal basis.
3. **Challenge the demand** where the Data Importer has grounds to do so and is not legally prohibited from mounting a challenge.
4. **Minimise disclosure:** Where disclosure is ultimately required, disclose only the minimum personal data necessary to satisfy the demand.

### 9.2 Suspension Trigger

Where the Data Importer determines or the Data Exporter reasonably concludes that:

- the Data Importer cannot notify the Data Exporter of a public authority demand due to a legal prohibition on notification; or
- the volume or nature of demands indicates that local laws do not ensure an adequate level of protection;

**the Data Exporter may immediately suspend the transfer** of personal data under these Clauses pending resolution or termination under Section 12.

### 9.3 Suspension Procedure

| Step | Action | Responsible Party | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Issue written notice of suspension | Data Exporter | Immediate |
| 2 | Acknowledge suspension and cease processing new transferred data | Data Importer | Within [24] hours |
| 3 | Assess whether the issue can be remediated | Both Parties | Within [30] days |
| 4 | Resume transfer (if resolved) or proceed to termination | Data Exporter | Following assessment |

## 12. Termination Rights and Data Return or Deletion

*Termination provisions must address both voluntary termination and termination triggered by compliance failures. Data return and deletion obligations are essential to ensure personal data does not remain with the Data Importer beyond the authorised period.*

### 12.1 Term

These Clauses take effect on **[Effective Date]** and remain in force until terminated in accordance with this Section or the expiration of the underlying commercial agreement, whichever is earlier.

### 12.2 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate these Clauses by providing **[90] days'** written notice to the other Party, subject to completion of data return or deletion obligations under Section 12.4.

### 12.3 Termination for Cause

The Data Exporter may terminate these Clauses with immediate effect upon written notice if:

1. The Data Importer commits a material breach of these Clauses that is not remedied within **[30] days** of written notice;
2. The Data Importer becomes insolvent, enters administration, or is subject to equivalent insolvency proceedings;
3. A competent authority determines that the Data Importer's processing violates applicable data protection law;
4. The Data Importer is unable to comply with these Clauses as a result of changes to local laws of [Recipient Country]; or
5. A transfer suspension under Section 9 has not been resolved within **[60] days**.

### 12.4 Data Return and Deletion

Upon termination or expiration of these Clauses, the Data Importer shall, at the Data Exporter's election:

| Option | Data Importer's Obligation | Timeframe | Verification |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Return** | Return all personal data in a structured, machine-readable format | Within [30] Business Days of termination | Written confirmation of complete return |
| **Deletion** | Securely delete or destroy all personal data and all existing copies, including from sub-processors | Within [30] Business Days of termination | Written certification of deletion including method used |

The Data Importer may retain personal data beyond this period solely where required by [Recipient Country]'s mandatory law, in which case the Data Importer shall notify the Data Exporter of the legal basis, scope, and duration of retention.

## 13. Liability and Indemnification

*This section establishes each Party's liability exposure for breach of the Clauses and the mechanism for compensation of data subjects.*

### 13.1 Data Exporter Liability

The Data Exporter shall be liable to data subjects for damages caused by the Data Exporter's breach of these Clauses or applicable Malaysian data protection law.

### 13.2 Data Importer Liability

The Data Importer shall be liable to data subjects and to the Data Exporter for damages caused by the Data Importer's breach of these Clauses.

### 13.3 Joint Liability

Where a data subject has suffered damage as a result of a breach by either Party and it is not possible to ascertain which Party caused the damage, both Parties shall be jointly and severally liable. A Party that has paid full compensation may seek contribution from the other Party.
