## 2. Purpose and Scope
The purpose of this Master Information Security Policy (ISP) is to establish the overarching framework for protecting [Organization Name]'s information assets from all threats, whether internal or external, deliberate or accidental. 
**Scope:** This policy applies to all employees, contractors, third-party vendors, and any entity accessing [Organization Name]'s information systems or data.

## 3. Core Security Principles
[Organization Name] commits to the following principles:
1.  **Confidentiality:** Information is accessible only to authorized individuals.
2.  **Integrity:** Accuracy and completeness of information and processing methods are safeguarded.
3.  **Availability:** Authorized users have access to information and associated assets when required.
4.  **Least Privilege:** Access rights are granted strictly on a "need-to-know" and "need-to-use" basis.
5.  **Security by Design:** Security is embedded into the lifecycle of all systems and projects.

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities (RACI)
*   **Board of Directors:** Accountable for overall cyber risk oversight.
*   **CISO:** Responsible for the implementation, monitoring, and enforcement of this ISP.
*   **System Owners:** Responsible for the classification and protection of data within their systems.
*   **All Employees:** Responsible for complying with this policy and reporting suspected incidents immediately.

## 5. Subordinate Policies
This ISP acts as the "Tier 1" apex document. It mandates compliance with the following "Tier 2" policies:
*   Identity & Access Management (IAM) Policy
*   Data Classification & Handling Standard
*   Cyber Incident Response Plan (CIRP)
*   Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM) Policy

## 6. Exception Management
Any deviation from this policy requires a formal **Risk Acceptance/Exception Request**.
1.  Requests must be submitted in writing with a valid business justification.
2.  Exceptions are time-bound (maximum 12 months) and must be approved by the CISO.
3.  Critical risks must be escalated to the Board Risk Committee.

## 7. Enforcement & Non-Compliance
Violations of this ISP or its subordinate policies will be treated as a severe disciplinary matter. Sanctions may range from formal warnings up to termination of employment or contract, and potential legal action under the **Computer Crimes Act 1997**.

## 8. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
Effectiveness of this policy is measured via:
*   **KPI 1:** Number of security incidents resulting in data loss (Target: 0).
*   **KPI 2:** Percentage of staff completing annual security awareness training (Target: >95%).
*   **KPI 3:** Percentage of approved policy exceptions reviewed before expiration (Target: 100%).
