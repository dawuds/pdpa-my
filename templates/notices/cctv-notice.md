## 1. Purpose and Scope

This notice is issued by [Organization Name] in compliance with the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA), Section 7** and applicable guidance from the Personal Data Protection Commissioner of Malaysia. It informs individuals present in CCTV-monitored areas of the nature, purpose, and legal basis of video surveillance, the identity of the data controller, data retention practices, and the rights available to data subjects.

This notice applies to all premises operated by [Organization Name] where closed-circuit television (CCTV) surveillance systems are in operation.

## 4. Purposes of CCTV Surveillance

*List all purposes for which CCTV footage is collected and processed. Purposes must be specific, explicit, and legitimate. Do not process footage for purposes incompatible with those stated here.*

CCTV footage is collected and processed for the following purposes:

- **Security:** Prevention, detection, and investigation of criminal activity, including theft, trespass, and vandalism.
- **Safety:** Ensuring the physical safety of employees, customers, contractors, and visitors on the premises.
- **Loss Prevention:** Protection of company assets and prevention of unauthorised access to restricted areas.
- **Incident Investigation:** Supporting internal investigations and providing evidentiary records where required.
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Meeting obligations under applicable Malaysian laws and regulations.

## 5. Retention Period

*State the standard retention period for CCTV footage. Footage must not be retained longer than necessary for the stated purposes. The Commissioner's guidance and industry practice for financial institutions typically prescribes 30 days unless specific circumstances require longer retention.*

| Category | Retention Period | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Standard footage | 30 days | Automatically overwritten upon expiry |
| Footage under active investigation | Until conclusion of investigation | Subject to legal hold procedures |
| Footage disclosed to law enforcement | As directed by the relevant authority | Copies retained per court or regulatory order |

Footage that is not subject to an active hold or investigation will be automatically deleted or overwritten after **[30] days** from the date of recording.

---

## 6. Disclosure of CCTV Footage

*Specify the categories of third parties to whom footage may be disclosed and the circumstances justifying such disclosure. Disclosures must be limited to what is necessary and proportionate.*

CCTV footage may be disclosed to the following parties where legally permitted or required:

- **Law Enforcement Agencies:** Royal Malaysia Police (PDRM), Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC), or other competent authorities pursuant to a lawful request, court order, or statutory obligation.
- **Regulatory Authorities:** Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM), Securities Commission Malaysia, or other relevant regulators where required for supervisory or investigative purposes.
- **Authorised Security Personnel:** Licensed security service providers engaged by [Organization Name] for premises protection, subject to confidentiality obligations.
- **Internal Personnel:** Authorised employees on a need-to-know basis (e.g., HR, Compliance, Legal) in connection with an active investigation or disciplinary proceeding.
- **Legal Advisers:** External counsel engaged by [Organization Name] in connection with legal proceedings or regulatory matters.

[Organization Name] does not sell, rent, or share CCTV footage for commercial purposes.

## 7. Data Subject Rights

*Explain the rights available to individuals whose images are captured on CCTV, and how those rights may be exercised, in accordance with PDPA Part III. Note that some rights may be subject to limitations where disclosure would prejudice law enforcement or the rights of other individuals.*

Under the PDPA 2010, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data captured by our CCTV systems:

| Right | Description | How to Exercise |
|---|---|---|
| **Right of Access** | Request access to CCTV footage in which you appear | Submit a written Data Access Request to the DPO |
| **Right of Correction** | Request correction of inaccurate personal data held about you | Submit a written Data Correction Request to the DPO |
| **Right to Withdraw Consent** | Where processing is consent-based, withdraw consent at any time | Submit a written notice to the DPO |
| **Right to Limit Processing** | Request that processing be ceased or limited in specified circumstances | Submit a written request to the DPO |

**To exercise your rights**, please contact the DPO at the details listed in Section 3. Requests will be acknowledged within **[21] calendar days** and responded to within **[30] calendar days** in accordance with PDPA requirements. [Organization Name] reserves the right to verify your identity before fulfilling any request.

> **Note:** Access to footage may be restricted where it would prejudice the prevention or detection of crime, the apprehension or prosecution of offenders, or the rights and freedoms of other data subjects.
