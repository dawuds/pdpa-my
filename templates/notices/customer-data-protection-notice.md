## Purpose and Scope

This Customer Data Protection Notice ("Notice") is issued by **[Organization Name]** ("we", "us", "our") in accordance with Section 7 of the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**, which requires data controllers to inform data subjects of the purposes for which their personal data is being processed at or before the time of collection.

This Notice applies to all personal data collected from customers and prospective customers of [Organization Name] through any channel, including but not limited to in-branch interactions, digital platforms, telephone, and third-party intermediaries. It describes how we collect, use, disclose, retain, and protect your personal data throughout the course of our relationship with you.

By engaging our services or providing your personal data to us, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and consented to the practices described in this Notice.

## 2. Primary Purposes of Processing

*Describe the core operational purposes for which personal data is processed. These are the purposes directly necessary to deliver the contracted service and should form the lawful basis for collection.*

Your personal data is processed for the following primary purposes:

- **Service Delivery** — To process applications, open accounts, onboard you as a customer, and deliver the financial products and services you have requested.
- **Account Management** — To manage and administer your account(s), including updating your records, handling queries, and processing instructions.
- **Billing and Payments** — To process payments, collections, fee deductions, and issue statements, invoices, and receipts.
- **Credit Assessment** — To evaluate creditworthiness, assess risk, and make lending or financing decisions.
- **Regulatory Compliance** — To fulfil obligations under applicable laws and regulations, including anti-money laundering (AML), know-your-customer (KYC), and tax reporting requirements.
- **Security and Fraud Prevention** — To authenticate your identity, detect suspicious activity, and prevent financial crime.
- **Customer Support** — To respond to enquiries, complaints, and service requests, and to maintain records of our interactions.

## 7. Rights of Data Subjects Under PDPA

*Enumerate the rights available to customers under the PDPA. Include reference to the specific PDPA sections for each right to support regulatory referencing.*

As a data subject under the PDPA, you have the following rights:

| Right | Description | Relevant PDPA Section |
|---|---|---|
| **Right of Access** | Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you | s30 |
| **Right of Correction** | Request correction of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated personal data | s34 |
| **Right to Withdraw Consent** | Withdraw consent to processing, subject to legal and contractual limitations | s38 |
| **Right to Prevent Processing for Direct Marketing** | Opt out of marketing communications at any time | s43 |
| **Right to Prevent Processing Likely to Cause Damage or Distress** | Object to processing that is causing or likely to cause unwarranted harm | s42 |

To exercise any of the above rights, please refer to Section 8 of this Notice for contact details. We will respond to valid requests within **21 days** of receipt (or such period as may be prescribed by the PDPA Commissioner).

We reserve the right to refuse requests that are manifestly unfounded, excessive, or where exemptions under the PDPA apply (e.g. for the purposes of preventing or detecting crime, or where disclosure is required by law).
