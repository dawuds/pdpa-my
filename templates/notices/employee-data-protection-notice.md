## 1. Introduction

[Organization Name] (hereinafter referred to as "the Company," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the personal data of its employees and job applicants in accordance with the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**, specifically the obligations set out under **Section 7** of the PDPA, which requires data controllers to inform data subjects of the purpose for which their personal data is being processed.

This Notice is issued to all current employees, former employees, and job applicants (collectively referred to as "you") to explain how the Company collects, uses, discloses, retains, and protects your personal data throughout the employment lifecycle.

By submitting your personal data to the Company — whether during the recruitment process, upon commencement of employment, or during the course of your employment — you acknowledge receipt of this Notice.

---

## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

*Describe the primary purpose of this notice: to fulfil the mandatory notification obligation under PDPA s7 and to ensure employees understand how their personal data is handled.*

This Notice establishes the framework by which [Organization Name] processes personal data pertaining to employees and applicants. It is designed to:

- Fulfil the mandatory notification obligations under **PDPA 2010, Section 7**;
- Promote transparency and trust between the Company and its workforce;
- Define the categories, purposes, lawful bases, and retention periods applicable to employee personal data; and
- Inform employees of their rights and the mechanisms available to exercise those rights.

### 2.2 Scope

*Specify who and what this notice applies to. Include all stages of the employment relationship.*

This Notice applies to:

- **Individuals:** All job applicants, current employees (full-time, part-time, contract, and temporary), secondees, and former employees of [Organization Name].
- **Data:** All personal data collected, processed, stored, or disclosed by the Company in connection with the employment relationship.
- **Systems:** All HR information systems, payroll platforms, performance management tools, and any other systems used to process employee personal data.

This Notice does **not** apply to personal data processed in connection with the Company's external customer relationships, which are governed by separate privacy notices.

## 4. Purposes of Processing

*For each processing purpose, clearly articulate why the data is needed. Align purposes with the lawful bases described in Section 5. This section forms the core of the s7 disclosure.*

The Company processes your personal data for the following purposes:

### 4.1 Recruitment and Selection

- Evaluating your suitability for a position;
- Conducting background checks and verifying qualifications;
- Communicating with you regarding your application;
- Maintaining a talent pipeline for future opportunities (where consent has been obtained).

### 4.2 Employment Administration and Contract Management

- Onboarding and maintaining your employment records;
- Administering your compensation, benefits, and payroll;
- Managing leave, attendance, and scheduling;
- Administering staff loans, advances, and expense reimbursements;
- Processing statutory deductions and contributions (EPF/KWSP, SOCSO/PERKESO, EIS, PCB/income tax).

### 4.3 Performance Management

- Conducting and recording performance appraisals;
- Managing promotions, transfers, and career development;
- Identifying training needs and recording development activities.

### 4.4 Health, Safety, and Welfare

- Administering occupational health and safety programmes;
- Processing group insurance enrolment and claims;
- Managing workplace injury or illness reporting.

### 4.5 Legal and Regulatory Compliance

- Fulfilling obligations under the **Employment Act 1955**, **Industrial Relations Act 1967**, **Occupational Safety and Health Act 1994**, **Income Tax Act 1967**, **EPF Act 1991**, **SOCSO Act 1969**, and other applicable Malaysian laws;
- Responding to lawful requests from regulatory authorities, law enforcement, and courts;
- Defending legal claims or proceedings involving the Company.

### 4.6 Internal Security and Audit

- Monitoring IT systems access and usage in accordance with the Company's IT Acceptable Use Policy;
- Conducting internal audits and investigations;
- Maintaining physical security of Company premises.

### 4.7 Post-Employment

- Providing employment references (where authorised);
- Processing final salary, benefits, and statutory entitlements upon termination;
- Retaining records as required by law.

## 9. Employee Rights

*This section is critical for PDPA compliance. Employees must be clearly informed of their rights and the practical mechanism to exercise them. PDPA grants rights of access and correction; describe these along with any applicable limitations.*

As a data subject under the PDPA 2010, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data held by the Company:

### 9.1 Right of Access

You have the right to request access to the personal data the Company holds about you. Upon receipt of a valid written request, the Company will respond within **[21 / 30] calendar days**.

**To exercise this right:** Submit a written request to the Data Protection Officer using the contact details in Section 10, specifying the categories of data you wish to access.

### 9.2 Right of Correction

You have the right to request correction of any personal data that is inaccurate, incomplete, or out of date. The Company will acknowledge your request within **[14] calendar days** and action corrections within **[21] calendar days**.

**To exercise this right:** Submit a written request to Human Resources or the DPO, specifying the data to be corrected and providing supporting evidence where applicable.

### 9.3 Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal. Note that withdrawal of consent may affect the Company's ability to provide certain employment-related services or benefits.

### 9.4 Right to Limit Processing

You may request that the Company limit the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances, for example where you contest the accuracy of data or where processing is unlawful.

### 9.5 Complaints

If you believe that the Company has not handled your personal data in accordance with the PDPA, you may raise a complaint with:

- **Internal:** The Data Protection Officer (see Section 10); or
- **External:** The **Personal Data Protection Department (JPDP)**, Ministry of Digital, Malaysia, at [www.pdp.gov.my](https://www.pdp.gov.my).

> **Note:** Certain rights under the PDPA may be subject to limitations. For example, requests that would prejudice an ongoing investigation, legal proceedings, or statutory obligations may be declined or deferred. The Company will communicate reasons for any such limitation in writing.
