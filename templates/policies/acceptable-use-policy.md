## 1. Purpose

*State the purpose of this policy and the outcomes it is designed to achieve. Reference the specific regulatory obligation that mandates or supports this policy.*

This Acceptable Use Policy ("Policy") establishes the standards and requirements governing the permitted and prohibited use of [Organization Name]'s information technology (IT) systems, networks, data assets, and personal data by all individuals who access these resources. This Policy is designed to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of organizational information systems and to ensure that the processing of personal data is conducted in a lawful, fair, and responsible manner in accordance with the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA).

This Policy supports [Organization Name]'s obligations under:

- **PDPA Section 8 (Security Principle):** To protect personal data held by [Organization Name] from any loss, misuse, modification, unauthorized or accidental access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction through practical steps including internal governance policies and access controls.
- **PDPA Section 9 (Retention Principle):** To ensure that personal data is not kept longer than is necessary for the fulfilment of the purpose for which it was collected, and that users of IT systems comply with approved retention schedules.

This Policy aims to:

- Define the boundaries of acceptable and unacceptable use of [Organization Name]'s IT resources and personal data;
- Protect data subjects whose personal data is processed by [Organization Name];
- Establish a culture of responsible information handling among all personnel; and
- Provide a clear framework for accountability and disciplinary action in the event of misuse.

## 5. Permitted Use of IT Systems and Personal Data

*Describe the categories of use that are explicitly sanctioned by the organization. Be clear about the boundaries of legitimate access and processing so that Users understand what is and is not authorized.*

### 5.1 General Permitted Use

[Organization Name]'s IT systems and personal data assets are provided to Users for the sole purpose of conducting legitimate business activities on behalf of [Organization Name]. Permitted use is limited to activities that are:

- **Authorized:** Within the scope of the User's defined role, responsibilities, and assigned access rights;
- **Necessary:** Required for the fulfilment of a specific business function or task;
- **Proportionate:** Limited to the minimum access to personal data required to complete the task (data minimization); and
- **Compliant:** Consistent with this Policy, applicable law, and [Organization Name]'s information security and data protection policies.

### 5.2 Permitted Uses of IT Systems

The following uses of [Organization Name]'s IT systems are permitted:

- Access to applications, databases, and systems as granted by the IT department in accordance with the User's role and approved access rights;
- Processing, storing, and transmitting business information using approved organizational platforms and tools;
- Communication with customers, partners, and colleagues using [Organization Name]'s authorized email and collaboration platforms;
- Accessing the internet for legitimate business research and operational purposes;
- Remote access to organizational systems via approved VPN or secure remote desktop solutions;
- Limited, incidental personal use of email and internet where it does not interfere with business operations, does not involve the processing of organizational personal data, and complies with Section 8 of this Policy.

### 5.3 Permitted Processing of Personal Data

Users who are authorized to access personal data in the course of their duties may:

- Access, retrieve, and process personal data strictly within the limits of their authorized role and for the specific purpose for which access was granted;
- Share personal data with internal colleagues who have a legitimate business need and appropriate access rights;
- Transfer personal data to authorized third-party data processors under a valid Data Processing Agreement (DPA) approved by the Legal or Compliance function;
- Process sensitive personal data only where explicitly required by their job function and with any additional controls specified by the Data Protection Officer (DPO).

### 5.4 Access Rights and the Principle of Least Privilege

*Document the organization's access provisioning model here, referencing any access management policy or procedure.*

| **Access Level** | **Description** | **Authorization Required** |
|---|---|---|
| Standard User | General system access for day-to-day functions | Line Manager + IT Approval |
| Elevated User | Access to sensitive or restricted data sets | Line Manager + Department Head + IT |
| Privileged Access | Administrative or system-level access | CISO Authorization + IT |
| Third-Party Access | External access for contracted purposes | Contract Owner + Legal + IT |

- Access rights shall be reviewed at minimum [quarterly / annually or as specified in the Access Management Policy].
- Access rights shall be revoked immediately upon termination or role change.
- Users shall not share credentials or access tokens with any other individual under any circumstances.

## 6. Prohibited Activities

*Enumerate specific behaviors that are forbidden. This section should be comprehensive and unambiguous to support disciplinary action if required. Prohibited activities should be linked to the relevant PDPA obligations where applicable.*

### 6.1 Unauthorized Access

The following activities relating to unauthorized access are strictly prohibited:

- Accessing, attempting to access, or assisting others to access IT systems, applications, or personal data beyond the scope of assigned access rights;
- Using another User's credentials, access tokens, session cookies, or any other authentication means to gain access to systems;
- Bypassing, disabling, or circumventing access controls, authentication mechanisms, security software, or audit logging systems;
- Exploiting vulnerabilities in organizational or third-party systems for any purpose not sanctioned under an authorized penetration testing engagement;
- Accessing personal data without a documented and legitimate business purpose.

### 6.2 Data Exfiltration and Unauthorized Disclosure

The following activities relating to data handling are strictly prohibited:

- Copying, transferring, or extracting organizational data or personal data to unauthorized external storage devices (USB drives, external hard drives, personal cloud storage);
- Transmitting personal data to personal email accounts or non-organizational systems;
- Sharing personal data with any third party without a valid legal basis, data processing agreement, or prior written approval from the DPO;
- Disclosing organizational confidential information or personal data on social media, public forums, or to members of the public;
- Printing or creating physical copies of personal data files beyond operational necessity without applying appropriate physical security controls;
- Transferring personal data outside Malaysia to jurisdictions without adequate data protection standards without prior approval from the DPO and in compliance with PDPA cross-border transfer requirements.

### 6.3 Malicious and Harmful Activities

- Installing unauthorized software, applications, or scripts on organizational devices;
- Introducing malware, ransomware, spyware, or any other malicious code into [Organization Name]'s systems;
- Conducting or facilitating cyber attacks, phishing, social engineering, or any other form of attack against internal or external systems;
- Using organizational IT systems to conduct unauthorized cryptocurrency mining or any activity for personal financial gain;
- Using organizational systems to access, store, or distribute unlawful material, including material that is defamatory, obscene, or in breach of intellectual property rights.

### 6.4 Misuse of Personal Data

The following activities relating to personal data misuse are prohibited and may constitute a breach of the PDPA:

- Processing personal data for purposes incompatible with the stated purpose of collection (purpose limitation);
- Retaining personal data beyond the approved retention period specified in [Organization Name]'s Data Retention Schedule;
- Altering, falsifying, or corrupting personal data records;
- Using customer or employee personal data for personal benefit, third-party commercial interests, or any purpose unrelated to [Organization Name]'s legitimate business activities;
- Failing to apply prescribed security controls when handling sensitive personal data.

### 6.5 Other Prohibited Activities

- Using organizational IT resources for personal business activities or for operating a secondary employment without prior written approval;
- Engaging in any activity that violates applicable Malaysian law or regulation;
- Threatening, harassing, or intimidating colleagues, customers, or third parties using organizational communication systems;
- Misrepresenting one's identity or organizational affiliation in electronic communications.

## 7. Personal Device and BYOD Requirements

*Define the conditions under which personal devices may be used to access organizational systems or data, and the security controls that apply. If the organization does not permit BYOD, state this explicitly.*

### 7.1 BYOD Policy Status

*Select and complete the applicable option.*

**Option A — BYOD Permitted:**
[Organization Name] permits the use of personally owned devices to access organizational systems and data under strictly controlled conditions as set out in this section. Access via personal devices is a privilege, not a right, and may be revoked at any time.

**Option B — BYOD Not Permitted:**
[Organization Name] does not permit the use of personally owned devices to access organizational systems, networks, or personal data. All work-related activities must be conducted exclusively on [Organization Name]-issued devices.

### 7.2 BYOD Eligibility and Enrollment

*Where BYOD is permitted, document enrollment and eligibility requirements.*

- Users wishing to access organizational systems on personal devices must submit a written BYOD enrollment request to [IT Department / IT Helpdesk].
- Approval is subject to the User's role, the nature of the data to be accessed, and satisfactory completion of the device security assessment.
- Enrollment requires acceptance of the BYOD terms of use and installation of [Organization Name]'s approved Mobile Device Management (MDM) solution.
- The following device types are eligible for BYOD enrollment: [Smartphones / Tablets / Laptops — specify as appropriate].

### 7.3 Security Requirements for BYOD Devices

All personal devices enrolled under the BYOD program must comply with the following minimum security standards:

| **Control** | **Requirement** |
|---|---|
| Operating System | Current, supported OS version with automatic security updates enabled |
| Screen Lock | PIN, password, biometric authentication; automatic lock after [5] minutes of inactivity |
| Encryption | Full-device encryption enabled |
| MDM Enrollment | [Organization Name]'s approved MDM solution installed and active |
| Antivirus / Endpoint Protection | Approved endpoint security software installed |
| Remote Wipe | Remote wipe capability enabled via MDM |

- Upon termination of employment or BYOD authorization, [Organization Name] reserves the right to remotely wipe the organizational data container from the device.

### 7.6 User Responsibilities for Personal Devices

- Users are responsible for ensuring their personal devices meet the requirements of this Policy at all times;
- Users must report any loss, theft, or compromise of a BYOD-enrolled device to [IT Security / Helpdesk] immediately;
- [Organization Name] is not responsible for any loss of personal data on a User's personal device resulting from enforcement of MDM policies, including remote wipe.

## 8. Email and Internet Usage Guidelines

*Set out the specific rules governing the use of organizational email and internet access. These guidelines directly support the PDPA Security Principle by preventing inadvertent disclosure of personal data.*

### 8.1 Email Usage

#### 8.1.1 General Principles

- Organizational email accounts are provided for business purposes and must be used in a professional manner consistent with [Organization Name]'s standards;
- Users must not use personal email accounts (e.g., Gmail, Yahoo, Hotmail) for any business communication involving organizational data or personal data;
- Users must not set up automatic forwarding of organizational email to personal email accounts.

#### 8.1.2 Handling Personal Data via Email

- Personal data transmitted via email must be encrypted or password-protected where the email is directed to an external recipient or contains sensitive personal data;
- Users must verify recipient email addresses before sending personal data to prevent misdirected disclosures;
- Bulk transmission of personal data (e.g., customer lists, employee data) must use approved secure file transfer mechanisms rather than email attachments;
- The use of BCC for mass communications involving personal data must comply with the approved bulk communication procedure to prevent unauthorized disclosure of recipients' email addresses.

#### 8.1.3 Email Security

- Users must exercise caution with unsolicited emails, unexpected attachments, and links from unknown senders;
- Suspected phishing emails must be reported immediately to [IT Security Team / Email: security@[organization].com];
- Users must not open attachments or click links in suspicious emails and must follow the [Phishing Response Procedure — reference document];
- Mass marketing emails must not be sent from individual user accounts; approved email marketing platforms must be used.

#### 8.1.4 Retention and Archiving

- Organizational email is subject to the data retention schedules defined in [Data Retention Policy — reference document];
- Users must not delete emails that may be required for regulatory reporting, litigation hold, or audit purposes.

### 8.2 Internet Usage

#### 8.2.1 Permitted Internet Use

- Accessing websites and online resources required for legitimate business functions;
- Downloading files from trusted sources required for business operations, subject to antivirus scanning;
- Accessing approved cloud-based business applications via web browser.

#### 8.2.2 Prohibited Internet Activities

The following internet activities are prohibited on [Organization Name]'s network or via organizational devices:

- Accessing websites containing illegal, obscene, discriminatory, or otherwise unlawful content;
- Accessing personal social media, streaming, or gaming platforms during working hours, except during approved break periods and where it does not involve organizational data;
- Downloading or streaming large files (e.g., video content, software) unrelated to business purposes that may degrade network performance;
- Bypassing [Organization Name]'s web filtering controls using proxy services, VPNs not provided by the organization, or any other circumvention tool;
- Conducting personal commercial transactions or online shopping using organizational devices during business hours;
- Submitting, uploading, or sharing organizational data or personal data to any unauthorized cloud storage, AI, or third-party online service.

#### 8.2.3 Use of AI Tools and Third-Party Online Services

*This section is critical for PDPA compliance given the proliferation of cloud-based AI tools.*

- Users must not input organizational personal data, customer information, confidential business data, or any PDPA-protected information into publicly available AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot unless approved) or any third-party online service not approved by [Organization Name];
- A list of approved AI and third-party online tools is maintained by [IT / DPO] and published at [internal intranet link];
- Any request to use a new AI or online service that will involve organizational or personal data must be submitted to [IT / DPO] for review and approval before use.

### 8.3 Social Media Guidelines

- Users must not share organizational confidential information, customer personal data, or internal matters on personal social media accounts;
- Users must clearly distinguish personal views from official organizational positions in any public forum;
- Users representing [Organization Name] on official social media channels must comply with the [Social Media Policy — reference document].

## 9. Consequences of Policy Violations

*Define the disciplinary framework that applies to breaches of this Policy. Reference relevant HR and legal frameworks. Ensuring consequences are proportionate and clearly communicated supports deterrence and fairness.*

### 9.1 General Principles

Violations of this Policy are treated seriously by [Organization Name] and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or engagement, and referral to law enforcement authorities where criminal conduct is involved.

Disciplinary outcomes will be proportionate to the nature and severity of the violation, taking into account:

- Whether the violation was intentional, negligent, or accidental;
- The sensitivity and volume of personal data affected;
- The potential or actual harm caused to data subjects or [Organization Name];
- Whether the violation was a first or repeated occurrence; and
- Whether the User cooperated with the investigation.

### 9.2 Disciplinary Framework

| **Severity** | **Examples** | **Potential Disciplinary Outcome** |
|---|---|---|
| **Minor** | Incidental personal use exceeding permitted limits; failure to lock screen when leaving workstation | Verbal warning; mandatory retraining |
| **Moderate** | Unauthorized access to data beyond role scope; sharing login credentials; forwarding business email to personal accounts | Written warning; suspension of access privileges; formal HR process |
| **Serious** | Deliberate unauthorized disclosure of personal data; bypassing security controls; introducing malware | Termination of employment / engagement; recovery of losses; civil action |
| **Critical** | Data exfiltration for commercial gain; sharing personal data with competitors; facilitating external attacks | Termination; referral to Royal Malaysia Police; notification to PDPA Commissioner |

### 9.3 PDPA Offences and Regulatory Consequences

*Reference the specific PDPA penalty provisions applicable to individual and organizational violations.*

Violations that constitute offences under the PDPA may expose [Organization Name] and individual Users to regulatory action by the Personal Data Protection Commissioner, including:

- Fines of up to **RM 500,000** and/or imprisonment for officers of [Organization Name] under relevant PDPA provisions;
- Mandatory notification to the Personal Data Protection Commissioner and affected data subjects in the event of a data breach;
- Regulatory audits, enforcement notices, or prohibition orders.

### 9.4 No Reduction of Legal Liability

Disciplinary action taken by [Organization Name] does not remove or reduce a User's personal legal liability under the PDPA, the Computer Crimes Act 1997, or any other applicable Malaysian legislation.

## 10. Employee Monitoring and Privacy Notice

*This section fulfills [Organization Name]'s transparency obligation under the PDPA Notice and Choice Principle (Section 6) by informing Users of monitoring activities. Monitoring must be proportionate, documented, and lawful.*

### 10.1 Purpose of Monitoring

[Organization Name] monitors the use of its IT systems and resources to:

- Protect organizational data and personal data from unauthorized access and disclosure;
- Ensure compliance with this Policy, applicable law, and regulatory requirements;
- Detect, investigate, and respond to security incidents and potential PDPA breaches;
- Maintain network performance and operational integrity; and
- Satisfy audit, regulatory, and legal obligations.

**By using [Organization Name]'s IT systems, Users acknowledge and consent to monitoring activities as described in this section.**

### 10.2 Scope of Monitoring

[Organization Name] may monitor, record, and audit the following activities on its systems and network:

| **Monitoring Activity** | **Scope** | **Retention Period** |
|---|---|---|
| Email content and metadata | All organizational email accounts | [12 months] |
| Internet browsing activity | All network traffic via organizational infrastructure | [90 days] |
| System access and authentication logs | All systems and applications | [12 months] |
| File access and data transfer logs | Sensitive systems and DLP alerts | [24 months] |
| Endpoint activity logs | Organization-managed devices | [90 days] |
| CCTV in office premises | Physical access areas | [30 days] |
| Badge access and physical access logs | All controlled access points | [12 months] |

### 10.3 Basis for Monitoring

Monitoring is conducted on the basis of [Organization Name]'s **legitimate interests** in protecting its assets and data subjects' personal data, and in compliance with [Organization Name]'s regulatory obligations. Monitoring activities are designed to be:

- **Targeted:** Focused on systems and activities presenting material risk;
- **Proportionate:** Limited to what is necessary for the stated purposes; and
- **Transparent:** Communicated to Users through this Policy and any applicable employment contract provisions.

### 10.4 Employee Privacy Rights

Users retain privacy rights as employees and individuals, and [Organization Name] will:

- Limit access to monitoring data to authorized personnel in IT Security, HR, Legal, and Compliance with a legitimate business need;
- Protect monitoring data from unauthorized access and use;
- Not use monitoring data for purposes incompatible with those stated in this Policy;
- Dispose of monitoring data in accordance with the approved retention schedule; and
- Comply with applicable employment law and data protection obligations when conducting investigations based on monitoring data.

### 10.5 Covert Monitoring

In exceptional circumstances, where there is reasonable suspicion of serious misconduct or criminal activity, [Organization Name] may conduct targeted covert monitoring. Any covert monitoring will require:

- Prior written approval from [CISO and Legal Counsel / relevant authority];
- Documented justification and proportionality assessment;
- Limitation to the specific User(s) and period under investigation.

## 11. Reporting Obligations for Suspected Breaches

*Define the mandatory reporting process for Users who discover or suspect a data breach, security incident, or policy violation. Swift reporting is critical for PDPA compliance and limiting harm to data subjects.*

### 11.1 Obligation to Report

**All Users have an unconditional obligation to report any suspected, actual, or near-miss data breach or security incident immediately upon discovery.** This obligation applies regardless of whether the User believes they caused or contributed to the incident.

Failure to report a known or suspected breach is itself a serious policy violation and may constitute an independent disciplinary or regulatory matter.

### 11.2 What to Report

Users must report any of the following:

- Unauthorized access to or disclosure of personal data (whether by themselves, another User, or an external party);
- Loss or theft of a device (organizational or BYOD-enrolled) containing personal data;
- Accidental transmission of personal data to an incorrect recipient;
- Discovery of malware, ransomware, or suspicious activity on organizational systems;
- Suspected phishing attacks or social engineering attempts, whether successful or not;
- Observation of another User engaging in prohibited activities under this Policy;
- Any other event that may represent a risk to the security or integrity of organizational data or personal data.

### 11.3 How to Report

| **Reporting Channel** | **Contact** | **Availability** |
|---|---|---|
| IT Security Helpdesk (Primary) | [security@organization.com / internal ext. XXXX] | Business hours |
| 24/7 Security Incident Hotline | [+60X-XXXX-XXXX] | 24/7 |
| Data Protection Officer | [dpo@organization.com / internal ext. XXXX] | Business hours |
| Line Manager | Direct reporting line | Business hours |
| Anonymous Reporting (Whistleblowing) | [Link to Whistleblowing Portal / Hotline] | 24/7 |

### 11.4 Information to Include in a Report

When reporting a suspected incident, Users should provide (where known):

- Date and time the incident was discovered or occurred;
- Description of what happened;
- Systems, applications, or data involved;
- Categories and estimated volume of personal data affected;
- Any actions already taken by the User;
- Contact details of the reporting User (unless reporting anonymously).

### 11.5 Incident Response and PDPA Notification Timeline

Upon receipt of an incident report, [Organization Name]'s [IT Security / Incident Response Team] will follow the [Incident Response Policy — reference document]. Key timelines:

| **Milestone** | **Target Timeline** |
|---|---|
| Initial assessment and triage | Within [4] hours of report |
| Containment actions initiated | Within [24] hours of report |
| Regulatory notification assessment completed | Within [48] hours of report |
| Notification to Personal Data Protection Commissioner (if required) | Within [72 hours] or as required by applicable regulation |
| Notification to affected data subjects (if required) | As directed by Commissioner or [Organization Name]'s DPO |

### 11.6 Non-Retaliation

[Organization Name] is committed to protecting individuals who report suspected breaches in good faith. Users who report incidents in good faith shall not be subjected to retaliation, adverse employment consequences, or any other form of negative treatment as a result of making a report. Malicious or knowingly false reports are not protected.

### 13.2 Key Responsibilities

**Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)**
- Holds overall ownership and accountability for this Policy;
- Ensures adequate monitoring, enforcement, and review of the Policy;
- Chairs the security incident response for breaches involving personal data.

**Data Protection Officer (DPO)**
- Advises on PDPA compliance requirements in the Policy;
- Oversees data breach notification obligations;
- Reviews and approves data processing agreements with third parties;
- Acts as the primary point of contact with the Personal Data Protection Commissioner.

**Human Resources (HR)**
- Administers the annual acknowledgement process;
- Manages disciplinary proceedings for Policy violations;
- Ensures Policy requirements are incorporated into employment contracts and onboarding.

**IT Department**
- Implements technical controls to enforce Policy requirements;
- Manages access provisioning, MDM, and endpoint security;
- Operates monitoring systems and responds to security alerts;
- Maintains logs and audit trails.

**Line Managers**
- Ensure direct reports are aware of and comply with this Policy;
- Escalate suspected violations and incidents promptly;
- Oversee acknowledgement completion within their teams.

**All Users**
- Read, understand, and comply with this Policy;
- Process personal data only within the limits of their authorized role;
- Report suspected breaches immediately;
- Complete annual acknowledgement and training requirements.
