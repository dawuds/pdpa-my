## 1. Purpose

*This section defines the objectives of the policy and the regulatory imperatives driving its existence. Clearly state the "why" before the "how."*

This Data Retention and Disposal Policy ("Policy") establishes the binding framework by which [Organization Name] ("the Organization") manages the lifecycle of personal data from the point of collection through to secure and verified disposal. The Policy ensures that the Organization fulfills its obligations under the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (Act 709)** ("PDPA"), specifically the Retention Principle stipulated under **Section 10**, which mandates that personal data shall not be retained longer than is necessary for the fulfillment of the purpose for which it was collected or subsequently processed.

The specific objectives of this Policy are to:

- Establish clear and consistent retention periods for all categories of personal data processed by the Organization, aligned with applicable legal, regulatory, and operational requirements.
- Define and standardize secure disposal procedures for personal data — both physical and electronic — when the data is no longer required.
- Protect data subjects from risks arising from unnecessary retention of their personal data, including unauthorized access, data breaches, and misuse.
- Ensure the Organization can demonstrate compliance with the PDPA and related subsidiary legislation, Bank Negara Malaysia ("BNM") guidelines, and sector-specific regulatory obligations where applicable.
- Provide clear guidance to all employees, contractors, and third parties who handle personal data on their obligations under this Policy.
- Reduce organizational risk exposure associated with the indefinite or excessive retention of personal data.

### 3.1 The PDPA Retention Principle (Section 10)

Section 10 of the PDPA provides that:

> *"A data controller shall not retain personal data in a form that permits the identification of the data subject for any longer than is necessary for the fulfillment of the purpose, including any directly related purpose, for which the personal data was to be used."*

The Organization interprets this obligation as requiring it to:

1. Define a specific, documented retention period for each category of personal data and the purpose for which it is held.
2. Actively delete or anonymize personal data upon the expiry of its retention period, unless a legal hold or other lawful basis for continued retention applies.
3. Ensure that retention periods are reviewed regularly and updated to reflect changes in law, regulation, or business purpose.

## 5. Retention Principles Aligned with PDPA Section 10

*This section operationalizes the PDPA Retention Principle into actionable organizational policy. Each principle should be actionable and auditable. Use this section to translate the legal obligation into practical organizational obligations.*

### 5.1 Core Retention Principles

The Organization adheres to the following principles when determining how long personal data is retained:

#### 5.1.1 Necessity Principle

Personal data shall be retained **only for as long as it is necessary** to fulfill the specific, documented purpose for which it was collected or subsequently processed. Once the purpose is fulfilled and no other lawful basis for continued retention exists, the data must be disposed of securely without delay.

#### 5.1.2 Purposeful Retention Principle

Retention must be tied to a **documented and legitimate purpose**. Retention of personal data "in case it may be useful" or for purposes not declared to the data subject at the point of collection is not permitted. Any new purpose for which historical data is to be retained must be assessed for PDPA compliance before retention continues.

#### 5.1.3 Minimum Retention Principle

Where a choice exists between a longer and a shorter retention period — and both satisfy legal and operational requirements — the Organization shall adopt the **shorter period**. The Organization does not retain personal data beyond the minimum period required by applicable law or business necessity.

#### 5.1.4 Legal and Regulatory Compliance Principle

Where a statutory or regulatory instrument mandates a **minimum retention period**, the Organization shall comply with that minimum period as a floor. However, this does not authorize retention beyond that period unless a separate lawful basis is established. Known mandatory minimum periods for this Organization's sector are documented in the Retention Schedule (Section 6).

#### 5.1.5 Legal Hold Supremacy Principle

Where personal data is subject to a **Legal Hold** (as described in Section 7), the normal retention period is suspended. Personal data under a Legal Hold shall not be disposed of until the hold is formally lifted by the DPO or Legal Counsel, regardless of whether the standard retention period has expired.

#### 5.1.6 Active Management Principle

Retention is not passive. The Organization shall **actively identify, review, and dispose of** personal data in accordance with the Retention Schedule. System-level controls, scheduled reviews, and documented disposal records are required to demonstrate active compliance.

#### 5.1.7 Format-Agnostic Principle

These retention principles apply to personal data in **all formats and media**, including electronic records, physical documents, audio and video recordings, emails, instant messages, backup tapes, and portable storage devices.

#### 5.1.8 Cross-Border Consistency Principle

Where personal data is transferred to or processed in a jurisdiction outside Malaysia, the higher standard — whether under the PDPA or the law of the recipient jurisdiction — shall apply to the retention and disposal of that data.

### 5.2 Determining the Appropriate Retention Period

*Guide document authors in selecting the correct retention period for each data category. This section should explain the decision logic that underpins the Retention Schedule.*

The Organization applies the following hierarchy when determining the appropriate retention period for a category of personal data:

1. **Statutory minimum period** — the minimum period mandated by law (e.g., 6 years under AMLA, 7 years under LHDN requirements).
2. **Regulatory guidance** — periods recommended or required by BNM or other sector regulators.
3. **Contractual obligation** — periods required by agreements with counterparties or customers.
4. **Business necessity** — the period genuinely necessary for operational, audit, or dispute resolution purposes, documented and justified by the relevant department head.
5. **Data subject consent** — where consent is the lawful basis and no other basis applies, the period for which consent was obtained.

Where multiple periods apply, the **longest applicable period** shall govern, subject to Section 5.1.5 (Legal Hold Supremacy).

### 5.3 Prohibited Retention Practices

The following practices are explicitly prohibited under this Policy:

- Retaining personal data indefinitely without a defined retention period.
- Retaining personal data past its retention period because disposal is inconvenient or costly.
- Archiving personal data to a system or location that circumvents the retention schedule.
- Creating derivative datasets that effectively perpetuate the use of personal data whose retention period has expired.
- Retaining personal data in backup systems beyond the maximum retention period without a legal hold justification.
- Transferring personal data to a third party to circumvent disposal obligations.

## 7. Legal Hold Procedures

*A Legal Hold (also called a Litigation Hold or Preservation Hold) suspends normal retention and disposal activities. This section defines when holds are triggered, how they are managed, and how they are released. Poor legal hold management is a significant litigation and regulatory risk.*

### 7.1 Trigger Events for Legal Hold

A Legal Hold shall be declared by the DPO in consultation with Legal Counsel upon the occurrence — or **reasonable anticipation** — of any of the following events:

- Commencement of civil, criminal, or regulatory proceedings to which the Organization is a party.
- Receipt of a formal regulatory investigation notice, inquiry, or subpoena from BNM, the Personal Data Protection Commissioner, MACC, PDRM, or any other competent authority.
- Receipt of a statutory demand or pre-litigation correspondence that indicates imminent legal proceedings.
- Internal investigation that may give rise to external proceedings.
- Regulatory examination that requests preservation of specific data categories.
- Any other circumstance in which Legal Counsel reasonably anticipates that personal data may be required as evidence.
- [Any Organization-specific trigger events]

### 7.2 Legal Hold Declaration Process

Upon identification of a Legal Hold trigger event, the following steps shall be taken:

| Step | Action | Responsible Party | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Legal Counsel or Compliance identifies potential trigger event | Legal Counsel / Compliance | Immediately upon identification |
| 2 | Legal Counsel issues formal Legal Hold Notice to DPO | Legal Counsel | Within 24 hours of trigger identification |
| 3 | DPO logs the Legal Hold in the Legal Hold Register (Appendix D) | DPO | Within 24 hours of notification |
| 4 | DPO notifies all relevant department heads and IT of the scope of the hold | DPO | Within 48 hours of trigger identification |
| 5 | IT suspends automated deletion/disposal for affected data | IT / System Administrator | Within 48 hours of DPO notification |
| 6 | Department custodians identify and quarantine affected physical records | Department Heads | Within 5 business days |
| 7 | DPO confirms Legal Hold is in effect in writing to all custodians | DPO | Within 5 business days |
| 8 | Legal Hold status is reviewed and updated quarterly | DPO / Legal Counsel | Quarterly |

### 7.3 Scope of Legal Hold

The Legal Hold Notice shall specify:

- The matter name and reference number.
- The categories of personal data subject to the hold.
- The date range of data to be preserved.
- The systems, storage locations, and custodians covered.
- The format in which data should be preserved.
- The date the hold takes effect.
- Contact information for the supervising Legal Counsel.

### 7.4 Legal Hold Compliance Obligations

During a Legal Hold:

- **No personal data within the scope of the hold may be disposed of**, regardless of whether its standard retention period has expired.
- Employees notified of the hold must immediately preserve all relevant data within their control and must not alter, delete, or conceal any data within the scope of the hold.
- System administrators must disable automated deletion routines for affected data sets.
- New data created in connection with the matter during the hold period is automatically subject to the same hold.
- Any accidental deletion or disposal within the scope of a hold must be reported to Legal Counsel and the DPO immediately.

### 7.5 Legal Hold Release Process

A Legal Hold shall be formally released only upon written instruction from Legal Counsel confirming that:

- The relevant proceedings or investigation have concluded.
- All appeals and further proceedings are exhausted or barred.
- There is no further need to preserve the data for the matter in question.

Upon release, the DPO shall:

1. Issue a formal Legal Hold Release Notice to all custodians.
2. Update the Legal Hold Register.
3. Reinstate normal retention and disposal procedures for the affected data.
4. Initiate disposal of any data whose retention period expired during the hold period, unless a separate lawful basis for continued retention exists.

## 8. Secure Disposal Methods

*This section operationalizes the Security Principle (PDPA s9) in the context of disposal. Different types of media require different disposal methods. Insufficient disposal methods (e.g., simply deleting a file) are a significant compliance risk.*

### 8.1 Guiding Principles for Disposal

All disposal of personal data must be:

- **Irreversible** — the data cannot be reconstructed or recovered after disposal.
- **Documented** — a Record of Disposal must be created for each disposal event.
- **Authorized** — disposal must be authorized by the relevant Department Head or DPO prior to execution.
- **Timely** — disposal must occur within **[X] days** of the expiry of the applicable retention period, unless a Legal Hold applies.
- **Proportionate** — the method used must be proportionate to the sensitivity of the data being disposed of.

### 8.2 Disposal Methods for Electronic Records

#### 8.2.1 Deletion and Overwriting

*Standard deletion (moving to a recycle bin) is NOT an approved disposal method for personal data as it does not render the data irrecoverable.*

| Method | Description | Suitable For | Standards Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Secure Overwriting (Data Wiping)** | Overwriting all data blocks on the storage medium with random data patterns, typically using multiple passes. | Hard disk drives (HDDs), USB drives, NAS storage | NIST SP 800-88 Rev. 1; DoD 5220.22-M |
| **Cryptographic Erasure** | Destroying the encryption key that protects encrypted data, rendering the encrypted data unreadable. Suitable where the medium cannot be physically destroyed. | Encrypted storage, cloud-hosted data, self-encrypting drives (SEDs) | NIST SP 800-88 Rev. 1 |
| **Degaussing** | Applying a powerful magnetic field to a magnetic storage medium to destroy the stored data. | Magnetic tape, HDDs (note: not suitable for SSDs or flash storage) | NIST SP 800-88 Rev. 1 |
| **Secure File Deletion Software** | Use of certified software (e.g., Blancco, Eraser) that overwrites data at the file level. | Specific files or folders on active systems | Vendor certification required |

#### 8.2.2 Physical Destruction

| Method | Description | Suitable For | Standards Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Disintegration/Shredding (Media)** | Physical destruction of storage media using industrial shredders that reduce media to particles not exceeding [X] mm. | HDDs, SSDs, USB drives, optical media, smart cards | NIST SP 800-88; [Applicable standard] |
| **Incineration** | Burning of storage media or paper in a certified incineration facility. | Highly sensitive physical records; damaged media | [Applicable standard] |
| **Pulverizing/Crushing** | Mechanical destruction of storage media. | HDDs, SSDs, optical media | NIST SP 800-88 |

#### 8.2.3 Cloud and SaaS-Hosted Data

| Scenario | Approved Disposal Method |
|---|---|
| Cloud storage (IaaS) | Cryptographic erasure of encryption keys; confirm with cloud provider that data is removed from all storage tiers including backups |
| SaaS applications | Formal deletion request to SaaS provider; obtain written confirmation of deletion from all environments including DR and backups |
| Multi-tenant environments | Cryptographic erasure; verify data isolation with provider |
| [Other scenario] | [Approved method] |

#### 8.3.1 Paper Records

| Data Sensitivity | Approval Required | Additional Requirements |
|---|---|---|

### 8.5 Prohibited Disposal Methods

The following disposal methods are explicitly **prohibited**:

- Placing documents containing personal data in general waste or recycling bins.
- Overwriting files using standard OS delete functions (e.g., Shift+Delete or emptying the Recycle Bin) without verified secure overwrite.
- Donating, re-selling, or returning storage media containing personal data without prior certified sanitization.
- Disposing of IT equipment to e-waste facilities without first obtaining a certificate confirming secure data sanitization.
- Reformatting a drive as the sole disposal method (does not meet the irreversibility requirement).
- Leaving personal data in a cloud environment after subscription cancellation without confirming deletion.

## 9. Third-Party Disposal Certification Requirements

*Third-party disposal introduces a significant compliance risk. The Organization remains responsible for the data even after it leaves its premises. This section defines the minimum standards for engaging third-party disposal providers.*

### 9.1 Selection of Approved Disposal Vendors

The Organization shall maintain an **Approved Disposal Vendor Register** (Appendix E). All disposal of personal data involving third parties must be conducted only through vendors on the Approved Register.

Before a vendor may be added to the Approved Register, the following due diligence must be completed and documented:

| Due Diligence Requirement | Description | Assessment Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Business registration verification | Vendor is a validly registered entity in Malaysia | Prior to engagement; annually |
| Capability assessment | Vendor possesses appropriate equipment, certifications, and trained staff for the disposal methods required | Prior to engagement; annually |
| Security assessment | Vendor's transport and handling procedures, access controls, and chain-of-custody processes are adequate | Prior to engagement; annually |
| Insurance verification | Vendor carries adequate public liability and professional indemnity insurance | Prior to engagement; annually |
| Regulatory compliance | Vendor holds all required permits and licenses (e.g., DOE approval for incineration) | Prior to engagement; annually |
| Data processing agreement | A written agreement is in place under PDPA s4(2) | Prior to any disposal event |
| References and track record | Vendor can provide verifiable references from comparable clients | Prior to engagement |

### 9.2 Contractual Requirements for Third-Party Disposal

Any contract or data processing agreement with a third-party disposal vendor must include:

- A clear description of the scope of data and media to be disposed of.
- The specific disposal method(s) to be employed.
- A requirement that the vendor provide a **Certificate of Destruction** for each disposal event within **[X] business days** of completion.
- A prohibition on sub-contracting disposal to unauthorized parties without the Organization's prior written consent.
- A requirement that the vendor maintain chain-of-custody records from collection to final destruction.
- A right of audit allowing the Organization to inspect the vendor's facilities and records.
- Data breach notification obligations.
- Liability provisions proportionate to the sensitivity of the data disposed of.
- Compliance with applicable Malaysian law, including PDPA.

### 9.3 Certificate of Destruction Requirements

A valid Certificate of Destruction issued by a third-party disposal vendor must include, at minimum:

- The name and registration number of the disposal vendor.
- The date(s) on which disposal was carried out.
- A description of the materials disposed of (type, quantity, and where possible, identifiers such as asset tags or serial numbers).
- The disposal method used and, where applicable, the standard or specification applied.
- The name and signature of the authorizing representative of the disposal vendor.
- A declaration that the materials described have been irreversibly destroyed.
- The Certificate reference number for cross-referencing in the Organization's Record of Disposal.

### 9.4 Chain-of-Custody Requirements

For all third-party disposals, the Organization shall maintain a documented chain-of-custody record covering:

1. Internal authorization and release of media/records for disposal.
2. Transfer to the third-party vendor, including a signed collection manifest.
3. Receipt acknowledgment by the vendor.
4. Confirmation of destruction, including Certificate of Destruction reference number.

Chain-of-custody records shall be retained for **[X] years** as part of the Record of Disposal.

## 10. Records of Disposal Documentation

*The ability to demonstrate that personal data was disposed of in accordance with this Policy is critical for PDPA compliance. This section defines what records must be kept, for how long, and by whom.*

### 10.1 Requirement to Maintain Disposal Records

The Organization shall maintain a **Disposal Log** (Appendix F) documenting every instance of personal data disposal. The Disposal Log is a compliance record and shall be maintained by the DPO or a delegate.

*Note: The retention of Disposal Records is distinct from the retention of the personal data itself. Disposal Records do not contain personal data and may be retained for longer periods for audit and regulatory purposes.*

### 10.2 Minimum Content of a Disposal Record

Each entry in the Disposal Log must capture, at minimum, the following information:

| Field | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Disposal Reference Number** | Unique identifier for the disposal event | DIS-2025-001 |
| **Date of Disposal** | The date on which disposal was completed | [DD/MM/YYYY] |
| **Data Category** | The category/categories of personal data disposed of (not the data itself) | Customer identification data; Transaction records |
| **Volume / Quantity** | The volume of data or number of records disposed of | 1,500 customer files; 2 HDDs |
| **Retention Period Expired** | Confirmation that the retention period had expired before disposal | Yes — retention period expired [DD/MM/YYYY] |
| **Disposal Method** | The method used for disposal | Secure overwrite (NIST SP 800-88); Cross-cut shredding |
| **Disposal Location** | Where the disposal was carried out | [Location] / [Vendor name] |
| **Authorized By** | Name and role of the officer who authorized disposal | [Name], Head of [Department] |
| **Executed By** | Name and role of the officer who carried out the disposal | [Name], IT Security Officer |
| **Certificate of Destruction Reference** | Reference number of the Certificate of Destruction (if applicable) | COD-[Vendor]-2025-001 |
| **Legal Hold Check** | Confirmation that no Legal Hold applied to the data | Confirmed — no Legal Hold in effect |
| **Remarks** | Any additional relevant information | [Notes] |

### 10.3 Automated Disposal Records

Where personal data is disposed of through automated system processes (e.g., automated deletion upon expiry of retention period in a records management system), the system shall generate and retain an automated disposal log containing, at minimum:

- Timestamp of deletion.
- Data category or record type deleted.
- System identifier and version.
- Volume or record count.
- The retention rule applied.

Automated disposal logs shall be reviewed by the DPO on a **[quarterly / monthly]** basis to confirm that automated disposal is functioning correctly and that no exceptions have arisen.

### 10.4 Retention of Disposal Records

Disposal Records, including the Disposal Log and Certificates of Destruction, shall be retained for a minimum of **[7] years** from the date of disposal. This retention period for Disposal Records is set independently of the retention periods applicable to the personal data itself and is intended to enable the Organization to demonstrate compliance during regulatory inspections and audits.

Disposal Records shall be stored securely and be accessible to the DPO and authorized Compliance personnel upon request.

## 11. Review Triggers for Retention Schedule Updates

*The Retention Schedule is a living document that must be updated when the legal, regulatory, or business landscape changes. This section defines the events that must trigger an off-cycle review in addition to the annual review.*

### 11.1 Scheduled Annual Review

The Retention Schedule shall be formally reviewed at least **once per calendar year**. The annual review shall be conducted by the DPO with input from:

- Legal Counsel (regulatory and legislative changes).
- Head of Compliance (regulatory examination findings).
- Heads of all relevant departments (operational changes).
- IT / Data Architecture (system and storage changes).
- Risk Management (emerging risks).

The findings and outcomes of the annual review shall be documented and submitted to [Approval Authority] for endorsement. Version history shall be updated accordingly.

### 11.2 Off-Cycle Review Triggers

An off-cycle review of the Retention Schedule shall be initiated within **[30] days** of any of the following events:

| Trigger Event | Description | Action Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Legislative Amendment** | Amendment to the PDPA, AMLA, FSA, Employment Act, Income Tax Act, or any other statute affecting data retention obligations | Review and update all affected entries in the Retention Schedule |
| **New BNM Policy Document or Circular** | Issuance of a new BNM policy document, circular, or guideline affecting data retention | Review and update affected entries; assess operational impact |
| **New Business Activity or Product Launch** | Introduction of a new product, service, or business activity involving new categories of personal data | Add new entries to the Retention Schedule for the new data categories |
| **System Change** | Migration to a new core system, CRM, HRMS, or data platform that alters data storage architecture | Verify that retention and disposal controls are configured correctly in the new system |
| **Merger, Acquisition, or Restructuring** | Corporate restructuring that results in new data flows, new data categories, or new regulatory obligations | Comprehensive review of the Retention Schedule with Legal |
| **Regulatory Examination Finding** | A finding by a regulator (BNM, PDPC, etc.) relating to data retention practices | Immediate remediation of the specific finding and review of related entries |
| **Data Breach** | A personal data breach that reveals gaps in retention or disposal practices | Targeted review of the data category and disposal method involved |
| **Material Change in Processing Purpose** | A change in the purpose for which a significant category of personal data is processed | Review and update the affected entries to ensure continued PDPA compliance |
| **Request from Data Subject** | A pattern of erasure requests or complaints indicating that retention periods may be excessive | Review and potentially reduce the relevant retention periods |
| **Vendor Change** | Change of a third-party disposal vendor or data processor | Update Approved Disposal Vendor Register; verify continued compliance |
| **[Other trigger]** | [Description] | [Action] |

### 11.3 Emergency Review

In the event of a critical regulatory development (e.g., emergency legislation, urgent BNM directive, data breach with significant regulatory impact), the DPO may convene an emergency review of the Retention Schedule with less than 30 days' notice. Emergency reviews and their outcomes shall be documented and tabled at the next available [Governance Committee] meeting.

### 12.2 Specific Role Descriptions

#### 12.2.1 Data Protection Officer (DPO)

The DPO has overall responsibility for the implementation, maintenance, and oversight of this Policy. The DPO's specific responsibilities include:

- Owning and maintaining this Policy and the Retention Schedule.
- Providing guidance to departments on the interpretation of retention periods and disposal requirements.
- Managing the Legal Hold process in consultation with Legal Counsel.
- Maintaining the Disposal Log and reviewing Certificates of Destruction.
- Reporting on compliance with this Policy to [Senior Management Committee / Board].
- Initiating annual and off-cycle reviews of the Retention Schedule.
- Engaging with the Personal Data Protection Commissioner and other regulators on matters relating to data retention and disposal.

#### 12.2.2 Legal Counsel

Legal Counsel's responsibilities include:

- Advising on statutory and regulatory retention requirements.
- Issuing Legal Hold Notices and managing the Legal Hold process in coordination with the DPO.
- Advising on the legality of specific retention periods and disposal methods.
- Reviewing contracts with third-party disposal vendors.

#### 12.2.3 Heads of Department / Data Custodians

Department Heads are responsible for:

- Implementing this Policy within their departments.
- Ensuring that personal data within their area of responsibility is identified, classified, and managed in accordance with the Retention Schedule.
- Authorizing disposal of personal data within their departments.
- Reporting to the DPO any instances of non-compliance with this Policy discovered within their departments.
- Ensuring that all staff within their departments complete required training on this Policy.

#### 12.2.4 IT / Data Management

IT's responsibilities include:

- Implementing technical controls to enforce retention periods in organizational systems.
- Executing secure disposal of electronic records in accordance with this Policy.
- Configuring and maintaining automated disposal processes.
- Maintaining and providing automated disposal logs to the DPO.
- Implementing Legal Hold suspensions at the system level when notified by the DPO.
- Overseeing the sanitization of IT assets prior to disposal or reuse.

#### 12.2.5 All Employees

All employees who handle personal data are responsible for:

- Complying with this Policy and any department-specific implementing procedures.
- Not retaining personal data beyond the applicable retention period.
- Disposing of personal data only through approved methods.
- Immediately reporting any suspected disposal failure or breach to their Department Head and the DPO.
- Completing training on this Policy as required.

## 13. Compliance and Enforcement

*This section makes clear that this Policy is binding and that non-compliance carries consequences. This is important for both deterrence and demonstrating to regulators that the Organization takes compliance seriously.*

### 13.1 Compliance Monitoring

The DPO shall monitor compliance with this Policy through:

- **Quarterly review** of the Disposal Log to verify that disposal is occurring in accordance with the Retention Schedule.
- **Annual audit** of a sample of disposal records, Certificates of Destruction, and Legal Hold Register entries.
- **System-level reporting** from IT on automated disposal processes, including exception reports where disposal did not occur as scheduled.
- **Department compliance assessments** conducted by the DPO or an appointed third party on a [annual/biannual] basis.
- **Incident reporting** — any reported failure of disposal controls shall be investigated and findings reported to [Senior Management Committee].

### 13.2 Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with this Policy may constitute:

- A breach of the Personal Data Protection Act 2010, which may result in regulatory enforcement action by the Personal Data Protection Commissioner, including fines and directions.
- A breach of other applicable Malaysian legislation.
- A breach of the Organization's internal Code of Conduct or employment terms, which may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.
- Personal liability for employees who willfully or negligently cause personal data to be retained beyond its retention period or disposed of insecurely.

### 13.3 Reporting Non-Compliance

Any employee who discovers or suspects non-compliance with this Policy must report it to their Department Head and the DPO without delay. Reports may also be made through the Organization's [Whistleblowing Channel / Compliance Hotline]. Employees who report in good faith will be protected from retaliation in accordance with the Organization's [Whistleblower Policy].

### 13.4 Policy Exceptions

Any exception to this Policy must be:

- Approved in writing by the DPO.
- Documented with a clear justification and risk assessment.
- Time-limited with a defined review date.
- Recorded in the Policy Exception Register maintained by Compliance.
