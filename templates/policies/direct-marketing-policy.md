## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Direct Marketing Policy ("Policy") establishes the requirements, obligations, and controls governing the use of personal data by [Organization Name] ("the Organization") for direct marketing purposes. The Policy ensures that all direct marketing activities are conducted in full compliance with the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA), in particular Sections 7, 40, 41, 42, and 43, and any subsidiary legislation, guidelines, or codes of practice issued by the Personal Data Protection Commissioner ("Commissioner").

The Policy is designed to:

- Protect the rights of data subjects with respect to the use of their personal data for marketing communications;
- Establish clear obligations on the Organization and its personnel when conducting or commissioning direct marketing activities;
- Define consistent standards for obtaining, recording, and honouring consent and opt-out preferences;
- Ensure the Organization maintains audit-ready evidence of compliance with applicable data protection legislation.

### 1.2 Scope

This Policy applies to:

- **All personnel** of [Organization Name], including employees, contractors, agents, and third-party vendors acting on behalf of the Organization, who are involved in the planning, execution, or oversight of direct marketing activities.
- **All personal data** held or processed by the Organization that is used, or proposed to be used, for direct marketing purposes, regardless of the channel or medium through which marketing communications are delivered.
- **All direct marketing channels**, including but not limited to: email, short message service (SMS), multimedia messaging service (MMS), telephone/telemarketing, postal mail, in-app push notifications, social media, and any other electronic or physical communication channel used to send marketing materials.
- **All products and services** marketed by [Organization Name] or on its behalf by authorized third parties.

This Policy does **not** apply to:

- Communications that are purely operational or transactional in nature (e.g., account statements, product alerts, security notifications), provided such communications contain no promotional content;
- Internal communications between employees of the Organization.

### 1.3 Geographic Application

This Policy applies to all direct marketing activities directed at individuals located in Malaysia or where the personal data of Malaysian residents is processed by the Organization, consistent with the territorial scope of the PDPA 2010.

## 4. Direct Marketing Consent Requirements

### 4.1 General Consent Principle

*Describe the Organization's baseline standard for obtaining consent before using personal data for direct marketing. Articulate the distinction between opt-in and opt-out models, and specify which applies by default for the Organization.*

In accordance with PDPA 2010 s40 and the Notice and Choice Principle (s7), the Organization shall not use personal data for direct marketing without first ensuring that a valid and documented basis for doing so exists. The Organization adopts a **consent-first** approach to direct marketing: all direct marketing communications shall be based on an explicit, affirmative opt-in consent obtained from the data subject, unless a specific lawful basis under the PDPA permits otherwise.

### 4.2 Requirements for Valid Consent

Consent obtained for direct marketing purposes shall meet all of the following criteria:

- **Freely given:** Consent must not be made a condition of entering into a contract, receiving a service, or accessing any benefit, unless the direct marketing is a core element of the service requested.
- **Specific:** Consent must clearly specify the purposes for which personal data will be used (i.e., direct marketing), the types of products or services to be promoted, and the channels through which communications will be delivered.
- **Informed:** The data subject must be provided with sufficient information, including the Organization's identity, the types of data to be used, and the means by which they may withdraw consent.
- **Unambiguous:** Consent must be obtained through a clear, affirmative act (e.g., ticking an unchecked checkbox, signing a consent form). Pre-ticked boxes, silence, or inactivity do not constitute valid consent.
- **Distinguishable:** Where consent is sought alongside other terms and conditions, the consent for direct marketing must be presented separately and distinctly.

### 4.4 Consent for New Purposes or Channels

Where the Organization wishes to use personal data for a direct marketing purpose or channel not covered by an existing consent, or where there has been a material change in the scope of marketing activities, fresh consent must be obtained from the data subject prior to commencing such use.

### 4.5 Withdrawal of Consent

Withdrawal of consent for direct marketing must be as easy to execute as providing consent. The Organization shall ensure that every direct marketing communication includes a clear and functional mechanism for the data subject to withdraw consent (opt out). Withdrawal of consent shall not affect the lawfulness of any processing conducted prior to withdrawal.

## 5. Opt-Out Rights and Obligations

### 5.1 Right to Opt Out

*Describe the data subject's right to opt out under s43 PDPA 2010, and articulate the Organization's obligations upon receiving an opt-out request.*

In accordance with PDPA 2010 s43, every data subject has an absolute right to require the Organization to cease using their personal data for direct marketing purposes at any time and without providing a reason. This right applies regardless of whether consent was originally given, and regardless of any contractual relationship that may exist between the data subject and the Organization.

### 5.2 Opt-Out Request Channels

The Organization shall provide and maintain at least the following opt-out request channels:

| Channel | Mechanism | Availability |
|---|---|---|
| Email communication | Unsubscribe link in every marketing email | Every marketing email |
| SMS / MMS | Reply "STOP" to marketing SMS | Every marketing SMS |
| Telephone / IVR | Verbal opt-out during call; IVR option | All telemarketing calls |
| Website | Privacy preference centre / unsubscribe page | [URL / Portal Name] |
| Branch / in-person | Written or verbal request to any branch officer | All branches |
| Written request | Letter or email to DPO | [DPO email / postal address] |
| Customer service hotline | [Hotline number] | [Operating hours] |

### 5.3 Processing of Opt-Out Requests

Upon receipt of a valid opt-out request, the Organization shall:

1. Acknowledge receipt of the opt-out request within **[X] business days** (recommended: 3 business days).
2. Cease all direct marketing communications to the data subject within **[X] business days** of acknowledgment (recommended: 14 calendar days, or as soon as practicable).
3. Update the data subject's marketing preference record in all relevant systems and databases.
4. Add the data subject's contact details to the Organization's Suppression List (see Section 7).
5. Confirm to the data subject that their opt-out has been actioned, specifying the effective date.

### 5.4 Prohibition Following Opt-Out

Once a data subject has exercised the opt-out right:

- Their personal data **must not** be used for any direct marketing purpose, regardless of channel.
- Their contact details **must not** be shared with third parties for the purpose of direct marketing on behalf of the Organization.
- The opt-out preference **must be preserved** even if the data subject's contact details are subsequently updated, or if the data subject enters into a new product or service relationship with the Organization, unless the data subject provides a fresh, affirmative opt-in consent.

### 5.5 No Cost to Data Subject

The exercise of the opt-out right shall be free of charge to the data subject. The Organization shall not impose any fee, penalty, or disadvantage on a data subject for exercising this right.

## 7. Suppression List Management

### 7.1 Purpose and Scope of the Suppression List

*Describe the Organization's Suppression List, its purpose, scope, and the systems in which it is maintained. Authors should specify the actual database, CRM, or marketing platform used to maintain suppression records.*

The Organization shall maintain a centralized Suppression List ("the List") comprising the contact details and preference records of all data subjects who have:

- Exercised their right to opt out of direct marketing (all channels or specific channels);
- Been identified as deceased;
- Been identified as having a marker indicating they should not be contacted for marketing purposes (e.g., hardship markers, complaint markers, vulnerable person flags);
- Been removed from marketing contact under any court order, regulator direction, or applicable legal obligation.

### 7.2 Suppression List Standards

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| **Centralization** | A single authoritative Suppression List must be maintained, covering all marketing channels. Departmental or campaign-specific sub-lists are permitted but must be synchronized with the central List at least [daily / weekly]. |

| **Update Frequency** | Opt-out requests must be reflected in the Suppression List within **[X] business days** of receipt (recommended: 1 business day). |
| **Pre-Campaign Screening** | All marketing contact lists must be cross-referenced against the current Suppression List immediately before each campaign dispatch. |

The Organization acknowledges the existence of national and sector-specific Do Not Call (DNC) mechanisms designed to protect individuals from unsolicited telemarketing communications. ### 8.2 DNC Screening Obligations

## 9. Third-Party List Acquisition

### 9.1 Policy on Third-Party Lists

*Describe the restrictions and due diligence requirements for acquiring and using contact lists sourced from third parties for direct marketing purposes. Authors should specify the approval process and the contractual safeguards required.*

The acquisition of personal data from third parties (including data brokers, affiliated entities, business partners, or other organizations) for use in direct marketing campaigns presents heightened risks of PDPA non-compliance. The Organization shall apply strict controls to any proposed acquisition and use of third-party marketing lists.

### 9.2 Restrictions on Third-Party List Acquisition

The Organization shall **not** acquire or use third-party lists for direct marketing purposes unless all of the following conditions are satisfied:

- [ ] **Prior approval:** Acquisition of any third-party list must be approved in advance by the [DPO / Compliance team / relevant governance committee].
- [ ] **Lawful basis verification:** The Organization must obtain written confirmation from the third-party supplier that the personal data on the list was collected lawfully, that the data subjects were notified of the possibility of third-party use for direct marketing, and that valid consent or another lawful basis exists for such use.
- [ ] **Data Processing Agreement (DPA):** A Data Processing Agreement, compliant with PDPA 2010 requirements, must be in place before any third-party personal data is received or used.
- [ ] **Source documentation:** The Organization must retain documentation evidencing the source of each third-party list, the consent or lawful basis represented by the supplier, and the date of acquisition.
- [ ] **PDPA compliance assessment:** A PDPA compliance assessment must be completed by the DPO before the list is used in any campaign.
- [ ] **Suppression and DNC screening:** All third-party lists must be screened against the Organization's Suppression List and the DNC Registry before use, in the same manner as internally generated lists.

### 9.4 Prohibited Sources

The Organization shall not acquire personal data for direct marketing purposes from the following sources:

- Lists compiled from publicly available directories where data subjects have not indicated consent to third-party marketing use;
- Lists obtained through data scraping, harvesting, or other automated collection from websites or social media platforms without explicit authorization;
- Lists sold or supplied by entities unable to demonstrate a lawful basis for the original collection of the data;
- Any source that appears to offer data at a price or scale inconsistent with legitimate data collection practices.

## 11. Children and Vulnerable Persons

### 11.1 General Prohibition

*Describe the Organization's policy on marketing to children and vulnerable persons. Authors should align this section with the Organization's broader customer suitability and responsible marketing frameworks, and with any sector-specific requirements from BNM or relevant regulators.*

The Organization recognizes that children and vulnerable persons require heightened protection in the context of direct marketing, given that they may not be able to give fully informed consent or may be susceptible to undue influence. The Organization shall apply enhanced safeguards when marketing to, or collecting personal data from, these groups.

### 11.2 Definition of Children

For the purposes of this Policy, a "child" means any individual under the age of **18 years** (or such other age as may be specified by applicable law or regulatory guidance from time to time).

### 11.3 Marketing to Children

- The Organization shall **not** target direct marketing communications at individuals who are known or reasonably believed to be under the age of 18, unless the product or service is specifically designed and regulated for use by minors and parental or guardian consent has been obtained.
- The Organization shall not collect personal data from children for direct marketing purposes without the verifiable consent of the child's parent or legal guardian.
- Where age verification mechanisms are not available or reliable, the Organization shall apply conservative defaults and shall not target marketing at consumers in age brackets where significant proportions may be minors.

### 11.4 Definition and Identification of Vulnerable Persons

A "vulnerable person" for the purposes of this Policy includes, but is not limited to:

- Individuals with diminished mental capacity;
- Individuals experiencing financial hardship or distress markers (as identified through the Organization's internal systems);
- Elderly individuals who may be susceptible to financial exploitation;
- Individuals who have raised complaints regarding high-pressure marketing or expressed distress in interactions with the Organization.

### 11.5 Safeguards for Vulnerable Persons

| Safeguard | Standard |
|---|---|
| **Flagging** | The Organization's CRM or customer data system must support the flagging of accounts as belonging to vulnerable persons. |
| **Marketing exclusion** | Data subjects flagged as vulnerable must be automatically excluded from all direct marketing campaigns unless the campaign is specifically designed to support vulnerable customers (e.g., hardship assistance communications). |
| **Agent training** | All customer-facing and telemarketing staff must be trained to identify indicators of vulnerability and to take appropriate action, including ceasing the marketing call and escalating to a specialist team. |
| **Script review** | Marketing scripts and materials must be reviewed by the DPO and Compliance team to ensure they do not contain content likely to exploit the circumstances of vulnerable persons. |
| **Complaint monitoring** | The Organization shall monitor complaints and opt-out patterns for indicators that vulnerable persons have been inappropriately targeted or subjected to undue marketing pressure. |

## 13. Policy Breach and Escalation

### 13.1 Definition of Policy Breach

A breach of this Policy ("Marketing Compliance Breach") includes, but is not limited to:

- Sending marketing communications to a data subject who has opted out, without a fresh and documented consent;
- Failing to screen a contact list against the Suppression List or DNC Registry before a campaign;
- Using personal data for direct marketing without a valid consent or lawful basis;
- Acquiring or using a third-party marketing list without completing the required due diligence;
- Sending marketing communications to a known or identified child without parental consent;
- Failing to process an opt-out request within the required timeframe;
- Failure to maintain required consent or campaign records.

### 13.2 Breach Reporting and Escalation

| Severity | Definition | Escalation Path | Response Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Breach affecting more than [1,000] data subjects, or involving data of a sensitive nature, or likely to cause significant harm. | DPO → CEO → Board / Risk Committee → PDPC (if required) | Notify DPO within **[2 hours]**; assess regulatory notification within **[24 hours]** |
| **High** | Breach affecting [100–1,000] data subjects, or a systematic failure of controls (e.g., suppression screening not applied to a campaign). | DPO → Compliance Committee | Notify DPO within **[24 hours]** |
| **Medium** | Isolated breach affecting fewer than [100] data subjects, with no indication of systemic failure. | DPO → Head of Marketing | Notify DPO within **[3 business days]** |
| **Low** | Minor procedural deviation with no data subject impact, promptly self-identified and corrected. | Marketing Operations → DPO (monthly reporting) | Document and report in next monthly DPO report |

### 13.3 Regulatory Notification

The Organization shall comply with any obligation to notify the Personal Data Protection Commissioner of data protection breaches arising from direct marketing non-compliance, in accordance with applicable PDPA provisions and any notification guidelines issued by the Commissioner.

### 13.4 Disciplinary Action

Personnel found to have deliberately or negligently breached this Policy may be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the Organization's Human Resources policies, up to and including termination of employment. Matters involving criminal conduct shall be referred to the appropriate law enforcement authorities.
