## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*Describe why this policy exists, the regulatory drivers, and what it is designed to achieve for the organization.*

This policy establishes the framework and requirements governing the selection, engagement, ongoing oversight, and offboarding of third parties and data processors who process personal data on behalf of [Organization Name] (hereinafter referred to as "the Organization"). It ensures that the Organization meets its obligations as a data user under the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**, particularly with respect to:

- The instruction and control of data processors in accordance with **PDPA Section 9**;
- The lawful transfer of personal data outside Malaysia by or through processors under **PDPA Section 34**;
- The accountability obligations and enforcement provisions applicable under **PDPA Section 129A** as amended.

The policy ensures that personal data entrusted to the Organization by data subjects is afforded appropriate protection throughout the lifecycle of any third-party or processor relationship, from initial vendor assessment through to contract termination and secure data disposal.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define precisely who and what this policy applies to — include entity types, geographies, data types, and any explicit exclusions.*

This policy applies to:

**In Scope:**

- All business units, departments, subsidiaries, and branches of [Organization Name] that engage or intend to engage third parties who process personal data on the Organization's behalf;
- All third parties, vendors, contractors, service providers, consultants, and outsourced partners acting as **data processors** as defined under the PDPA;
- All categories of personal data processed by such third parties, including but not limited to customer data, employee data, financial data, health-related data, and any sensitive personal data as defined under the PDPA;
- Processing activities carried out within Malaysia and any cross-border processing arrangements;
- Cloud service providers, IT managed services, payment processors, marketing analytics providers, HR outsourcing providers, and any other entity that accesses, stores, transmits, or otherwise processes personal data on behalf of the Organization.

**Out of Scope:**

- Third parties engaged solely in their capacity as independent data users (i.e., parties processing data for their own purposes under their own authority);
- Publicly available data sources where no personal data is disclosed by the Organization;
- [Any other exclusions specific to Organization Name].

## 2. Definitions

*Provide clear, legally grounded definitions for all key terms used in this policy. Align definitions with PDPA 2010 terminology where applicable.*

For the purposes of this policy, the following definitions shall apply:

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Data User** | [Organization Name], in its capacity as a person who either alone or jointly or in common with other persons processes any personal data or has control over or authorises the processing of any personal data, as defined under PDPA Section 4. |
| **Data Processor** | Any person, other than an employee of the data user, who processes personal data solely on behalf of the data user, and does not process personal data for any of the data processor's own purposes, as defined under PDPA Section 4. |
| **Third Party** | Any external party engaged by the Organization, including vendors, suppliers, contractors, agents, and subcontractors, who may have access to or process personal data held by the Organization. |
| **Personal Data** | Any information in respect of commercial transactions that is being processed wholly or partly by means of equipment operating automatically in response to instructions given for that purpose, processed otherwise than by such means that forms part of a relevant filing system, or is recorded with the intention that it should be so processed, as defined under PDPA Section 4. |
| **Sensitive Personal Data** | Personal data consisting of information as to the physical or mental health or condition of a data subject, his political opinions, his religious beliefs or other beliefs of a similar nature, the commission or alleged commission by him of any offence, or any other personal data as the Minister may determine by order published in the Gazette, as per PDPA Section 4. |
| **Processing** | Collecting, recording, holding, storing, adapting, altering, retrieving, using, disclosing, transferring, transmitting, publishing, correcting, erasing, or destroying personal data. |
| **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** | A legally binding contract between the Organization (as data user) and a data processor setting out the terms, conditions, and obligations governing the processing of personal data on the Organization's behalf. |
| **Subprocessor** | A third party engaged by a data processor to carry out specific processing activities on behalf of the data user. |
| **Cross-Border Transfer** | Any transfer of personal data to a place outside Malaysia. |
| **Due Diligence** | The pre-engagement assessment process conducted by the Organization to evaluate the suitability, capability, and compliance posture of a prospective data processor before entering into a processing relationship. |
| **Risk Tier** | A classification assigned to a third party or data processor based on the nature, volume, and sensitivity of personal data they process and the potential impact of a data breach or non-compliance event. |
| **Offboarding** | The formal process of terminating a data processor relationship, including the secure return or deletion of personal data, revocation of access, and contract closeout. |
| **JPDP** | Jabatan Perlindungan Data Peribadi — the Department of Personal Data Protection of Malaysia, the regulatory authority administering the PDPA. |
| **DPO** | Data Protection Officer of [Organization Name]. |

## 3. Policy Statement

*State the Organization's overarching commitment and position regarding third-party and data processor management.*

[Organization Name] is committed to ensuring that personal data in its custody is protected at all times, including when processed by third parties and data processors acting on its behalf. The Organization recognises that, as the data user, it retains full legal accountability for the lawful and secure processing of personal data, regardless of whether such processing is carried out by internal staff or external processors.

Accordingly, [Organization Name] shall:

- **Conduct structured due diligence** on all prospective data processors before engagement, proportionate to the risk classification of the processing activity;
- **Execute Data Processing Agreements** with all data processors as a mandatory contractual prerequisite to commencement of any personal data processing;
- **Maintain ongoing oversight** of data processors through periodic audits, assessments, and performance reviews throughout the duration of the processor relationship;
- **Restrict and monitor cross-border data transfers** carried out by or through processors to ensure compliance with PDPA Section 34 and applicable whitelist or adequacy determinations;
- **Require prompt notification** from processors in the event of any actual or suspected personal data breach, security incident, or material change in processing arrangements;
- **Ensure secure and verified disposal or return** of all personal data upon termination of any data processor relationship;
- **Maintain a central register** of all data processors engaged by the Organization, including the categories of personal data processed and the legal basis for processing.

Non-compliance with this policy by internal stakeholders or external processors will be treated as a serious matter and may result in disciplinary action, contract termination, or regulatory escalation as appropriate.

## 4. Third-Party Risk Classification Framework

*This section defines how the Organization categorizes third parties and data processors by risk level, and the corresponding governance obligations for each tier. Tailor the risk criteria and tier thresholds to match the Organization's risk appetite and the nature of its data processing activities.*

### 4.1 Risk Classification Principles

The Organization classifies all third parties and data processors across a defined risk tier structure based on the following criteria:

- **Volume of personal data** processed (number of data subjects affected);
- **Sensitivity of personal data** categories involved (including sensitive personal data);
- **Criticality of the business function** supported by the processor;
- **Nature and extent of access** to the Organization's systems, networks, or premises;
- **Geographic location** of processing and subprocessor chains;
- **Processor's data protection maturity** and certification status;
- **Regulatory scrutiny** applicable to the processing activity.

### 4.2 Risk Tier Definitions

| Risk Tier | Classification | Criteria | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — Critical** | Highest Risk | Processes sensitive personal data; large-scale processing (>10,000 data subjects); direct system access; cross-border transfers involved | Core banking processors, payment gateway providers, cloud infrastructure providers holding customer data |
| **Tier 2 — High** | High Risk | Processes standard personal data at scale (1,000–10,000 data subjects); significant system integration; or processes any sensitive personal data in limited volume | HR outsourcing, CRM platforms, employee health benefit administrators |
| **Tier 3 — Medium** | Moderate Risk | Processes limited personal data (100–999 data subjects); indirect access; low sensitivity | Marketing analytics, training platforms, event management services |
| **Tier 4 — Low** | Lower Risk | Incidental or minimal access to personal data; fewer than 100 data subjects; standardized service with no customization of processing | Courier services with limited address data, facility management |

### 4.3 Tier-Based Governance Obligations

*For each risk tier, specify the minimum governance requirements the Organization imposes. These should be proportionate to the risk level.*

| Governance Obligation | Tier 1 — Critical | Tier 2 — High | Tier 3 — Medium | Tier 4 — Low |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Full due diligence assessment | Mandatory | Mandatory | Mandatory | Simplified assessment |
| Data Processing Agreement | Mandatory | Mandatory | Mandatory | Mandatory |
| On-site audit rights | Annually | Annually | Every 2 years | Upon request |
| Remote / documentary audit | Semi-annually | Annually | Annually | Every 2 years |
| Security questionnaire | Mandatory | Mandatory | Mandatory | Simplified |
| ISO 27001 or equivalent certification | Required | Preferred | Preferred | Not required |
| Subprocessor notification requirement | 30 days advance notice | 30 days advance notice | 60 days advance notice | Notification on request |
| Breach notification SLA | 24 hours | 24 hours | 48 hours | 72 hours |
| DPO approval required for engagement | Mandatory | Mandatory | Recommended | Departmental sign-off |
| Annual processor performance review | Mandatory | Mandatory | Biennial | Ad hoc |

### 4.4 Reclassification Process

*Describe when and how a processor's risk tier may be reviewed and changed.*

- Risk tiers shall be reviewed at a minimum annually as part of the processor performance review cycle;
- Immediate reclassification may be triggered by: a data breach or security incident, material change in processing scope, acquisition or restructuring of the processor entity, regulatory change, or adverse audit findings;
- Reclassification proposals shall be submitted to the DPO for approval and recorded in the Third-Party Register;
- Processors shall be notified in writing of any reclassification that results in increased compliance obligations.

## 5. Due Diligence Requirements Before Engagement

*This section sets out the mandatory steps the Organization must complete before entering into any data processing relationship. The level of due diligence must be proportionate to the processor's assigned risk tier.*

### 5.1 Pre-Engagement Approval Gate

No third party or data processor shall commence processing personal data on behalf of [Organization Name] until:

1. A risk tier assessment has been completed and formally assigned;
2. All tier-appropriate due diligence steps have been completed and documented;
3. A signed Data Processing Agreement is in place;
4. DPO approval (or departmental approval for Tier 4) has been obtained and recorded;
5. The processor has been entered into the Organization's Third-Party and Processor Register.

### 5.2 Due Diligence Assessment Components

*Specify the documents, certifications, and assessments required at each stage of the pre-engagement process.*

#### 5.2.1 Documentation Review

Prospective processors shall be required to provide the following documentation, subject to risk tier:

| Document | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 | Tier 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Data Protection / Privacy Policy | Required | Required | Required | Required |
| Information Security Policy | Required | Required | Required | Not required |
| ISO 27001 Certificate (or equivalent) | Required | Preferred | Optional | Not required |
| SOC 2 Type II Report | Required | Preferred | Optional | Not required |
| Penetration Testing Report (last 12 months) | Required | Required | Optional | Not required |
| Business Continuity / Disaster Recovery Plan | Required | Required | Optional | Not required |
| Subprocessor List | Required | Required | Required | Required |
| Data Flow Diagram (showing personal data flows) | Required | Required | Preferred | Not required |
| Evidence of staff data protection training | Required | Required | Optional | Not required |
| Regulatory compliance attestation (PDPA or equivalent) | Required | Required | Required | Required |
| Financial stability / credit check | Required | Required | Optional | Not required |
| References / past client attestations | Required | Optional | Optional | Not required |

#### 5.2.2 Security Assessment

For Tier 1 and Tier 2 processors, the Organization shall conduct or commission a technical security assessment that shall include, at minimum:

- Completion of the Organization's **Vendor Security Questionnaire** (see **Appendix A**);
- Review of the processor's vulnerability management and patch management practices;
- Assessment of access control and identity management arrangements;
- Review of encryption standards applied to data in transit and at rest;
- Evaluation of third-party penetration testing results;
- [Any additional technical assessment criteria specific to Organization Name].

#### 5.2.3 Regulatory and Legal Standing

*Document the checks performed to confirm that the prospective processor has no disqualifying regulatory or legal history.*

The Organization shall conduct the following regulatory standing checks:

- Confirmation that the processor holds any required regulatory registrations, licences, or permits relevant to the processing activities;
- Review of any prior enforcement actions, fines, or regulatory findings issued by JPDP or equivalent data protection authorities;
- Sanctions screening against applicable lists (e.g., OFAC, UN, national blacklists);
- Company registry verification and UBO (Ultimate Beneficial Owner) identification where required by applicable law;
- Conflict of interest check against existing vendor relationships.

#### 5.2.4 Due Diligence Outcome and Record

Upon completion of due diligence, the assessor shall prepare a **Due Diligence Assessment Report** that includes:

- Summary of findings across each assessment component;
- Risk tier confirmation or adjustment;
- Identified risks and proposed mitigations;
- Recommendation to proceed, proceed with conditions, or decline engagement;
- DPO review and sign-off.

The completed Due Diligence Assessment Report shall be retained in the **Third-Party and Processor Register** for the duration of the processor relationship and for a minimum of **[X] years** post-termination in accordance with the Organization's data retention schedule.

## 6. Mandatory Contractual Clauses — Data Processing Agreements

*This section defines the minimum mandatory provisions that must be included in all Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) executed between the Organization and data processors. These clauses give effect to the Organization's obligations under PDPA Section 9.*

### 6.1 Requirement to Execute a DPA

Consistent with **PDPA Section 9**, [Organization Name] shall ensure that all data processors process personal data only on the basis of a written contract or legally binding act that is enforceable as between the data user and processor. No processing of personal data by a third party shall be permitted in the absence of a fully executed DPA.

The DPA shall be signed by an authorized representative of the processor prior to, and as a condition of, commencement of any processing activities. The DPA template shall be maintained by the DPO and reviewed annually.

### 6.2 Mandatory DPA Provisions

*The following provisions are mandatory in all DPAs executed by [Organization Name], regardless of risk tier. Additional clauses may be required for higher-risk tiers as indicated.*

All DPAs executed by [Organization Name] shall contain, at minimum, the following provisions:

#### 6.2.1 Subject Matter and Duration

- A clear description of the subject matter, nature, purpose, and duration of the processing;
- The type of personal data to be processed and the categories of data subjects;
- The rights and obligations of the Organization as data user.

#### 6.2.2 Processing Instructions

- An express requirement that the processor shall only process personal data on documented instructions from [Organization Name], and shall notify the Organization if, in the processor's opinion, an instruction infringes applicable data protection law;
- Prohibition on the processor processing personal data for any purpose other than that specified in the DPA.

#### 6.2.3 Confidentiality

- A binding obligation on the processor to ensure that persons authorised to process personal data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

#### 6.2.4 Security Measures

- Requirement to implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures to protect personal data, as specified in **Section 10.2** of this policy and as may be further detailed in the DPA Schedule;
- Obligation to assist the Organization in ensuring compliance with security obligations taking into account the nature of processing and information available to the processor.

#### 6.2.5 Subprocessor Controls

- Prohibition on engaging any subprocessor without prior written authorization from [Organization Name];
- Requirement that subprocessors be bound by equivalent data protection obligations as those imposed on the processor under the DPA;
- Processor's liability for subprocessor acts and omissions.

#### 6.2.6 Data Subject Rights Assistance

- Obligation on the processor to assist [Organization Name] in responding to data subject access requests and other data subject rights exercises, taking into account the nature of the processing;
- Defined response timelines consistent with the Organization's obligations under applicable law.

#### 6.2.7 Audit and Inspection Rights

- Right of [Organization Name] (or its designated auditors) to conduct audits and inspections of the processor in accordance with **Section 7** of this policy;
- Obligation on the processor to provide all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the DPA.

#### 6.2.8 Data Breach Notification

- Mandatory notification obligation upon discovery of any actual or suspected personal data breach, within the timeframes specified by risk tier in **Section 10.3** of this policy;
- Obligation to assist the Organization in breach containment, investigation, and regulatory notification.

#### 6.2.9 Cross-Border Transfer Controls

- Prohibition on transferring personal data outside Malaysia without prior written approval from [Organization Name];
- Obligation to comply with any conditions or safeguards specified by the Organization in relation to cross-border transfers.

#### 6.2.10 Deletion or Return of Data

- Obligation, at the choice of [Organization Name], to delete or return all personal data upon termination or expiry of the DPA, and to delete existing copies unless applicable law requires retention.

#### 6.2.11 Governing Law

- The DPA shall be governed by the laws of Malaysia, and the parties submit to the jurisdiction of Malaysian courts for resolution of any disputes arising under the DPA.

### 6.3 Additional Clauses for Tier 1 and Tier 2 Processors

*For higher-risk engagements, the following supplementary provisions shall also be included in the DPA.*

For Tier 1 — Critical and Tier 2 — High processors, the DPA shall additionally include:

- **Step-in rights** for the Organization in the event of a serious processor non-compliance event;
- **Business continuity obligations**, including minimum RTO/RPO targets for systems processing personal data;
- **Change management notification**, requiring the processor to notify the Organization of material changes to its security environment, processing infrastructure, or key personnel;
- **Insurance requirements**, specifying minimum levels of cyber liability and professional indemnity insurance;
- **Benchmarking and SLA provisions** for data access, availability, and integrity;
- **Regulatory cooperation obligations**, requiring the processor to cooperate with JPDP or any other competent authority in connection with any investigation involving the Organization's personal data;
- [Any other Organization-specific provisions].

### 6.4 DPA Template Management

- The DPO shall maintain the Organization's standard DPA template and all approved variations;
- Material deviations from the standard DPA template proposed by a processor require DPO approval before execution;
- All executed DPAs shall be stored in the **Third-Party and Processor Register** and indexed to the relevant vendor record;
- DPA templates shall be reviewed annually and updated to reflect changes in applicable law, regulatory guidance, or organizational risk appetite.

## 7. Audit and Inspection Rights Over Processors

- Conduct scheduled and unannounced audits of the processor's facilities, systems, and records relating to the processing of personal data;
- Review and inspect documentation, policies, procedures, logs, and reports relevant to the processor's data protection compliance;
- Conduct interviews with the processor's personnel involved in the processing of personal data;
- Commission independent third-party auditors to conduct assessments on the Organization's behalf.

### 7.2 Audit Schedule and Trigger Events

|---|---|---|---|---|

#### 7.2.2 Unscheduled / Cause Audits

## 8. Subprocessor Authorization and Notification Requirements

*This section governs the Organization's approach to subprocessors — third parties engaged by the Organization's data processors to carry out specific processing activities. The Organization, as data user, retains accountability for subprocessor compliance.*

### 8.1 General Authorization Requirement

Consistent with the Organization's DPA provisions, no data processor shall engage a subprocessor to process [Organization Name]'s personal data without prior written authorization from the DPO or designated approver.

Authorization may be granted in one of two forms:

- **Specific authorization**: The Organization expressly approves each individual subprocessor before engagement; or
- **General authorization with notification**: The Organization provides general approval for the processor to engage subprocessors subject to a mandatory advance notification and objection procedure as defined in Section 8.3.

The applicable form of authorization shall be specified in the DPA and shall be proportionate to the processor's risk tier.

| Risk Tier | Subprocessor Authorization Model |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 — Critical | Specific prior written authorization for each subprocessor |
| Tier 2 — High | Specific prior written authorization for each subprocessor |
| Tier 3 — Medium | General authorization with advance notification (minimum 30 days) |
| Tier 4 — Low | General authorization with notification within [X] days of engagement |

### 8.2 Approved Subprocessor List

Each data processor shall maintain and provide to the Organization a current and complete list of all subprocessors engaged in relation to the Organization's personal data. This list shall include:

- Subprocessor name and registered entity details;
- Country of establishment and country(ies) where processing occurs;
- Nature and purpose of processing performed by the subprocessor;
- Categories of personal data processed;
- Data retention terms applied by the subprocessor;
- Key security certifications held by the subprocessor.

The Approved Subprocessor List shall be reviewed and reconfirmed at each scheduled audit cycle and upon any change.

### 8.3 Subprocessor Change Notification and Objection Procedure

*Detail the notification process the processor must follow before adding or replacing a subprocessor, and the Organization's right to object.*

Where general authorization applies, the processor shall notify the Organization of any intended changes to the approved subprocessor list (additions or replacements) by:

1. Providing written notice to the DPO at least **[30 / 60] calendar days** before the change takes effect;
2. Including in the notice: the subprocessor's identity and location, the nature of processing, and the effective date of engagement;
3. Allowing the Organization a period of **[14 / 21] calendar days** from receipt of the notice to raise a documented objection.

If the Organization raises an objection on legitimate data protection grounds, the processor shall:

- Not engage the proposed subprocessor pending resolution;
- Work with the Organization in good faith to identify an alternative arrangement;
- If no resolution is reached within **[30] calendar days**, the matter shall be escalated as a contract dispute.

### 8.4 Flow-Down of Obligations

Processors shall impose upon each subprocessor, by way of written contract, data protection obligations that are at least equivalent to those imposed on the processor under the DPA with [Organization Name]. In particular, the subprocessor contract shall contain equivalent provisions regarding:

- Processing instructions and purpose limitation;
- Confidentiality;
- Security measures;
- Data subject rights assistance;
- Breach notification;
- Cross-border transfer controls;
- Deletion or return of data upon termination.

The processor remains fully liable to [Organization Name] for the performance of the subprocessor's obligations, as if the processor were performing such obligations itself.

### 8.5 Subprocessor Register

The DPO shall maintain a centralized **Subprocessor Register** as a component of the Third-Party and Processor Register, recording all approved subprocessors and their associated processors, processing activities, and authorization dates. The Subprocessor Register shall be reviewed and updated upon each notification of a subprocessor change.

## 9. Cross-Border Transfer Restrictions on Processors

*This section applies the requirements of PDPA Section 34 to data processors and subprocessors that process or store personal data outside Malaysia. The Organization must ensure that any cross-border transfer by or through a processor is lawful and adequately protected.*

### 9.1 General Prohibition on Unauthorized Cross-Border Transfers

No data processor shall transfer, transmit, store, or otherwise make available personal data obtained from [Organization Name] to any location outside Malaysia without:

1. Prior written approval from the DPO;
2. A lawful basis for the transfer under PDPA Section 34; and
3. Appropriate transfer safeguards in place as determined by the Organization.

This prohibition extends to subprocessors and any further sub-tiers of the processing chain.

### 9.2 Lawful Bases for Cross-Border Transfer

*List the recognized grounds under PDPA Section 34 and JPDP guidance under which cross-border transfers to processors may be authorized.*

The Organization recognises the following lawful bases for cross-border transfers by processors:

| Lawful Basis | Description | Applicable PDPA Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Whitelist Jurisdiction** | Transfer to a country or territory specified in the Minister's order as providing adequate protection | PDPA Section 34(1) |
| **Data Subject Consent** | The data subject has given consent to the proposed transfer, with appropriate information provided | PDPA Section 34(2)(a) |
| **Contractual Necessity** | Transfer is necessary for the performance of a contract between the data subject and the data user | PDPA Section 34(2)(b) |
| **Data User Instruction** | Transfer is made on the instructions of the data user and is necessary for a contract between the data user and a third party | PDPA Section 34(2)(c) |
| **Adequate Safeguards** | Transfer is subject to adequate safeguards as may be determined by the Commissioner | PDPA Section 34(2)(d) — refer to current JPDP guidance |
| **[Other basis per JPDP guidance]** | [Description] | [Reference] |

### 9.3 Pre-Approval Process for Processor Cross-Border Transfers

Before approving any processor cross-border transfer, the DPO shall assess:

- The destination country or territory and its data protection adequacy status;
- The specific categories and volume of personal data to be transferred;
- The purpose and legal basis for the transfer;
- The safeguards in place at the destination processor / subprocessor;
- The data subject's rights and available remedies in the destination jurisdiction;
- Applicable data localization requirements under Malaysian sectoral regulations (e.g., BNM Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, if applicable);
- Any relevant JPDP guidance or Commissioner's determinations in force.

The outcome of the assessment shall be documented in the **Cross-Border Transfer Assessment Record** (see **Appendix C**) and retained with the relevant DPA.

### 9.4 Contractual Safeguards for Cross-Border Transfers

Where a cross-border transfer by a processor is approved, the DPA shall include specific provisions addressing:

- The destination country or countries and specific locations of processing;
- The lawful basis under which the transfer is approved;
- The security standards and certifications required at the destination;
- The requirement for equivalent data subject rights in the destination jurisdiction, or compensating measures where rights are less robust;
- A flow-down requirement ensuring that any further onward transfer by the destination processor requires prior written approval from [Organization Name];
- The right to suspend or terminate the transfer in the event of a change in the destination jurisdiction's adequacy status or legal environment.

### 9.5 Ongoing Monitoring of Cross-Border Arrangements

- The DPO shall maintain a **Cross-Border Transfer Register** documenting all approved cross-border transfers by processors and subprocessors;
- The adequacy status of destination jurisdictions shall be reviewed annually or upon material change;
- Processors shall notify the Organization within **[X] business days** of any change in the jurisdictions in which personal data is stored or processed;
- The DPO shall assess the impact of any such change and may require additional safeguards or suspend the transfer pending re-assessment.

- Not notify any third parties (including regulatory authorities or affected data subjects) regarding the breach without prior written approval from the DPO, unless required to do so by applicable law;
- Provide a final Post-Incident Report within **[14 / 21] calendar days** of breach containment, including a root cause analysis and remediation plan;

## 11. Offboarding and Data Return or Deletion Requirements

*This section governs the process for terminating a data processor relationship and ensuring the secure and verified disposal or return of all personal data held by the processor. Inadequate offboarding is a significant source of ongoing data protection risk and must be managed with the same rigour as onboarding.*

### 11.1 Triggers for Offboarding

The offboarding process shall be initiated upon occurrence of any of the following events:

- Expiry or termination of the DPA or underlying service contract;
- Mutual agreement to discontinue the processor relationship;
- Termination for cause (including material breach of the DPA, serious security incident, or regulatory non-compliance);
- Insolvency or cessation of business by the processor;
- Change in organizational strategy or service requirements rendering the processor relationship unnecessary;
- Reclassification of the processing activity as to be performed by an internal team or a replacement processor.

### 11.2 Offboarding Planning

Upon initiation of offboarding, the relevant business owner shall notify the DPO and Procurement at least **[60 / 90] calendar days** before the intended termination date (where the termination is planned). An **Offboarding Plan** shall be prepared that includes:

- Identification of all personal data held by the processor (inventory);
- Determination of whether data is to be returned to the Organization or securely deleted;
- Timeline for data handover or deletion;
- Access revocation schedule;
- Handling of any active processing activities or in-flight transactions involving personal data;
- Transition plan for continuity of service (if applicable);
- Confirmation of subprocessors to be notified and their respective offboarding obligations;
- Identification of any regulatory notifications or data subject communications required.

### 11.3 Data Return or Deletion

Upon termination of the DPA, the processor shall, within the timeframes agreed in the Offboarding Plan (and in any event not later than **[30] calendar days** following the effective date of termination):

**Option A — Data Return:**
- Return all personal data to the Organization in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format;
- Provide a complete manifest of all data returned;
- Following confirmation of successful receipt by the Organization, securely delete all remaining copies of the personal data from all systems, including backup and archival systems.

**Option B — Secure Deletion:**
- Securely delete all personal data from all production, backup, archival, and test environments;
- Apply a recognized secure deletion standard (e.g., NIST SP 800-88, DoD 5220.22-M, or equivalent);
- For physical media, provide certificates of destruction issued by a certified media destruction provider.

Regardless of the option selected, the processor shall provide the Organization with a written **Data Deletion / Return Certificate** confirming:

- The scope of data deleted or returned;
- The deletion method or standard applied;
- The date of deletion or return completion;
- Confirmation that no copies of the personal data are retained by the processor or any of its subprocessors, except as required by applicable law.

### 11.4 Access Revocation

Upon initiation of offboarding:

- All logical access (user accounts, API keys, credentials, VPN access, system integrations) granted to the processor shall be scheduled for revocation;
- Access shall be revoked no later than the effective termination date, or earlier if required by the Offboarding Plan;
- Physical access (premises, secure areas) shall be revoked upon the effective termination date;
- The IT and Security team shall confirm revocation of all access to the DPO in writing within **[5] business days** of the effective termination date.

### 11.5 Subprocessor Offboarding

The Organization's data processor is responsible for ensuring that all subprocessors engaged in processing the Organization's personal data are subject to equivalent offboarding obligations, including:

- Notification of termination of the subprocessing arrangement;
- Return or secure deletion of personal data by subprocessors within the same timelines;
- Provision of subprocessor deletion certificates to the Organization.

### 11.6 Post-Termination Obligations

Following completion of offboarding:

- The processor relationship shall be closed in the **Third-Party and Processor Register**;
- All executed DPAs, due diligence records, audit reports, and offboarding documentation shall be retained by the Organization for a minimum of **[X] years** in accordance with the Organization's data retention schedule;
- Any residual disputes arising from the processor relationship shall be escalated to Legal and the DPO;
- A post-termination review shall be conducted for Tier 1 and Tier 2 processors to identify lessons learned and feed into improvements to the Organization's processor management framework.

## 13. Non-Compliance and Enforcement

*This section defines the consequences of non-compliance with this policy, both for internal stakeholders and external data processors.*

### 13.1 Internal Non-Compliance

Non-compliance with this policy by [Organization Name] staff, including engaging a processor without completing required due diligence, executing a DPA without required approvals, or failing to report a processor-related breach, shall be treated as a serious disciplinary matter and may result in:

- Formal disciplinary proceedings under the Organization's disciplinary policy;
- Mandatory data protection training and supervised remediation;
- Escalation to senior management;
- Referral to the Human Resources function;
- Where applicable, reporting to the relevant professional regulatory body.

### 13.2 Processor Non-Compliance

Where a data processor is found to be in breach of the DPA or the requirements of this policy, the Organization shall:

1. **Issue a formal notice of breach** to the processor, specifying the non-compliance and required remediation;
2. **Require a remediation plan** from the processor within **[5 / 10] business days**;
3. **Increase audit frequency** or impose additional monitoring requirements pending remediation;
4. **Suspend the processing** activity if the breach presents an immediate material risk to data subjects;
5. **Terminate the DPA** if the processor fails to remediate within the agreed timeline or commits a material breach;
6. **Notify JPDP** as required by applicable law or as determined appropriate by the DPO;
7. **Seek legal remedies** including damages and injunctive relief as appropriate.

### 13.3 Escalation Framework

| Severity | Escalation Path | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Critical non-compliance (e.g., unauthorized processing, unnotified breach) | DPO → Senior Management → Board / Risk Committee | Immediate |
| High non-compliance (e.g., repeated audit failures, failure to remediate findings) | DPO → Senior Management | Within 24 hours |
| Medium non-compliance (e.g., late reporting, subprocessor notification failure) | DPO → Business Owner | Within 3 business days |
| Low non-compliance (e.g., procedural gaps, documentation deficiencies) | DPO → Processor relationship owner | Within 5 business days |

No exception shall be granted for a period exceeding **[12] months** without re-assessment and re-approval.
