## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This procedure establishes a structured and consistent approach for [Organization Name] to receive, acknowledge, investigate, and resolve complaints submitted by data subjects in relation to the processing of their personal data. It ensures that complaints are handled fairly, transparently, and in compliance with the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**, including the obligations set out under sections 30, 34, 43, 101, and 102 of the Act.

This procedure also defines the circumstances and process for escalating unresolved complaints to the **Personal Data Protection Commissioner** ("the Commissioner"), thereby meeting [Organization Name]'s statutory obligations and demonstrating accountability to data subjects and regulators.

### 1.2 Scope

This procedure applies to:

- All **data subjects** (individuals whose personal data is processed by [Organization Name]) who wish to submit a complaint relating to any aspect of data protection, including the collection, use, disclosure, storage, correction, or deletion of their personal data.
- All **employees, contractors, vendors, and third-party processors** acting on behalf of [Organization Name] who receive or handle data protection complaints.
- All **personal data processing activities** conducted by [Organization Name], including those carried out through digital platforms, physical channels, and third-party service providers.
- **All business divisions, departments, and subsidiaries** of [Organization Name] operating within Malaysia or processing personal data of Malaysian residents.

### 1.3 Out of Scope

This procedure does not apply to:

- Complaints relating to financial products or services that are governed exclusively by other regulatory frameworks (e.g., BNM's Financial Services Act 2013), unless such complaints also involve a data protection element.
- Internal employee grievances relating to HR matters, unless the grievance involves the processing of personal data.
- Freedom of Information requests or queries that do not constitute a complaint under the PDPA.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 RACI Matrix

*The table below assigns responsibility for each key activity in the complaint handling lifecycle. Authors should verify role titles against [Organization Name]'s current organisational structure.*

| Conduct trend analysis and reporting | A | R | I | C | — | I |
| Review and update this procedure annually | A | C | I | C | — | A |

### 4.2 Role Descriptions

**Data Protection Officer (DPO)**

The DPO is the primary accountable owner of this procedure. The DPO is responsible for ensuring that all complaints are handled in accordance with the PDPA and this procedure, escalating systemic issues to senior management, liaising with the Commissioner where required, and maintaining oversight of the complaint register.

**Complaint Handler**

The Complaint Handler is the individual assigned day-to-day responsibility for managing a specific complaint. The Complaint Handler conducts the investigation, coordinates with relevant business units, drafts response communications to the data subject, and maintains complete records of the complaint file.

**Business Unit Head**

The Business Unit Head provides subject matter expertise and access to relevant data, systems, and staff during the investigation. They are responsible for implementing any remedial actions identified as part of the resolution.

**Legal & Compliance**

Legal & Compliance is consulted on complaints that raise potential regulatory, civil, or criminal liability. They advise on response strategies where referral to the Commissioner is anticipated and manage any formal correspondence with the Commissioner's office.

**IT / Security**

IT / Security is consulted in cases where a complaint involves a suspected data breach, unauthorized access, or technical failure that has led to the misuse of personal data.

**Senior Management**

Senior Management is informed of complaints that have been escalated internally and provides approval authority for any significant remedial action, compensation, or referral to the Commissioner.

## 6. Acknowledgement of Complaints

*This section establishes the timeline and content requirements for acknowledging receipt of a complaint. Acknowledgement within 3 business days is a key compliance obligation.*

### 6.1 Acknowledgement Timeline

Upon receipt of a complaint through any approved channel, [Organization Name] shall issue a written acknowledgement to the complainant **within three (3) business days**.

### 6.2 Acknowledgement Content

The acknowledgement communication shall include the following information:

- Confirmation that the complaint has been received and logged.
- A unique **Complaint Reference Number** assigned from the complaint register.
- The name and contact details of the assigned Complaint Handler.
- An explanation of the complaint handling process, including the expected timeframe for resolution.
- A reference to the data subject's right to escalate the complaint to the Commissioner under **s101 of the PDPA** if the complaint is not resolved to their satisfaction.
- A request for any additional information required to investigate the complaint, if applicable.

## 7. Complaint Classification and Assignment

*This section describes how complaints are categorised and allocated to the appropriate handler. Accurate classification enables efficient investigation and informs trend reporting.*

### 7.1 Complaint Categories

All complaints shall be classified into one or more of the following categories upon intake:

| Category Code | Category | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| CAT-01 | Access Rights | Complaint that a right of access request (s30 PDPA) was refused, delayed, or not properly fulfilled. | Data subject claims their SAR was not responded to within the required timeframe. |
| CAT-02 | Correction Rights | Complaint that a request to correct inaccurate personal data (s34 PDPA) was refused or not actioned. | Data subject's address was not updated despite a written correction request. |
| CAT-03 | Unauthorised Disclosure | Complaint that personal data was disclosed to an unauthorized third party. | Data subject received marketing calls from a company they did not engage. |
| CAT-04 | Data Breach | Complaint arising from a known or suspected breach of the data subject's personal data. | Data subject notified that their account details appeared in a public leak. |
| CAT-05 | Excessive Collection | Complaint that [Organization Name] collected personal data beyond what was necessary. | Data subject objects to collection of biometric data for a routine transaction. |
| CAT-06 | Retention | Complaint that personal data was retained beyond its necessary period. | Data subject requested erasure after account closure; data not deleted. |
| CAT-07 | Marketing / Opt-Out | Complaint that the data subject's opt-out of direct marketing was not respected. | Data subject continues to receive promotional emails after unsubscribing. |
| CAT-08 | General | Any other complaint relating to the processing of personal data not captured above. | [Describe] |

### 7.2 Priority Classification

| Priority | Criteria | Target Resolution |
|---|---|---|
| **High** | Complaint involves a potential data breach, ongoing unauthorized processing, regulatory referral risk, or vulnerable data subject. | [10] business days |
| **Medium** | Complaint relates to unfulfilled data subject rights or non-compliance with consent obligations. | [20] business days |
| **Low** | Complaint relates to process, communication, or service quality with no ongoing data risk. | [30] business days |

### 7.3 Assignment

Upon classification, the DPO (or designated delegate) shall assign the complaint to an appropriate Complaint Handler within **one (1) business day** of receipt. Assignment shall consider:

- The category and complexity of the complaint.
- The business unit(s) involved in the processing activity.
- The Complaint Handler's current workload and subject matter expertise.
- Any conflict of interest (the Complaint Handler must not be from the team directly implicated in the complaint).

The assignment shall be recorded in the Complaint Register.

## 8. Investigation Procedure and Evidence Gathering

*This section outlines the steps the Complaint Handler must follow to conduct a thorough and impartial investigation. The investigation must be completed within the target resolution timeframe established at classification.*

### 8.1 Investigation Initiation

Within **two (2) business days** of assignment, the Complaint Handler shall:

1. Review the complaint in full and confirm the complaint category and priority.
2. Identify all internal stakeholders, systems, and data processing activities relevant to the complaint.
3. Notify the relevant Business Unit Head of the complaint and the requirement to cooperate with the investigation.
4. Prepare an **Investigation Plan** (see Appendix B) documenting the scope, approach, and evidence to be gathered.

### 8.2 Evidence Gathering

The Complaint Handler shall gather all evidence relevant to the complaint. Evidence may include, but is not limited to:

- **System records** — access logs, audit trails, processing records, and database entries relating to the data subject's personal data.
- **Communications** — emails, letters, call recordings, chat logs, or other correspondence between [Organization Name] and the data subject.
- **Consent records** — evidence of how and when consent was obtained, including the specific purposes consented to.
- **Data subject rights records** — logs of any prior access, correction, or other rights requests submitted by the data subject.
- **Policies and procedures** — copies of the policies and procedures in effect at the time of the alleged breach.
- **Vendor and third-party records** — where the complaint involves a data processor, relevant agreements and processing logs.

### 8.3 Evidence Integrity

All evidence gathered must be:

- Documented in the complaint file with a clear chain of custody.
- Stored securely in the designated complaint management system or secure file share: [System Name / Location].
- Retained in accordance with the Records Retention Schedule: [Document ID/Title].

### 8.4 Data Subject Engagement During Investigation

Where additional information is required from the data subject, the Complaint Handler shall:

1. Contact the data subject in writing, clearly stating the information required and the reason for the request.
2. Provide a reasonable deadline (minimum [10] business days) for the data subject to respond.
3. Document all communications with the data subject in the complaint file.

Where the data subject fails to respond within the deadline, the Complaint Handler may proceed to resolution on the basis of available evidence, informing the data subject accordingly.

### 8.5 Investigation Findings

Upon completion of evidence gathering, the Complaint Handler shall prepare written **Investigation Findings** documenting:

- A summary of the complaint and the investigation scope.
- Key facts established during the investigation.
- An assessment of whether [Organization Name]'s processing of the data subject's personal data complied with the PDPA and relevant internal policies.
- Recommended resolution and any proposed remedial actions.

The Investigation Findings shall be reviewed and approved by the DPO before a resolution is communicated to the data subject.

## 9. Resolution Options and Compensation Considerations

*This section defines the range of outcomes available at the conclusion of an investigation and the process for determining appropriate remedial action, including any financial compensation.*

### 9.1 Resolution Outcomes

Following review of the Investigation Findings, the DPO shall determine one of the following resolution outcomes:

| Outcome Code | Outcome | Description |
|---|---|---|
| RES-01 | **Complaint Upheld — Remedial Action Taken** | The investigation confirms that [Organization Name] failed to comply with the PDPA or its own policies. Remedial action is taken and communicated to the data subject. |
| RES-02 | **Complaint Partially Upheld** | The investigation confirms some, but not all, aspects of the complaint. Partial remedial action is taken. |
| RES-03 | **Complaint Not Upheld** | The investigation finds no evidence of non-compliance. The data subject is provided with a clear explanation. |
| RES-04 | **Complaint Withdrawn** | The data subject withdraws the complaint prior to resolution. The complaint file is closed and documented. |
| RES-05 | **Complaint Referred to Commissioner** | Internal resolution cannot be achieved and the complaint is referred to or has been escalated by the data subject to the Commissioner. |

### 9.2 Remedial Actions

Where a complaint is upheld (fully or partially), remedial actions may include one or more of the following:

- **Correction or deletion** of the data subject's personal data where it was found to be inaccurate, excessive, or unlawfully retained.
- **Cessation of processing** where processing was found to be without a valid legal basis or consent.
- **Provision of access** to the data subject's personal data where access was wrongfully refused.
- **Notification to third parties** where personal data was disclosed to an unauthorized recipient and notification is feasible.
- **Process improvement** — amendment to internal policies, procedures, systems, or training to prevent recurrence.
- **Formal apology** — a written apology to the data subject where appropriate.
- **Financial compensation** — refer to Section 9.3.

### 9.3 Compensation Considerations

*Authors should tailor this section to [Organization Name]'s compensation policy and risk appetite. Any financial compensation must be approved by the appropriate level of authority.*

Where the complaint is upheld and the data subject has suffered demonstrable loss, damage, or distress as a result of [Organization Name]'s non-compliance, [Organization Name] may consider offering financial compensation. The following factors shall be considered:

- The nature and severity of the non-compliance.
- The actual or potential harm suffered by the data subject (e.g., financial loss, reputational damage, distress).
- Whether the non-compliance was the result of deliberate, negligent, or inadvertent conduct.
- [Organization Name]'s prior compensation history for similar complaints.
- Legal advice received from [Organization Name]'s internal or external legal counsel.

| Compensation Level | Approval Authority | Amount Range |
|---|---|---|
| Level 1 — Minor | DPO | Up to [RM XXXX] |
| Level 2 — Moderate | [Senior Manager / Head of Compliance] | [RM XXXX] – [RM XXXX] |
| Level 3 — Significant | [CEO / Board] | Above [RM XXXX] |

All offers of compensation shall be documented in the complaint file and, where accepted, the terms of settlement shall be recorded in a signed written agreement.

## 10. Escalation to DPO and Management

*This section defines the triggers and process for escalating complaints beyond the Complaint Handler to the DPO, senior management, or Board level.*

### 10.1 Escalation Triggers

A complaint shall be escalated to the DPO and/or senior management where any of the following conditions are met:

- The complaint involves a **suspected or confirmed personal data breach** affecting a significant number of data subjects.
- The complainant has indicated an **intention to refer the complaint to the Commissioner** or to seek legal remedies.
- The complaint raises a **systemic issue** indicating a broader failure in [Organization Name]'s data protection practices.
- The complaint involves **sensitive personal data** (as defined under s4 PDPA), including health information, financial data, or biometric data.
- The resolution requires **remedial action or compensation** above Level 1 authority (see Section 9.3).
- The complaint cannot be resolved within the **target resolution timeframe**.
- The complaint involves a **senior employee, director, or officer** of [Organization Name].
- The DPO identifies **regulatory or reputational risk** arising from the complaint.

### 10.2 Escalation Process

| Step | Action | Responsibility | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Complaint Handler identifies escalation trigger and notifies DPO. | Complaint Handler | Immediately upon trigger |
| 2 | DPO reviews complaint file and confirms escalation requirement. | DPO | Within 1 business day |
| 3 | DPO briefs relevant Business Unit Head and [Head of Compliance / Legal]. | DPO | Within 1 business day |
| 4 | DPO prepares **Escalation Summary** and circulates to Senior Management where required. | DPO | Within 2 business days |
| 5 | Senior Management convenes for decision on resolution strategy, remedial action, and/or Commissioner referral. | Senior Management | Within [3] business days |
| 6 | Resolution strategy is communicated to Complaint Handler for execution. | DPO | Within 1 business day of decision |
| 7 | Data subject is notified of updated resolution timeframe where applicable. | Complaint Handler | Within 1 business day |

### 10.3 Escalation Register

All escalated complaints shall be separately flagged in the Complaint Register and included in DPO reporting to Senior Management and the Board (see Section 13.3).

## 11. Communication with Data Subject on Outcome

*This section governs the final communication to the data subject following the conclusion of the investigation. Clear, transparent communication is essential to maintaining trust and demonstrating accountability.*

### 11.1 Outcome Communication Timeline

[Organization Name] shall communicate the outcome of the complaint to the data subject **within the target resolution timeframe** set at the time of classification (see Section 7.2), or within an extended timeframe communicated to the data subject in advance.

### 11.2 Outcome Communication Content

The written outcome communication shall include:

- A reference to the **Complaint Reference Number** and date of receipt.
- A summary of the **complaint as understood** by [Organization Name].
- A clear statement of the **outcome** (upheld, partially upheld, or not upheld).
- A **plain language explanation** of the investigation findings and the basis for the outcome.
- Details of any **remedial actions** taken or planned, including timelines for implementation.
- Where applicable, details of any **compensation** offered and how it may be accepted.
- A statement of the data subject's right to **refer the complaint to the Commissioner** under s101 of the PDPA if they are not satisfied with the outcome, including the Commissioner's contact details.
- Contact details of the DPO for any follow-up queries.

### 11.3 Outcome Communication Template

*Authors should attach or reference the standard outcome letter template. See **Appendix C** for the template.*

### 11.4 Communication Method

Outcome communications shall be sent via the same channel used by the data subject to submit the complaint, unless the data subject has requested an alternative method.

### 11.5 Follow-Up

Where the data subject responds to the outcome communication with further information or requests a review, the Complaint Handler shall:

1. Assess whether the new information materially changes the investigation findings.
2. Where material new information is provided, reopen the investigation and notify the data subject of the revised timeline.
3. Where no new information is provided, confirm the outcome and reiterate the data subject's right to escalate to the Commissioner.

## 12. Escalation to the Personal Data Protection Commissioner

*This section governs the process applicable when a complaint is referred to the Commissioner, either by the data subject or by [Organization Name], pursuant to ss101–102 of the PDPA.*

### 12.1 Data Subject's Right to Refer

Under **s101 of the PDPA**, a data subject who is not satisfied with the outcome of an internal complaint, or where [Organization Name] has failed to respond within a reasonable time, has the right to submit a complaint directly to the Commissioner. [Organization Name] shall not obstruct, discourage, or penalise any data subject who exercises this right.

### 12.2 Notification of Commissioner Referral

Where a data subject notifies [Organization Name] that they have referred or intend to refer a complaint to the Commissioner, the Complaint Handler shall:

1. Immediately notify the **DPO and Legal & Compliance**.
2. Ensure that the **complaint file** is complete, accurate, and securely preserved.
3. Initiate a legal hold on all records relating to the data subject's personal data and the complaint.

### 12.3 Response to Commissioner's Investigation

Where the Commissioner initiates an investigation under **s102 of the PDPA**, [Organization Name] shall:

| Step | Action | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Designate a **Single Point of Contact** (SPOC) for all communications with the Commissioner. | DPO / Legal |
| 2 | Acknowledge receipt of the Commissioner's investigation notice within the timeframe specified. | SPOC |
| 3 | Compile and submit all requested documents, records, and evidence to the Commissioner. | Complaint Handler / IT |
| 4 | Cooperate fully with any on-site inspection or interview conducted by the Commissioner's officers. | All departments |
| 5 | Review and respond to any **Preliminary Findings** issued by the Commissioner within the specified timeframe. | DPO / Legal |
| 6 | Implement any **remedial directions** issued by the Commissioner promptly and document compliance. | DPO / Business Unit |
| 7 | Report on implementation of Commissioner's directions to Senior Management and the Board. | DPO |

### 12.4 Non-Cooperation

Failure to cooperate with a Commissioner investigation may constitute an offence under the PDPA. All employees are required to cooperate fully with investigations and must not destroy, conceal, or alter any records relevant to a complaint.

## 13. Record-Keeping and Trend Analysis

*This section establishes [Organization Name]'s obligations to maintain accurate complaint records and to use complaint data to drive continuous improvement in data protection practices.*

### 13.1 Complaint Register

The DPO shall maintain a **Complaint Register** for all data protection complaints received. The register shall be stored in [Designated System / Secure File Location] and updated in real time by the assigned Complaint Handler.

The Complaint Register shall capture, at a minimum, the following fields:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Complaint Reference Number | Unique identifier assigned at intake (format: PDPA-COMP-[YYYY]-[NNNN]) |
| Date Received | Date the complaint was received |
| Data Subject Name | Full name of the complainant |
| Data Subject Contact | Email / telephone / postal address |
| Channel of Receipt | Email / Online Form / In Person / Post / Telephone |
| Complaint Summary | Brief description of the complaint |
| Category | Classification code (CAT-01 to CAT-08) |
| Priority | High / Medium / Low |
| Assigned Complaint Handler | Name and department |
| Date Acknowledged | Date acknowledgement sent |
| Investigation Start Date | Date investigation formally commenced |
| Target Resolution Date | Based on priority classification |
| Outcome | RES-01 to RES-05 |
| Remedial Actions | Description of actions taken |
| Compensation Offered | Amount and acceptance status |
| Commissioner Referral | Yes / No; if Yes, reference number |
| Date Closed | Date complaint file formally closed |
| Notes | Any additional relevant information |

### 13.2 Complaint File

For each complaint, the Complaint Handler shall maintain a **Complaint File** containing:

- Original complaint communication (email, form, letter, or written record of in-person submission).
- Acknowledgement communication.
- Investigation Plan.
- All evidence gathered during the investigation.
- Investigation Findings document.
- All communications with the data subject.
- Outcome communication.
- Records of any compensation agreed.
- Any correspondence with the Commissioner.

Complaint files shall be retained for a minimum of **[7] years** from the date of closure, in accordance with [Organization Name]'s Records Retention Schedule ([Document ID/Title]).

### 13.3 Trend Analysis and Reporting

The DPO shall conduct a **quarterly analysis** of complaint data to identify patterns, systemic issues, and areas for improvement. The analysis shall be documented in a **Complaint Trends Report** and shall include:

- Total number of complaints received by category and priority.
- Average resolution time by category and priority.
- Number of complaints upheld, partially upheld, and not upheld.
- Number of complaints escalated internally and to the Commissioner.
- Identified root causes of recurring complaints.
- Recommended corrective actions and process improvements.
- Status of previously recommended actions.

The Complaint Trends Report shall be presented to:

| Audience | Frequency | Format |
|---|---|---|
| DPO (internal review) | Quarterly | Full report |
| Senior Management / Compliance Committee | Quarterly | Summary dashboard |
| Board / Board Risk Committee | Annually | Annual summary |
| External Auditor / Regulator | Upon request | As requested |

### 13.4 Corrective and Preventive Actions

Where trend analysis identifies a systemic issue or recurring non-compliance, the DPO shall initiate a **Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA)** through [Organization Name]'s compliance management process. Each CAPA shall have:

- A designated owner.
- A defined corrective action.
- An implementation deadline.
- A verification step to confirm effectiveness.

CAPAs arising from complaint trend analysis shall be tracked in the Complaint Register and reported in the Complaint Trends Report.

## 14. Review and Approval

### 14.1 Review Schedule

This procedure shall be reviewed **annually**, or earlier in the event of:

- An amendment to the PDPA or related subsidiary legislation.
