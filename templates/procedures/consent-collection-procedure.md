## 1. Purpose

This procedure establishes the minimum standards and step-by-step requirements for collecting, recording, and managing valid consent from data subjects at [Organisation Name], in full compliance with the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA) of Malaysia.

Valid consent under the PDPA must satisfy the following cumulative criteria, as interpreted in accordance with s2 and s6 of the Act:

- **Freely given** — the data subject must not be subjected to coercion, undue pressure, or a power imbalance that compromises the voluntary nature of their agreement;
- **Specific** — consent must relate to a clearly identified and defined processing purpose, not a broad or open-ended category of activities;
- **Informed** — the data subject must have received, in a clear and accessible manner, all material information necessary to understand what they are consenting to before providing consent; and
- **Unambiguous** — consent must be expressed through a clear, affirmative act; silence, inaction, or ambiguity shall not constitute valid consent.

This procedure provides operational guidance to all personnel involved in the design, delivery, and recording of consent interactions, and ensures that [Organisation Name]'s consent practices are audit-ready, defensible, and aligned with regulatory expectations.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to:

- **All personal data processing activities** conducted by [Organisation Name] that rely on consent as the legal basis under s6 of the PDPA;
- **All channels** through which consent is collected, including digital platforms, physical forms, telephone, and in-person interactions;
- **All departments and business units** of [Organisation Name] that collect, process, or manage personal data from data subjects, including customers, prospective customers, employees, contractors, and third-party individuals where consent is required;
- **All third-party vendors, agents, and service providers** operating on behalf of [Organisation Name] who collect personal data under a data processing agreement; and
- **All personal data categories**, including sensitive personal data as defined under s4 of the PDPA.

This procedure does **not** apply to processing activities for which another lawful basis under s6 applies (e.g., contract performance, legal obligation, or vital interests), unless consent is additionally obtained as a matter of policy.

## 5. Consent Collection Channels and Method-Specific Requirements

*This section defines each channel through which [Organisation Name] collects consent and the specific procedural requirements applicable to each. All channels must meet the baseline validity criteria in Section 1. Teams must consult the DPO before introducing a new consent collection channel not listed here.*

### 5.1 Overview of Approved Consent Collection Channels

| Channel | Method | Applicable Contexts | Approved |
|---|---|---|---|
| Digital — Web Platform | Online form with active opt-in checkbox | Account registration, product applications, marketing | Yes |
| Digital — Mobile Application | In-app consent screen with affirmative tap | Onboarding, feature enablement, notifications | Yes |
| Digital — Email | Linked consent form or structured reply | Remote customer interactions | Yes / Subject to DPO approval |
| Physical — Paper Form | Signed written consent form | Branch, in-person, field engagements | Yes |
| Verbal — Telephone | Recorded verbal consent with script | Call centre, outbound sales | Yes / Subject to compliance controls below |
| In-Person — Branch | Written or digital form completed in-branch | Account opening, advisory services | Yes |
| Third-Party Agent | Agent-collected consent on behalf of [Organisation Name] | Authorised agent networks | Yes / Subject to agent agreement |

### 5.2 Digital Channel Requirements

*Document each step required before, during, and after a digital consent interaction. Include system configuration requirements and user experience (UX) standards.*

1. **Pre-Interaction Design:**
   - Consent form must be presented **prior** to any personal data being collected or processed.
   - The consent request must be clearly separated from terms and conditions, privacy policies, or other contractual documents.
   - Default states for all consent checkboxes or toggles must be **unchecked / off**.
   - The purpose for which consent is sought must be displayed **adjacent to** the consent mechanism, not linked away.

2. **At Point of Collection:**
   - The data subject must take a clear affirmative action (e.g., tick a box, tap "I Agree", or click a clearly labelled consent button).
   - The system must render the full s7 notice text or a clear summary with a link to the full notice before the affirmative action is enabled.

3. **Post-Interaction Recording:**
   - See Section 9 for technical recording requirements.

### 5.3 Physical Channel Requirements

*Define requirements for paper-based consent, including form design, witnessing, and document handling.*

1. **Form Design:**
   - All physical consent forms must be reviewed and approved by the DPO prior to deployment.
   - Forms must include: the full organisation name, the specific purpose(s) for which consent is sought (one box per purpose), the data subject's right to withdraw consent and how to do so, the contact details of the DPO, and space for the data subject's signature and date.
   - Forms must not contain pre-ticked boxes. See Section 8.

2. **Completion and Witnessing:**
   - The staff member facilitating the consent interaction must confirm verbally that the data subject has read and understood the consent request.
   - Where the data subject cannot read the form (e.g., due to language barriers or disability), the form must be read aloud in full, and the staff member must note this on the form.

3. **Post-Collection Handling:**
   - See Section 10 for scanning, storage, and linkage requirements.

### 5.4 Verbal / Telephone Channel Requirements

*Define the script, recording, and storage requirements for verbal consent obtained over the telephone.*

1. **Pre-Call Preparation:**
   - All telephone consent interactions must use a DPO-approved script. Scripts must be version-controlled and stored in [Document Location].
   - The call must be recorded using [Organisation Name]'s approved call recording system.

2. **Consent Script — Minimum Elements:**
   - Identification of [Organisation Name] and the agent's name;
   - The specific purpose(s) for which consent is sought;
   - Categories of personal data to be processed;
   - Third parties to whom data may be disclosed;
   - The data subject's right to withdraw consent at any time and the method for doing so;
   - A clear question seeking affirmative consent (e.g., "Do you agree to [Organisation Name] processing your personal data for [purpose]? Please say 'Yes' or 'No'").

3. **Recording and Storage:**
   - The call recording must be tagged with the data subject's account or reference number, date, time, and agent ID.
   - Recordings must be retained in accordance with the Records Retention Schedule.
   - A consent record entry must be created in [CRM / Consent Management System] within [X] business hours of the call.

### 5.5 Third-Party Agent Requirements

*Define the obligations on agents collecting consent on behalf of [Organisation Name].*

- Agents must be trained on this procedure and the requirements of the PDPA prior to collecting any consent.
- Agents must use only [Organisation Name]-approved consent forms and scripts.
- Agents must submit collected consent records to [Organisation Name] within [X] business days of collection.
- [Organisation Name] retains liability as data controller for any consent collected by agents on its behalf. Non-compliant collection by an agent must be reported to the DPO and addressed under the agent agreement.

## 7. Granular Consent Design

*Granular consent ensures that data subjects retain meaningful control over their personal data by requiring a separate affirmative act for each distinct processing purpose. This section defines the design principles and implementation requirements for granular consent at [Organisation Name].*

### 7.1 Principle of Granularity

[Organisation Name] shall adopt a **one consent, one purpose** model:

- Each distinct processing purpose must be presented to the data subject as a **separate, independent consent item**.
- A data subject's agreement to one purpose must not be construed as agreement to any other purpose.
- The consequences of declining any individual consent item must be clearly stated, and the data subject must not be penalised for withholding consent for purposes that are not necessary for the delivery of the core service.

### 7.2 Purpose Classification Framework

*The table below provides a framework for classifying processing purposes and determining the applicable consent treatment. The DPO must maintain a complete and current Purpose Register.*

| Purpose Category | Example | Consent Required? | Granular Item Required? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core Service Delivery | Processing transaction data to execute a payment | No (Contract basis) | N/A |
| Marketing — Own Products | Sending promotional material for [Organisation Name] products | Yes | Yes |
| Marketing — Third-Party Products | Sharing data with partners for their marketing | Yes | Yes |
| Analytics and Profiling | Behavioural profiling for product personalisation | Yes | Yes |
| Research and Development | Using anonymised data for product improvement | Yes (if identifiable) | Yes |
| Sensitive Data Processing | Processing health data for insurance underwriting | Yes | Yes |
| Cross-Border Data Transfer | Transferring data to overseas processors | Yes (if required) | Yes |

### 7.3 Consent Form Design Requirements for Granular Consent

- Each consent item must be presented with:
  - A clear, plain-language description of the specific purpose;
  - The category of personal data involved;
  - Whether and to whom the data may be disclosed;
  - A standalone, unchecked checkbox or toggle.
- Items must not be grouped under a single "agree to all" option **unless** a separate granular option is also clearly and equally presented.
- The order of presentation must not be designed to nudge data subjects toward broader consent (e.g., by presenting "agree to all" first or making individual items harder to find).

### 7.4 Sample Granular Consent Presentation

*The following is an example structure for a consent form that presents three separate purposes. Replace with [Organisation Name]'s actual purposes.*

---

**[Organisation Name] — Your Privacy Choices**

Please indicate your preferences below. You may agree to some or all options. Your core service will not be affected if you decline items marked as optional.

☐ **Marketing — [Organisation Name] Products:** I agree to [Organisation Name] processing my contact information and product usage data to send me personalised offers and promotions for [Organisation Name]'s products and services by email, SMS, and/or telephone. *(Optional)*

☐ **Marketing — Partner Products:** I agree to [Organisation Name] sharing my name and contact details with selected third-party partners for the purpose of sending me offers and promotions for their products and services. *(Optional)*

☐ **Behavioural Analytics:** I agree to [Organisation Name] analysing my transaction history and platform usage behaviour to create a personalised profile, for the purpose of improving product recommendations presented to me. *(Optional)*

## 8. Prohibition on Bundled or Pre-Ticked Consent

*This section establishes clear prohibitions that are directly enforceable at the operational level. All personnel responsible for designing consent mechanisms must read and confirm compliance with this section.*

### 8.1 Prohibited Practices

The following practices are **strictly prohibited** at [Organisation Name] and shall be treated as non-compliance events subject to the escalation process in Section 14:

| # | Prohibited Practice | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Pre-ticked checkboxes** | Any consent field (digital or physical) that is selected, activated, or enabled by default before any affirmative action by the data subject. |
| 2 | **Bundled consent** | Combining multiple distinct processing purposes into a single consent item that the data subject can only agree to or refuse in its entirety. |
| 3 | **Consent buried in T&Cs** | Embedding consent to personal data processing within general terms and conditions, service agreements, or privacy policies without a separate, prominent consent mechanism. |
| 4 | **Implied consent by inaction** | Treating a data subject's failure to opt out, silence, non-response, or continued use of a service as evidence of consent. |
| 5 | **Coercive conditioning** | Making access to a core, contracted service conditional on consent to processing activities that are not strictly necessary for that service. |
| 6 | **Dark patterns** | Any user interface design that makes it more difficult for a data subject to withhold or withdraw consent than to grant it (e.g., confusing language, asymmetric visual prominence, hidden opt-out). |

### 8.2 Pre-Deployment Review Obligation

- All new consent forms, screens, or scripts — and any material modifications to existing ones — must be submitted to the DPO for a prohibition compliance review **prior to deployment**.
- The DPO must complete the review within [X] business days and document the outcome in the Consent Asset Register.
- Deployment of a consent mechanism that has not passed DPO review is prohibited.

### 8.3 Retrospective Audit

- The DPO shall conduct a review of all active consent mechanisms no less than **annually**, and at any time following a change to the PDPA or related guidelines.
- Any consent mechanism found to embody a prohibited practice must be suspended immediately, remediated, and re-approved before reactivation.
- Data subjects who were subject to a non-compliant consent collection must be identified, notified, and offered an opportunity to provide valid consent.

## 10. Physical Consent — Scan, Storage, and Linking to Data Subject Record

*Physical consent forms create an evidentiary record that must be managed as carefully as any other regulatory document. This section defines the handling lifecycle for paper-based consent.*

### 10.1 Point-of-Collection Handling

1. **Staff Verification:** The staff member facilitating the consent interaction must verify the data subject's identity before the form is completed, in accordance with [Organisation Name]'s Customer Identification Procedure.
2. **Completeness Check:** Before the data subject leaves the interaction, the staff member must check that:
   - All required fields are completed;
   - The data subject's signature and date are present;
   - No consent boxes are pre-ticked or altered after signing.
3. **Copy for Data Subject:** The data subject must be offered a signed copy of the consent form at the time of collection. If a copy is declined, this should be noted on the staff's interaction record.

### 10.2 Scanning and Digitalisation

| Step | Requirement | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Scan | The physical consent form must be scanned to a minimum resolution of [300 DPI] within [1] business day of collection. | [Branch / Operations Staff] |
| 2. File Format | Scanned files must be saved in [PDF/A or specified format]. | [Branch / Operations Staff] |
| 3. File Naming | Files must be named according to the convention: `[CustomerID]_CONSENT_[YYYYMMDD]_[FormVersion].pdf` | [Branch / Operations Staff] |
| 4. Quality Check | The scan must be legible and complete; if not, the form must be re-scanned or the data subject re-contacted. | [Supervisor / DPO] |
| 5. Upload | The scanned file must be uploaded to [Document Management System / Consent Repository] on the day of scanning. | [Branch / Operations Staff] |

### 10.3 Storage and Retention

- Physical originals must be stored in a **locked, access-controlled** location at the collecting branch or office for [30 days] following scanning, then transferred to [Central Records Storage Location] or securely destroyed per the Records Retention Schedule.
- Digital scans must be retained for a minimum of [X years following the termination of the data subject's relationship with [Organisation Name]], or such longer period as required by law or regulation.
- Access to the digital consent repository must be restricted to authorised personnel and logged.

### 10.4 Linking to Data Subject Record

- Upon upload, the consent record must be linked to the data subject's master record in [CRM / Core Banking / HR System] using the data subject's unique identifier.
- The consent record link must include: form version, date of consent, purpose codes consented to, and a flag for each purpose indicating current consent status (active / withdrawn).
- Staff must confirm the link is successfully established before closing the interaction record.

## 11. Consent for Children and Vulnerable Persons

*Collecting consent from children or vulnerable persons requires additional safeguards to ensure that consent is genuinely free, informed, and valid. This section establishes the enhanced requirements applicable to these groups.*

### 11.1 Consent Involving Children

For the purposes of this procedure, a **child** is defined as a person under the age of 18 years.

**Age Verification:**
- [Organisation Name] must not knowingly collect personal data from or about a child without first obtaining **parental or guardian consent**, except where processing is required by law.
- All digital consent forms and onboarding flows must include an age verification step. Where a user indicates they are under 18, the consent flow must:
  1. Halt the standard consent process;
  2. Redirect to the parental/guardian consent process; and
  3. Not collect personal data until parental/guardian consent is confirmed.

**Parental / Guardian Consent Requirements:**

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Identity Verification | The parent or guardian must be identified and their identity verified in accordance with [Organisation Name]'s identification procedure. |
| Relationship Verification | Documentary evidence of the parental or guardian relationship must be obtained and retained. |
| Consent Scope | Consent must be specific to the processing purposes relevant to the child's interaction with [Organisation Name]. |
| Record | The consent record must identify both the child (as data subject) and the parent/guardian (as the consenting party), including their relationship. |
| Review | Parental consent should be reviewed and re-confirmed when the child approaches the age of 18, or at a natural interaction milestone. |

### 11.2 Consent Involving Vulnerable Persons

*Vulnerable persons include, but are not limited to, individuals with cognitive impairments, mental health conditions, physical disabilities affecting communication, individuals under undue influence, or individuals in circumstances of distress.*

**Identification of Vulnerability:**
- Staff must be trained to identify indicators of vulnerability during consent interactions. Training records must be maintained by [HR / Compliance Training Manager].
- If vulnerability is suspected, staff must not proceed with a standard consent interaction and must escalate to a [Supervisor / Designated Vulnerable Customer Officer].

**Enhanced Safeguards for Vulnerable Persons:**

| Safeguard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Capacity Assessment | A reasonable assessment of the individual's capacity to provide consent must be made and documented before proceeding. |
| Appropriate Support | Where possible and appropriate, an independent support person (not an [Organisation Name] employee) should be present. |
| Adjusted Communication | Consent information must be communicated in a manner appropriate to the individual's needs (e.g., simplified language, large print, alternative format). |
| Additional Time | The data subject must be given sufficient time to consider the consent request without pressure. |
| Third-Party Consent | Where the individual lacks capacity to consent, consent must be sought from a legally authorised representative (e.g., holder of a power of attorney). Documentary evidence of authority must be obtained and retained. |
| Supervisory Sign-Off | All consent records for vulnerable persons must be counter-signed by a supervisor before the processing commences. |

## 12. Consent Withdrawal Process and Downstream Notification

*Every data subject has the right to withdraw consent at any time. This section establishes the process for receiving, processing, and giving effect to consent withdrawal requests, including the obligations to notify downstream systems and third parties.*

### 12.1 Channels for Consent Withdrawal

[Organisation Name] must provide data subjects with **accessible and clear** means to withdraw consent through any of the following channels:

| Channel | Mechanism | Response Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Digital — Customer Portal | Consent management dashboard / toggle off | Immediate / [X] business days to downstream propagation |
| Digital — Email | Written request to [DPO email address] | Acknowledged within [2] business days; effected within [15] business days |
| Telephone | Request via [Customer Service / DPO hotline] | Acknowledged immediately; effected within [15] business days |
| Written — Physical | Letter or form submitted to [Address] | Acknowledged within [5] business days; effected within [15] business days |
| In-Person — Branch | Written withdrawal form completed at branch | Acknowledged immediately; effected within [15] business days |

### 12.2 Consent Withdrawal Processing Steps

**Step 1 — Receipt and Authentication:**
- Upon receiving a withdrawal request, the receiving staff member or system must verify the identity of the data subject before processing.
- The request must be logged in [CRM / Consent Management System] with: date received, channel, data subject identifier, and purposes specified for withdrawal (or "all" if global withdrawal).

**Step 2 — Acknowledgement:**
- A written acknowledgement must be issued to the data subject within the applicable timeframe above, confirming receipt of the withdrawal request and the expected timeframe for effect.

**Step 3 — Update Consent Records:**
- The data subject's consent record must be updated to reflect the withdrawal: set the withdrawal flag to TRUE, record the withdrawal timestamp, and identify the purposes withdrawn.
- The original consent record must **not** be deleted; it must be retained as a historical record for audit and dispute resolution purposes.

**Step 4 — Cessation of Processing:**
- Processing of the data subject's personal data for the withdrawn purpose(s) must cease within the applicable timeframe.
- Where processing has already commenced and cannot be reversed (e.g., data already shared), this must be documented and, where required, disclosed to the data subject.

**Step 5 — Downstream Notification:**

| Downstream Party | Notification Required? | Notification Method | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| Internal systems (CRM, marketing platform, analytics) | Yes | Automated flag / manual update | Within [X] business hours |
| Third-party data processors | Yes | Formal written notification | Within [X] business days |
| Third-party data controllers (where data was shared) | Yes (reasonable efforts) | Formal written notification | Within [X] business days |
| Regulators | Only if required by law or regulation | [Method] | Per regulatory requirement |

**Step 6 — Closure and Confirmation:**
- Following completion of Steps 3–5, a closure confirmation must be issued to the data subject, confirming that their withdrawal has been effected.
- The withdrawal request must be marked as closed in [CRM / Consent Management System] with the date of closure.

### 12.3 Consequences of Withdrawal and Data Subject Communication

- Data subjects must be informed **before** withdrawing consent of the potential consequences (e.g., inability to receive certain services or communications), so they can make an informed decision.
- Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing conducted prior to withdrawal.
- Where withdrawal of consent triggers the cessation of a service, this must be handled in accordance with the applicable service agreement and data retention obligations.

## 14. Non-Compliance and Escalation

*This section establishes the response framework for situations where consent has been collected in a manner that does not comply with this procedure or the PDPA.*

### 14.1 Examples of Non-Compliance Events

- Deployment of a consent form or script that was not approved by the DPO;
- Use of pre-ticked checkboxes or bundled consent (see Section 8);
- Failure to capture and store the minimum technical consent record (see Section 9);
- Collection of consent from a child without parental/guardian consent;
- Processing personal data after consent has been withdrawn;
- Failure to notify downstream parties of a consent withdrawal within the required timeframe.

### 14.2 Escalation Process

| Step | Action | Responsible | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Incident identified and logged in [Incident Register] | Identifying staff member | Immediately |
| 2 | Notify line manager and DPO | Identifying staff member | Within [4] hours |
| 3 | DPO assesses severity and regulatory notification obligation | DPO | Within [1] business day |
| 4 | Remediation plan developed and approved | DPO + Compliance | Within [3] business days |
| 5 | Remediation executed and evidence retained | Business Unit PIC + IT | Per plan |
| 6 | Affected data subjects notified (if required) | DPO | Per regulatory obligation |
| 7 | Post-incident review and procedure update (if required) | DPO + Compliance | Within [30] days of closure |
