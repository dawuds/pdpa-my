## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This procedure establishes the formal process by which [Organization Name] handles requests from data subjects to withdraw their consent for the processing of personal data, in accordance with **Section 38** of the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**. It ensures that withdrawal of consent is processed in a manner that is lawful, timely, fair, and consistent with the rights conferred upon data subjects under Malaysian law.

This procedure provides operational guidance to all personnel responsible for receiving, processing, and responding to consent withdrawal requests, and ensures that downstream systems, processors, and third-party recipients are appropriately notified.

### 1.2 Scope

This procedure applies to:

- All personal data collected and processed by [Organization Name] under a consent lawful basis, as defined in **PDPA s6**.
- All departments, business units, subsidiaries, and third-party data processors acting on behalf of [Organization Name].
- All consent withdrawal requests received through any channel, whether written, electronic, verbal, or through a self-service portal.
- All classes of personal data, including but not limited to: customer data, employee data, marketing contact data, and data held in third-party systems under data processing agreements.

### 1.3 Out of Scope

This procedure does **not** apply to:

- Requests to access, correct, or delete personal data, which are governed by separate procedures under PDPA s30 and s34.
- Processing activities that are not founded on consent as the lawful basis (e.g., processing necessary for the performance of a contract under s6(1)(b), or compliance with a legal obligation under s6(1)(c)).
- Anonymous or pseudonymous data that cannot be re-identified.

## 2. Definitions

*This section defines key terms used throughout this procedure to ensure consistent interpretation across all departments.*

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Consent** | Any freely given, specific, and informed indication of a data subject's agreement to the processing of their personal data, as contemplated under PDPA s6(1)(a). |
| **Withdrawal of Consent** | A data subject's revocation of previously granted consent, submitted through an approved intake channel, as provided under PDPA s38. |
| **Data Subject** | An individual who is the subject of personal data processed by [Organization Name]. |
| **Data User** | [Organization Name], in its capacity as the entity that processes personal data and determines the purposes and means of such processing. |
| **Data Processor** | Any person or organization that processes personal data on behalf of [Organization Name] under a data processing agreement. |
| **Third-Party Recipient** | Any external party to whom personal data has been disclosed by [Organization Name] other than as a Data Processor. |
| **Suppression List** | A centrally maintained register of data subjects who have withdrawn consent, used to prevent further processing of their personal data for specified purposes. |
| **Statutory Period** | The period within which cessation of processing must be completed following a valid consent withdrawal request, as required by applicable regulation or as committed to in [Organization Name]'s Privacy Notice. |
| **Alternative Lawful Basis** | A ground for processing personal data under PDPA s6(1) other than consent, which may permit continued processing notwithstanding withdrawal. |
| **DPO** | Data Protection Officer — the designated officer responsible for overseeing compliance with PDPA within [Organization Name]. |

### 3.2 Supporting Regulatory Instruments and Guidelines

### 3.3 Key Obligations Arising from this Framework

1. Withdrawal of consent must be made **as easy as the giving of consent** (PDPA s38(1)).
2. Upon receipt of a valid withdrawal notice, [Organization Name] must **cease processing** the personal data to the extent the processing was based solely on consent.
3. Processing activities that rely on **alternative lawful bases** may continue subject to the assessment set out in Section 6.
4. Data subjects must be **notified** of the outcome and any continuing processing within a defined response period.

## 4. Withdrawal Request Intake Channels

*This section describes the channels through which data subjects may submit consent withdrawal requests. PDPA s38 requires that withdrawal must be as easy as the giving of consent — the channels listed here must be reviewed regularly to ensure this standard is met.*

### 4.1 Principle

[Organization Name] shall ensure that the mechanisms available for withdrawing consent are **at least as accessible and straightforward** as the mechanisms used when consent was originally obtained. No data subject shall be required to navigate a more burdensome process to withdraw consent than to give it.

### 4.2 Approved Intake Channels

| Channel | Access Point | Operating Hours | Responsible Team | Target Acknowledgement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Online Self-Service Portal** | [URL of privacy portal] | 24/7 | IT / Data Management | Automated — immediate |
| **Email Request** | [privacy@organizationname.com.my] | Business hours | DPO Office | 1 business day |
| **Written Letter** | [Organization Name], Attn: Data Protection Officer, [Postal Address] | Business hours | DPO Office / Records | 2 business days |
| **In-Branch Request** | Any [Organization Name] branch | Branch operating hours | Branch Operations | Same day (form issuance) |
| **Contact Centre** | [Telephone number] | [Operating hours] | Contact Centre | Same call (verbally acknowledged) |

### 4.3 Request Form

All non-self-service channels shall direct the data subject to complete or submit **Form CW-01: Consent Withdrawal Request Form** (see Appendix A). The form shall capture:

- Full name of the data subject
- Identity verification details (see Section 5)
- Specific consent(s) being withdrawn (with reference to purpose category)
- Preferred method of confirmation
- Date of submission
- Data subject signature (or electronic equivalent)

### 4.4 Verbal Requests

Where a data subject submits a verbal withdrawal request via the contact centre or in-branch:

1. The receiving officer shall complete Form CW-01 on behalf of the data subject and read it back for confirmation.
2. The interaction shall be recorded in the case management system with a unique reference number.
3. A written confirmation of the verbal request shall be dispatched to the data subject's registered contact address within **[X] business days**.

## 5. Identity Verification Requirements

*This section establishes the minimum identity verification standards to be applied before a consent withdrawal request is processed. Verification protects both the data subject (from unauthorized withdrawals) and the organization (from processing invalid requests).*

### 5.1 Verification Obligation

[Organization Name] shall verify the identity of the requesting individual before actioning any consent withdrawal request. Verification must be proportionate to the sensitivity of the personal data concerned and the potential impact of the withdrawal.

### 5.2 Verification Methods

| Channel | Standard Verification Method | Enhanced Verification (Sensitive Data) |
|---|---|---|
| Online Self-Service Portal | Authenticated login (username + MFA) | Step-up authentication (biometric / OTP) |
| Email | Confirmation from registered email address + last 4 digits of NRIC | Scanned copy of NRIC + callback to registered number |
| Written Letter | Photocopy of NRIC (front and back) + signature | Notarised copy of NRIC |
| In-Branch | Presentation of original NRIC / passport | Original NRIC + secondary document |
| Contact Centre | Knowledge-based authentication (KBA) — [X] security questions | KBA + OTP to registered mobile |
| Mobile Application | Authenticated session (biometric login) | Step-up OTP |

### 5.3 Verification of Third-Party Representatives

Where a request is submitted by an authorized representative (e.g., legal guardian, solicitor holding power of attorney):

- Proof of authority must be provided (e.g., letter of authorization, power of attorney, court order).
- The representative's identity must be separately verified.
- Documentation must be retained on the case file.

### 5.4 Failed Verification

If identity cannot be verified:

1. The request shall be placed in a **pending** status.
2. The data subject shall be notified within **[X] business days** of the information required to complete verification.
3. If verification is not completed within **[X] calendar days**, the request shall be closed and the data subject notified.
4. A closed request due to failed verification shall **not** be treated as a valid withdrawal and processing shall continue.
5. All failed verification attempts shall be logged in the case management system.

## 6. Assessment of Alternative Lawful Bases for Continued Processing

*Upon receipt of a valid consent withdrawal, the organization must assess whether any processing previously grounded in consent can lawfully continue under an alternative basis under PDPA s6(1). This section describes the assessment framework.*

### 6.1 Assessment Trigger

Upon verification of a consent withdrawal request, the **DPO Office** shall initiate a **Lawful Basis Assessment** for each processing activity affected by the withdrawal.

### 6.2 Assessment Process

1. Retrieve the **Data Processing Register** entry for the relevant data subject and identify all processing activities linked to the withdrawn consent.
2. For each processing activity, evaluate whether an alternative lawful basis under **PDPA s6(1)** applies:

| Alternative Lawful Basis | PDPA Reference | Applicability Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| Necessary for performance of a contract to which the data subject is a party | s6(1)(b) | [Describe when this applies within [Organization Name]'s context] |
| Necessary for compliance with a legal obligation | s6(1)(c) | [Describe applicable legal or regulatory obligations] |
| Necessary to protect vital interests of the data subject | s6(1)(d) | [Describe circumstances, e.g., fraud prevention] |
| Necessary for administration of justice or exercise of statutory functions | s6(1)(e) | [Describe applicable scenarios] |
| Necessary for legitimate interests of the data user or third party | s6(1)(f) | [Describe and document the legitimate interest assessment] |

3. Document the assessment outcome in the **Consent Withdrawal Case Record** (Form CW-02, Appendix B).
4. Where an alternative lawful basis is identified, update the **Data Processing Register** to reflect the new lawful basis.

### 6.3 Communication of Alternative Basis

If processing is to continue under an alternative lawful basis:

- The data subject must be **notified** of the alternative basis in the withdrawal confirmation (see Section 10).
- The notification must clearly explain **which processing activities** will continue, **on what basis**, and the data subject's rights in respect of that continued processing.
- This notification shall not be used as a mechanism to coerce the data subject into re-consenting.

### 6.4 Documentation Retention

Assessment records shall be retained for a minimum of **[X] years** from the date of the withdrawal request, in accordance with [Organization Name]'s Records Retention Schedule.

## 8. Suppression List Update and System Propagation

*The suppression list is a critical operational control that prevents inadvertent reprocessing of data from subjects who have withdrawn consent. This section describes the maintenance and propagation requirements.*

### 8.1 Suppression List Overview

[Organization Name] maintains a **centralized Suppression List** (also referred to as the "Do Not Process Register") that records all data subjects who have withdrawn consent, specifying the scope of the withdrawal and effective date.

The Suppression List is the **authoritative reference** for consent status and must take precedence over any other record in any downstream system.

### 8.2 Suppression List Data Fields

The Suppression List shall capture, at minimum, the following fields:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Data Subject ID | Unique identifier within [Organization Name]'s systems |
| Full Name | As held on record |
| NRIC / Passport Number (masked) | e.g., XXXXXX-XX-[last 4 digits] |

| Alternative Basis Identified | Yes / No — with reference to applicable basis |
| Processing Activities Continuing | List of activities continuing under alternative basis |
| Expiry / Review Date | Where suppression is time-limited or subject to review |
| Last Updated By | Name and role of officer making the entry |

### 8.3 Propagation to Systems

## 9. Notification to Data Processors and Third-Party Recipients

*PDPA imposes obligations on data users to manage onward disclosures responsibly. This section governs how [Organization Name] notifies entities to whom it has shared the data subject's personal data of the consent withdrawal.*

### 9.1 Notification Obligation

Upon processing a valid consent withdrawal, [Organization Name] shall notify all **data processors** and **third-party recipients** who have received the data subject's personal data, where:

- The data was originally shared on the basis of the withdrawn consent; **and**
- The data processor or third-party recipient has no independent lawful basis to continue processing.

### 9.2 Identification of Recipients

The DPO Office shall, upon receipt of a verified withdrawal request, consult the **Data Sharing Register** to identify all parties to whom the relevant data subject's personal data has been disclosed.

| Recipient Type | Register Source | Responsible for Identification |
|---|---|---|
| Data Processors (contracted) | Data Processing Agreement Register | DPO Office / Legal |
| Third-Party Recipients (direct sharing) | Data Sharing Register | DPO Office |
| Sub-Processors (engaged by primary processors) | Data Processing Agreements (sub-processing clauses) | Primary Data Processor (contractual obligation) |

### 9.3 Notification Content

Notifications to data processors and third-party recipients shall include:

- Reference to the consent withdrawal request (case reference number)
- Identity of the data subject (sufficient to locate the record)
- The specific data or data categories affected
- The effective date of withdrawal
- Instruction to cease processing under the withdrawn consent basis
- Instruction to update their own suppression records
- Confirmation request with a specified response deadline
- Reminder of contractual obligations under the applicable data processing agreement

### 9.4 Notification Tracking

### 9.5 Non-Responsive Recipients

If a data processor or third-party recipient fails to confirm cessation within **[X] business days**:

1. A follow-up notification shall be issued by the DPO Office.

## 10. Data Subject Confirmation of Withdrawal

*The data subject must receive timely and clear confirmation that their withdrawal request has been processed. This section sets out the content and timeline for confirmation communications.*

### 10.1 Acknowledgement of Request

Upon receipt of a consent withdrawal request (and prior to completion of processing):

- [Organization Name] shall dispatch an **Acknowledgement Notice** to the data subject within **[X] business day(s)**.
- The acknowledgement shall include:
  - A unique case reference number
  - Confirmation that the request has been received and is being processed
  - The expected timeline for completion
  - Contact details for follow-up queries

### 10.2 Withdrawal Completion Confirmation

Upon completion of all cessation and suppression actions, [Organization Name] shall issue a **Withdrawal Completion Notice** to the data subject. This notice shall include:

- Confirmation that the withdrawal of consent has been actioned
- The effective date from which processing has ceased
- A summary of the **processing purposes** for which consent has been withdrawn
- Details of any **processing activities that will continue** under an alternative lawful basis (see Section 6.3), including:
  - The nature of the continuing processing
  - The lawful basis relied upon
  - The data subject's rights in respect of that continuing processing (e.g., right to object under PDPA s43)
- Details of **data retention** — clarification that personal data may be retained for the period required by law or contract, but will not be processed for the withdrawn purposes
- Contact details of the DPO for further queries
- Reference to [Organization Name]'s Privacy Notice

### 10.3 Confirmation Templates

*Reference templates for acknowledgement and completion notices are provided at Appendix C. All communications must be reviewed by the DPO Office before dispatch and must be maintained in a plain, accessible language register.*

### 10.4 Record of Confirmation

A copy of all communications dispatched to the data subject shall be retained in the **Consent Withdrawal Case Record** and linked to the data subject's master record in the case management system.

## 11. Exceptions Where Processing May Continue

*This section documents the specific circumstances under which [Organization Name] may lawfully continue to process personal data notwithstanding the withdrawal of consent. These exceptions are grounded in PDPA s6 and must be applied narrowly and documented carefully.*

### 11.1 Overview of Exceptions

Withdrawal of consent does not automatically require [Organization Name] to cease all processing. Processing may lawfully continue where:

1. The processing is necessary to comply with a **legal or regulatory obligation** applicable to [Organization Name].
2. The processing is necessary for the **performance of a contract** to which the data subject is a party, or to take steps at the request of the data subject prior to entering into a contract.
3. The processing is necessary to **protect the vital interests** of the data subject.
4. The processing is necessary for the **administration of justice** or for the exercise of functions conferred by a written law.
5. The processing is necessary for **legitimate interests** pursued by [Organization Name] or a third party, which are not overridden by the interests of the data subject.

### 11.2 Application of Exceptions Within [Organization Name]

*Authors should complete this table by mapping specific business processing activities to the applicable exception.*

| Processing Activity | Applicable Exception | Regulatory / Contractual Reference | Review Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| Retention of transaction records | Legal obligation — PDPA s6(1)(c) | [e.g., Anti-Money Laundering Act 2001, s22 — 6-year retention] | Annual |
| Servicing of existing loan / product agreements | Contract — PDPA s6(1)(b) | Terms and Conditions of [Product Name] | Upon contract expiry |
| Fraud investigation and prevention | Legitimate interests — PDPA s6(1)(f) | [Organization Name] Fraud Risk Management Policy | Annual |
| Regulatory reporting to BNM | Legal obligation — PDPA s6(1)(c) | [BNM Policy Document reference] | Upon regulatory change |
| [Additional processing activity] | [Applicable exception] | [Reference] | [Frequency] |

### 11.3 Safeguards When Relying on Exceptions

When continuing to process personal data under an exception following a consent withdrawal:

- The reliance on an alternative lawful basis must be **documented** in the Consent Withdrawal Case Record.
- The data subject must be **informed** (see Section 10.2) of the continuing processing and its basis.
- Processing under the exception shall be **limited to the minimum necessary** for the stated purpose.
- The exception shall be **reviewed periodically** to confirm it remains applicable.
- Data retained under an exception shall be **clearly flagged** in the relevant system to prevent inadvertent use for withdrawn purposes.

### 11.4 Limits of Exceptions

- Exceptions **may not be invoked** to continue processing for marketing, profiling, or non-essential purposes following a consent withdrawal.
- Reliance on the legitimate interests basis must be supported by a documented **Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA)** that weighs [Organization Name]'s interests against the data subject's rights.
- Any decision to rely on an exception following a withdrawal must be reviewed and approved by the **DPO**.
