## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This procedure establishes the process by which **[Organization Name]** handles requests from data subjects to correct inaccurate, incomplete, misleading, or not up-to-date personal data held by the organization, in accordance with the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**, specifically Sections 34, 35, and 36.

The procedure ensures that:

- Correction requests are received, acknowledged, and processed in a consistent, auditable, and timely manner;
- The identity of requesting data subjects is verified before any amendment is made;
- Corrections are applied systematically across all relevant data systems and processing environments;
- Third parties who received inaccurate data are notified where required; and
- Data subjects receive a formal response within the statutory timeframe prescribed under the PDPA.

### 1.2 Scope

This procedure applies to:

- **All personal data** processed by [Organization Name] in the course of its commercial transactions, as defined under Section 4 of the PDPA;
- **All data subjects** who have submitted, or intend to submit, a correction request in relation to personal data held about them;
- **All employees, contractors, and third-party processors** who receive, process, or respond to data correction requests on behalf of [Organization Name];
- **All data systems**, databases, records repositories, and third-party platforms in which personal data of data subjects is stored or processed.

### 1.3 Out of Scope

*Describe any categories of personal data or processing activities explicitly excluded from this procedure, such as data processed exclusively for domestic purposes or data held by regulatory authorities.*

- Personal data held solely for law enforcement or regulatory compliance purposes where exemptions apply under Section 45 of the PDPA;
- [Any additional exclusions specific to [Organization Name]].

## 2. Definitions

*Define all key terms used throughout this document. Definitions should align with the PDPA 2010 and any supplementary guidelines issued by the Personal Data Protection Commissioner.*

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Data Subject** | An individual who is the subject of personal data processed by [Organization Name], as defined under the PDPA 2010. |
| **Data Controller** | [Organization Name], being the entity that processes personal data for its own commercial transactions. |
| **Personal Data** | Any information in respect of commercial transactions which is being processed wholly or partly by means of equipment operating automatically, or recorded with the intention that it should be processed, that relates directly or indirectly to a data subject who is identified or identifiable. |
| **Correction Request** | A formal request submitted by a data subject or their authorised representative under Section 34 of the PDPA to correct, complete, or update their personal data held by the data controller. |
| **Data Correction Officer (DCO)** | The designated employee within [Organization Name] responsible for receiving, processing, and responding to data correction requests. |
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | The individual appointed by [Organization Name] with overall accountability for PDPA compliance, including oversight of data subject rights processes. |
| **Statutory Period** | The period within which [Organization Name] must respond to a correction request, as prescribed by the PDPA or any regulations made thereunder. |
| **Third Party** | Any external organisation, vendor, partner, or regulatory body to whom [Organization Name] has disclosed a data subject's personal data that is the subject of a correction request. |
| **Supporting Evidence** | Documentation or proof provided by a data subject to substantiate their correction request. |
| **Correction Register** | The centralised log maintained by [Organization Name] to record all incoming correction requests, their status, and outcomes. |

### 4.2 Role Descriptions

**Data Protection Officer (DPO)**
- Holds overall accountability for compliance with the PDPA and the proper execution of this procedure.
- Provides guidance on complex or disputed correction requests.
- Escalates material non-compliance to senior management.
- Reviews and approves this procedure annually.

**Data Correction Officer (DCO)**
- Acts as the primary point of contact for incoming correction requests.
- Manages the end-to-end processing of each request in accordance with this procedure.
- Maintains the Correction Register.

**Business / System Owner**
- Implements approved corrections within their respective data systems or business records.
- Confirms completion of amendments to the DCO.

**Legal / Compliance**
- Advises on legal grounds for refusal or dispute resolution.
- Reviews responses where a request is denied.

**IT / Data Management**
- Executes technical amendments to data systems as directed by the Business/System Owner.
- Confirms integrity of data after amendment.

## 6. Identity Verification of the Requesting Data Subject

### 6.1 Purpose of Verification

*Identity verification is a mandatory step before any correction is made to a data subject's personal data. This protects against fraudulent or unauthorised amendments to records.*

[Organization Name] must verify the identity of the requesting data subject before processing any correction. This step ensures that:

- The request is made by the data subject themselves or an authorised representative; and
- Personal data is not amended based on a fraudulent or mistaken request.

### 6.2 Verification Standards

The level of verification required should be proportionate to the sensitivity of the personal data involved.

| Data Sensitivity | Verification Method Required |
|---|---|
| **Standard personal data** (e.g., address, contact number) | Copy of government-issued photo ID (MyKad / Passport) |
| **Financial or account data** (e.g., account number, income) | Photo ID plus one secondary document (e.g., bank statement, utility bill) |
| **Sensitive personal data** (e.g., health data, biometrics) | Photo ID plus in-person or video verification, or notarised documentation |
| **Requests by authorised representative** | Data subject's photo ID plus written letter of authorisation plus representative's photo ID |

### 6.3 Verification Process

1. The DCO compares the identity documents submitted against the data subject's records held by [Organization Name];
2. Where identity cannot be confirmed through document review alone, the DCO may contact the data subject directly via a pre-registered communication channel to confirm the request;
3. If identity verification fails, the correction request is suspended. The DCO notifies the data subject in writing, stating the reason for suspension and the steps required to proceed;
4. A maximum of **[X] attempts** are permitted before the request is formally rejected on grounds of failed verification.

### 6.4 Documentation of Verification

The outcome of identity verification must be recorded in the Correction Register, including:

- Method of verification used;
- Date verification was completed;
- Name and role of the DCO who conducted the verification; and
- Outcome (Verified / Failed / Suspended).

Identity documents obtained for verification purposes must be handled in accordance with [Organization Name]'s Data Retention and Disposal Policy and must not be retained longer than necessary.

## 7. Assessment of Accuracy and Evidentiary Requirements

### 7.1 Objective

*Once identity is verified, the DCO must assess whether the personal data in question is in fact inaccurate, incomplete, misleading, or not up-to-date, and whether the evidence submitted supports the requested correction.*

The assessment phase determines whether:

- The existing personal data held by [Organization Name] is accurate; or
- The data subject's correction request is substantiated by sufficient evidence.

### 7.2 Evidentiary Standard

[Organization Name] requires that correction requests be supported by credible documentary evidence where the requested correction is not self-evident. The following table provides guidance on acceptable evidence by data category:

| Data Category | Example of Inaccuracy | Acceptable Supporting Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Name | Misspelt name | MyKad, birth certificate, deed poll |
| Date of birth | Incorrect date | MyKad, birth certificate, passport |
| Address | Outdated address | Utility bill, bank statement, MyKad (if updated) |
| Marital status | Incorrect status | Marriage certificate, divorce decree |
| Employment information | Outdated employer | Employment letter, payslip |
| Financial data | Incorrect income or balance | Official pay slip, bank statement |
| Health data | Incorrect diagnosis or medication | Written confirmation from registered medical practitioner |
| Contact information | Incorrect phone / email | [No documentary evidence typically required; verbal confirmation acceptable] |

### 7.3 Assessment Steps

1. The DCO reviews the personal data as currently held in [Organization Name]'s systems;
2. The DCO evaluates the supporting evidence submitted against the minimum evidentiary standard in Section 7.2;
3. Where necessary, the DCO may consult the relevant Business/System Owner or subject matter expert to confirm factual accuracy;
4. The DCO documents their assessment findings in the Correction Register;
5. The DCO makes one of the following determinations:

| Determination | Criteria | Next Step |
|---|---|---|
| **Correction Approved** | Evidence is sufficient; data is confirmed inaccurate | Proceed to Section 8 |
| **Additional Evidence Required** | Evidence is insufficient or ambiguous | Notify data subject; allow [X] days to submit further evidence |
| **Correction Refused** | Data is accurate as held, or evidence is insufficient after follow-up | Proceed to Section 10 (Dispute Resolution) |

### 7.4 Approval Authority

| Correction Type | Approval Required From |
|---|---|
| Routine correction (non-sensitive data) | Data Correction Officer |
| Sensitive personal data correction | Data Protection Officer |
| Correction involving financial or account records | DPO and [Head of Department / Compliance] |
| Correction affecting regulatory reporting data | DPO, Legal, and [relevant regulatory function] |

## 8. Amendment Procedure Across All Relevant Systems

### 8.1 Scope of Amendment

*Upon approval of a correction request, the DCO must ensure that the amendment is applied consistently across all systems, databases, and records in which the inaccurate personal data is held.*

[Organization Name] must not selectively correct personal data in only a subset of its systems. The DCO shall coordinate with all relevant Business/System Owners to ensure that the correction is applied to every location where the inaccurate data exists.

### 8.2 System Inventory Mapping

*Prior to initiating amendments, the DCO must identify all systems that hold the data subject's personal data. A current Data Asset Inventory or Records of Processing Activities (ROPA) should be referenced for this purpose.*

The DCO shall consult the [Organization Name] Data Asset Inventory / ROPA to identify all relevant systems. A system impact assessment must be documented for each correction request:

| System / Repository | Data Category Held | Amendment Required | System Owner | Completion Date |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Core Banking System / CRM] | [Name, address, contact] | [Yes / No] | [Name] | [DD/MM/YYYY] |
| [Customer Database] | [Contact, financial profile] | [Yes / No] | [Name] | [DD/MM/YYYY] |
| [Marketing Platform] | [Contact, preferences] | [Yes / No] | [Name] | [DD/MM/YYYY] |
| [Archival Records / Physical Files] | [Relevant data categories] | [Yes / No] | [Name] | [DD/MM/YYYY] |
| [Third-party processor systems] | [Relevant data categories] | [Yes / No — see Section 9] | [Name] | [DD/MM/YYYY] |

### 8.3 Amendment Execution

1. The DCO issues a formal **Amendment Instruction** to each relevant Business/System Owner, referencing the Request Reference Number and specifying the exact data to be amended;
2. System Owners execute the amendment within **[X] business days** of receiving the Amendment Instruction;
3. Where amendments involve physical or paper records, the original record must be annotated or substituted as appropriate, with the date of correction and the authority for the change noted;
4. IT / Data Management confirms the integrity of all amended records and that no residual instances of the inaccurate data remain in active systems;
5. Each System Owner returns a signed **Amendment Completion Confirmation** to the DCO.

**Amendment Instruction Template Reference:** Appendix B — Amendment Instruction Form

**Amendment Completion Confirmation Template Reference:** Appendix C — Amendment Completion Confirmation

### 8.4 Quality Assurance

The DCO shall perform a post-amendment check by:

- Pulling a data extract or record view from a sample of amended systems to confirm the correction has been applied correctly;
- Confirming that no duplicate or shadow records containing the old inaccurate data persist in any active system; and
- Documenting the outcome of the quality assurance check in the Correction Register.

## 9. Notification to Third Parties

### 9.1 Legal Obligation

*Section 36 of the PDPA requires [Organization Name] to notify third parties to whom the inaccurate personal data was previously disclosed, upon correction of that data. This section establishes the procedure for identifying and notifying those third parties.*

Where [Organization Name] has disclosed the personal data (that is the subject of a correction) to any third party, it must notify that third party of the correction as soon as practicable after the amendment is made to its own records.

### 9.2 Identifying Third-Party Disclosures

The DCO shall review all records of prior disclosure for the specific personal data corrected, including:

- Data sharing agreements and third-party disclosure logs;
- Processing agreements with data processors;
- Records of regulatory disclosures; and
- Any other documented disclosures in the data subject's record.

**Third-Party Disclosure Register Query:** The DCO shall query [Organization Name]'s Third-Party Disclosure Register using the data subject's identity and the relevant data category to identify all recipients.

### 9.3 Third-Party Notification Process

| Step | Action | Responsible | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify all third parties who received the inaccurate data | DCO | Within [X] business days of amendment |
| 2 | Prepare formal notification letter for each third party | DCO | Within [X] business days of amendment |
| 3 | Issue notification to each third party via secure channel | DCO | Within [X] business days of amendment |
| 4 | Record confirmation of receipt from each third party | DCO | Upon receipt |
| 5 | Update Correction Register with notification outcomes | DCO | Upon completion |

**Third-Party Notification Letter Template Reference:** Appendix D — Third-Party Notification Letter

### 9.4 Third-Party Notification Content

Each notification to a third party must include:

- The Request Reference Number (for internal tracking — do not disclose data subject details beyond what is necessary);
- A description of the personal data that was corrected;
- The correct data to be substituted, where appropriate;
- A request that the third party update their own records accordingly;
- A confirmation request that the third party has actioned the notification; and
- [Organization Name]'s contact details for follow-up queries.

### 9.5 Exceptions

Third-party notification may not be required where:

- It is not reasonably practicable to identify or contact the third party;
- The third party is a regulatory authority or law enforcement body and notification would prejudice an investigation; or
- The disclosure to the third party was made more than [X] years prior and the data is unlikely to still be in use.

Any exception relied upon must be documented in the Correction Register with reasons.

## 10. Dispute Resolution

### 10.1 Grounds for Refusing a Correction

*[Organization Name] may refuse a correction request on lawful grounds. This section establishes the process for managing disputes where a data subject disagrees with a refusal.*

[Organization Name] may decline to make a correction where:

- The personal data as held is accurate, complete, and up-to-date, and the data subject has not provided sufficient evidence to the contrary;
- The correction would conflict with a legal obligation, court order, or regulatory requirement;
- The request relates to data that is an expression of opinion rather than a statement of fact; or
- [Any other lawful grounds specific to [Organization Name]'s data processing context].

### 10.2 Communication of Refusal

Where a correction request is refused, the DCO must, within the statutory response period:

1. Notify the data subject in writing of the refusal;
2. State the reasons for the refusal clearly and specifically;
3. Inform the data subject of their right to attach a statement of correction to their record under Section 35 of the PDPA; and
4. Inform the data subject of their right to complain to the **Personal Data Protection Commissioner** if they are dissatisfied with the outcome.

**Refusal Letter Template Reference:** Appendix E — Data Correction Refusal Letter

### 10.3 Statement of Correction

Where [Organization Name] refuses to make a correction, the data subject may request that a **Statement of Correction** be attached to their personal data record, in accordance with Section 35 of the PDPA.

Upon receipt of such a request:

1. The DCO shall attach the data subject's written statement to their record within **[X] business days**;
2. The statement shall be disclosed together with the personal data in question whenever that data is processed or disclosed in future;
3. Where the personal data has previously been disclosed to a third party, [Organization Name] shall, where practicable, notify the third party of the existence of the statement of correction.

### 10.4 Internal Escalation

*Data subjects who are dissatisfied with an initial refusal may escalate within [Organization Name] before lodging an external complaint.*

| Stage 3 | Final internal decision communicated to data subject | DPO / Senior Management | Within [X] business days of Stage 2 |

## 11. Response to Data Subject Within Statutory Period

### 11.1 Statutory Response Period

*The PDPA prescribes a timeframe within which [Organization Name] must respond to a correction request. [Organization Name] must comply strictly with this period to avoid regulatory exposure.*

Under the PDPA and any applicable regulations, [Organization Name] must respond to a data correction request within **[21 days / prescribed statutory period]** of receipt of a valid and complete request.

> **Note:** The statutory period commences from the date a **complete** request is received, including all required identity verification documents and supporting evidence. The DCO must record and communicate this commencement date clearly.

### 11.2 Response Timeline Overview

| Milestone | Target Timeline |
|---|---|
| Acknowledgement of receipt issued | Within [X] business days of receipt |
| Identity verification completed | Within [X] business days of acknowledgement |
| Assessment of accuracy completed | Within [X] business days of successful verification |
| Amendment executed (if approved) | Within [X] business days of approval |
| Third-party notifications issued | Within [X] business days of amendment |
| Final response to data subject issued | Within statutory period from receipt of complete request |

### 11.3 Final Response Content

The final written response to the data subject must include:

- The Request Reference Number;
- A summary of the correction requested;
- The outcome of the request (approved / partially approved / refused);
- Where approved: confirmation that the correction has been made and the date of amendment;
- Where refused: the reasons for refusal, the right to attach a Statement of Correction, and the right to complain to the Commissioner;
- The name and contact details of the DCO handling the matter; and
- [Organization Name]'s internal complaints escalation pathway.

**Final Response Letter Templates:** See Appendix F (Approval) and Appendix E (Refusal).

### 11.4 Extension of Time

*Describe circumstances in which a request to extend the statutory response period may be made, if permitted under applicable regulations.*

Where [Organization Name] is unable to respond within the statutory period due to [prescribed circumstances], it shall:

1. Notify the data subject in writing before the expiry of the statutory period;
2. State the reasons for the extension and the expected date of response; and
3. Document the extension in the Correction Register.
