## 1. Purpose

*Describe why this procedure exists, the organizational commitment it demonstrates, and the specific regulatory obligation it fulfils.*

This procedure establishes the requirements and process for planning, delivering, recording, and evaluating data protection training across [Organization Name]. It ensures that all employees understand their obligations under the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)** and that personnel who handle personal data in the course of their duties are equipped with role-appropriate knowledge and skills to do so lawfully and responsibly.

[Organization Name] recognizes that a well-informed workforce is a critical control in minimizing the risk of personal data breaches, non-compliance, and reputational harm. This procedure operationalizes that commitment by defining minimum training standards, delivery timelines, assessment criteria, and recordkeeping obligations.

## 5. Training Needs Analysis

*This section defines how [Organization Name] determines what training is required, for whom, and at what depth, based on data access levels and job function risk.*

### 5.1 Purpose of Training Needs Analysis

[Organization Name] shall conduct a **Training Needs Analysis (TNA)** at least annually, and whenever significant changes occur to legislation, organizational structure, data processing activities, or incident patterns. The TNA ensures training programmes remain targeted, relevant, and proportionate.

### 5.2 TNA Methodology

The DPO, in collaboration with Human Resources, shall conduct the TNA using the following inputs:

- Current roles and job descriptions across all business units
- Data flow mapping and Records of Processing Activities (RoPA)
- Findings from the annual Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) programme
- Data incident and near-miss reports from the preceding year
- Results of the prior year's training assessments and completion statistics
- Regulatory updates issued by the Personal Data Protection Commissioner
- Staff feedback from training evaluations

### 5.3 Role and Data Access Classification

*Classify all roles according to the nature of personal data they handle. This classification drives the type and depth of training required.*

All roles shall be classified into one of the following tiers:

| Tier | Classification | Description | Typical Roles |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | General Awareness | Minimal or incidental exposure to personal data; no primary data processing responsibilities | Finance (non-customer-facing), Facilities, Engineering Support |
| **Tier 2** | Standard Handler | Regular interaction with personal data in structured systems as part of core duties | Customer Service, Sales, Operations, Administration |
| **Tier 3** | Elevated Handler | Significant personal data processing responsibilities; access to sensitive personal data or bulk datasets | HR, IT, Marketing (data analytics), Legal, Compliance |
| **Tier 4** | Specialist / Controller | Accountable for designing or overseeing data processing activities; risk assessment and policy responsibilities | DPO, CISO, Head of HR, Legal Counsel, Senior Management |

### 5.4 TNA Output

The TNA shall produce a documented **Training Plan** (see Appendix A) that specifies for each role tier:

- Required training modules and content
- Delivery method
- Frequency
- Target completion timelines
- Assessment requirements

The Training Plan shall be approved by the DPO and Head of Human Resources before implementation.

## 6. Mandatory Induction Training for New Employees

*This section defines the data protection training requirements that must be completed by all new employees as part of the onboarding process, within 30 days of commencement.*

### 6.1 Requirement

All new employees, regardless of role tier, **must** complete foundational data protection induction training within **30 calendar days** of their employment commencement date. This is a mandatory condition of employment with [Organization Name].

### 6.2 Induction Training Content

The induction training module shall cover, at minimum:

| Module | Topics Covered |
|---|---|
| **1. Introduction to PDPA** | Overview of the PDPA 2010, data protection principles, role of the Commissioner, penalties for non-compliance |
| **2. What is Personal Data?** | Categories of personal data, sensitive personal data, identification of personal data in the employee's work context |
| **3. Employee Obligations** | Lawful bases for processing, need-to-know principle, clean desk and screen locking, incident reporting obligations |
| **4. Data Subject Rights** | Rights of access, correction, and to prevent processing; how to handle data subject requests and escalation path |
| **5. Incident Reporting** | What constitutes a personal data incident, how to report it, to whom, and within what timeframe |
| **6. Organizational Policies** | [Organization Name] Data Protection Policy, Acceptable Use Policy, and where to find further guidance |

### 6.3 Delivery and Scheduling

- Human Resources shall enroll all new employees into the induction training module on or before their first working day.
- The preferred delivery method for induction training is **e-learning** to ensure consistent delivery and automatic tracking.
- Where an employee commences employment without system access to the e-learning platform, Human Resources shall arrange an in-person or facilitated session within the 30-day window.

### 6.4 Tracking and Escalation

- Human Resources shall monitor completion status weekly during the 30-day window.
- At **Day 21**, Human Resources shall send a reminder to employees who have not yet completed induction training and copy their line manager.
- At **Day 30**, any employee who has not completed induction training shall be escalated to the DPO and their line manager for immediate follow-up.
- Non-completion beyond **Day 45** without documented exceptional circumstance shall be referred to the Head of Human Resources for disciplinary consideration.

### 6.5 Assessment

New employees must pass the induction module assessment with a minimum score of **[X]%** (see Section 12 for pass mark and remediation procedures).

## 7. Annual Refresher Training Requirements

*This section defines the annual mandatory refresher training cycle applicable to all in-scope personnel, including frequency, content update requirements, and completion deadlines.*

### 7.1 Requirement

All in-scope personnel must complete an annual data protection refresher training module within each **calendar year**. The refresher training cycle shall run from **[1 January to 31 December]** of each year, with a target completion deadline of **[30 September]** to allow time for remediation before year-end.

### 7.2 Annual Refresh of Content

The DPO shall review and update the refresher training content no later than **[31 January]** each year to incorporate:

- Legislative or regulatory amendments published in the preceding year
- Lessons learned from internal personal data incidents and near-misses
- Emerging threats and data protection risks relevant to [Organization Name]'s processing activities
- Feedback received from the prior year's training evaluations
- Guidance or advisories issued by the Personal Data Protection Commissioner

| Component | Description | Duration (Approximate) |

## 9. Training Content Standards

*This section defines the minimum quality and coverage standards that all data protection training materials must meet, regardless of delivery method.*

### 9.1 Content Alignment with PDPA Principles

All training content must be aligned with the **seven data protection principles** prescribed under the PDPA:

| Principle | PDPA Reference | Minimum Content Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| General Principle | Section 5 | Lawful bases for processing; consent; legitimate purposes |
| Notice and Choice Principle | Section 7 | Requirement to notify data subjects; right to opt out |
| Disclosure Principle | Section 8 | Restrictions on disclosing personal data to third parties |
| Security Principle | Section 9 | Practical security measures; incident recognition and reporting |
| Retention Principle | Section 10 | Retention periods; secure disposal of personal data |
| Data Integrity Principle | Section 11 | Keeping personal data accurate, complete, and up to date |
| Access Principle | Section 12 | Responding to access and correction requests from data subjects |

### 9.2 Mandatory Content Elements

All mandatory training modules must include the following elements:

- [ ] Overview of the PDPA and its application to [Organization Name]
- [ ] Definition of personal data and sensitive personal data with practical examples from [Organization Name]'s context
- [ ] Employees' individual obligations and potential consequences of non-compliance (including personal liability)
- [ ] [Organization Name]'s Data Protection Policy and key supporting procedures
- [ ] Incident recognition, escalation, and reporting process
- [ ] Data subject rights and how to direct requests to the DPO
- [ ] Knowledge assessment questions

### 9.3 Content Development and Review Standards

- Training materials shall be developed or reviewed by the DPO in conjunction with Human Resources.
- Subject matter expert review is required for role-specific modules (e.g., IT training reviewed by CISO; HR training reviewed by Head of HR).
- All materials must be reviewed and approved before deployment; the DPO holds final approval authority.
- Materials must be reviewed at least **annually** or following any significant regulatory, operational, or risk change.
- Version control shall be maintained for all training materials, with version numbers aligned to this procedure's document management standards.

### 9.4 Plain Language Requirement

Training content must be written in **plain language** accessible to the target audience. Legal or technical jargon must be explained in plain terms. Where Bahasa Malaysia or other languages are required to reach specific employee groups, translated versions shall be made available.

## 10. Training Delivery Methods

*This section describes the approved methods for delivering data protection training and the circumstances under which each is appropriate.*

### 10.1 Approved Delivery Methods

[Organization Name] approves the following training delivery methods. Selection of delivery method must be documented in the Training Plan and must be appropriate for the audience, content complexity, and assessment requirements.

| Method | Description | Best Suited For | Assessment Capability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **E-Learning (Self-Paced)** | Online modules hosted on [Organization Name]'s Learning Management System (LMS); employees complete at their own pace within a defined window | Mandatory induction and refresher training; geographically dispersed staff; consistent baseline delivery | Built-in automated assessment and completion tracking |
| **Instructor-Led Classroom Training** | Facilitated face-to-face sessions led by the DPO or qualified trainer; enables Q&A and discussion | Role-specific deep-dive sessions; workshops; smaller groups requiring interaction | In-session quiz or written assessment; attendance register |

| **Blended Learning** | Combination of e-learning pre-reading followed by in-person application workshop | Advanced or role-specific modules requiring both knowledge and practical application | Combined automated pre-test and facilitator-assessed practical component |

## 12. Assessment, Pass Mark, and Remediation

*This section defines the minimum assessment requirements for each training category, the pass mark standard, and the process for managing employees who fail assessments or do not complete training within the required timeframe.*

### 12.1 Assessment Requirements

All mandatory data protection training modules must include a formal knowledge assessment. The assessment must:

- Contain a minimum of **[X] questions** per module
- Include a mix of question types: multiple choice, true/false, and scenario-based questions
- Be randomized from a question bank where the LMS capability allows, to prevent answer sharing
- Be completed individually by each employee without assistance

### 12.2 Pass Mark Standards

| Training Category | Minimum Pass Mark | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Mandatory Induction Training | **[X]%** (recommended: 80%) | All new employees must demonstrate foundational competency before handling personal data |
| Annual Refresher Training | **[X]%** (recommended: 75%) | Employees should demonstrate sustained awareness of PDPA obligations |
| Role-Specific Training (Tier 3) | **[X]%** (recommended: 80%) | Elevated handling roles require higher competency standard |
| Role-Specific Training (Tier 4 / DPO) | **[X]%** (recommended: 85%) | Specialist roles must demonstrate expert-level knowledge |

### 12.3 Failed Assessment — Remediation Process

Where an employee fails to achieve the required pass mark, the following remediation process applies:

**Step 1 — Immediate Notification**
- The LMS shall automatically notify the employee and their line manager of the failed assessment result on the day of failure.
- Human Resources shall be notified by automated LMS report or manual escalation within **[1] working day**.

**Step 2 — Remediation Period**
- The employee shall be granted a remediation period of **[X] calendar days** from the date of failure to re-complete the training module and re-sit the assessment.
- The line manager is responsible for ensuring the employee has sufficient time allocated to complete remediation.

**Step 3 — Re-Assessment**
- The employee must re-sit the full assessment (not just failed questions).
- The remediation attempt and result must be recorded in the Training Register.

**Step 4 — Continued Failure**
- If the employee fails the re-assessment, the matter shall be escalated to the Head of Human Resources and the DPO.
- A structured support plan shall be agreed, which may include one-to-one coaching, an alternative delivery method, or language support.
- A final third attempt shall be scheduled within a further **[X] calendar days**.

**Step 5 — Persistent Non-Compliance**
- Failure to pass after three attempts, or failure to engage with the remediation process, may constitute a performance or conduct matter to be handled under [Organization Name]'s **Performance Management / Disciplinary Policy**.
- The DPO shall document the matter and, where the employee's role involves access to personal data, consider whether access restrictions are appropriate pending resolution.

### 12.4 Non-Completion (Failure to Attempt) — Escalation Process

Where an employee fails to **attempt** mandatory training within the required timeframe (irrespective of assessment outcome):

| Day | Action | Responsible Party |
|---|---|---|
| Deadline Day −14 | First reminder sent via LMS / email | LMS (automated) / Human Resources |
| Deadline Day −7 | Second reminder sent; line manager copied | Human Resources |
| Deadline Day 0 | Deadline; non-completions flagged in dashboard | Human Resources |
| Deadline Day +7 | Escalation to line manager for individual follow-up | Human Resources / DPO |
| Deadline Day +21 | Escalation to Head of HR; formal notice issued to employee | Head of Human Resources |
| Deadline Day +30 | Referral to disciplinary process if no resolution | Head of Human Resources / DPO |

### 12.5 Records of Assessment and Remediation

All assessment scores, pass/fail outcomes, remediation attempts, and escalation actions must be recorded in the **Training Register** and retained in accordance with Section 11.3.

### 13.2 Role Descriptions

**Data Protection Officer (DPO)**
- Owns this procedure and is accountable for its effectiveness.
- Approves all training content and the annual Training Plan.
- Provides subject matter expertise and guidance on PDPA requirements.
- Monitors overall training compliance and reports to Senior Management.

**Head of Human Resources**
- Co-accountable with the DPO for the operational execution of this procedure.
- Ensures HR team resources are allocated to support training administration.
- Escalates persistent non-compliance cases through disciplinary channels.

**Human Resources Team**
- Manages day-to-day training administration including enrollment, reminders, records, and reporting.
- Maintains the Training Register.
- Coordinates scheduling of classroom and scenario-based training events.

**Line Managers**
- Responsible for ensuring their direct reports complete mandatory training within required timeframes.
- Support remediation for employees who fail assessments.
- Escalate concerns about individual training compliance to HR.

**IT / LMS Administrator**
- Maintains the technical functionality of the LMS platform.
- Provides technical support for e-learning module deployment and reporting.
- Ensures LMS enrollment data is synchronized with HR system data.

**All Employees**
- Are individually responsible for completing all assigned data protection training within the required timeframe.
- Must achieve the required pass mark on all assessments.
- Must apply the knowledge gained in the course of their duties.

### Appendix E — Glossary of PDPA Terms for Training Materials

*This glossary shall be incorporated into training materials and made available to all employees as a reference document.*

| Term | Plain Language Definition |
|---|---|
| **Personal data** | Information about a person that can identify them — for example, their name, IC number, address, email, phone number, medical information, or financial details. |
| **Sensitive personal data** | Special categories of personal data that require extra care — including health information, political opinions, religious beliefs, criminal offences, and biometric data. |
| **Data user** | [Organization Name] — the company that decides what personal data to collect, why, and how to use it. |
| **Data subject** | The individual whose personal data is being collected and used — for example, our customers or employees. |
| **Processing** | Anything done with personal data — collecting it, storing it, using it, sharing it, modifying it, or deleting it. |
| **Consent** | The data subject's freely given, specific, and informed agreement to the processing of their personal data. |
| **Data breach** | An incident where personal data is accidentally or unlawfully accessed, shared, lost, or destroyed without authorization. |
| **Data Subject Access Request (DSAR)** | A formal request by an individual to access the personal data that [Organization Name] holds about them. |
