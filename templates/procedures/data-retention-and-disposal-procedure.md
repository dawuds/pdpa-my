## 1. Purpose

*Describe the purpose of this procedure and the regulatory obligation it fulfils. Reference the specific PDPA section and the parent policy that this procedure operationalises.*

This procedure establishes the operational steps that [Organization Name] personnel must follow to implement the **[Organization Name] Data Retention and Disposal Policy**. It provides actionable instructions for the periodic review of data holdings, the application of approved retention schedules, the management of legal holds, and the secure disposal of personal data in a manner consistent with the requirements of the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA), Section 10 (Retention Principle)**.

The objective of this procedure is to ensure that personal data processed by [Organization Name] is:

- Retained only for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law or regulatory mandate;
- Disposed of securely, completely, and in a documented manner once the applicable retention period has expired; and
- Protected against unauthorised access, disclosure, or recovery during and after the disposal process.

This procedure supports [Organization Name]'s obligations under PDPA s10 and underpins broader accountability to the **Personal Data Protection Commissioner** and any applicable sectoral regulator.

## 5. Periodic Data Review Cycle and Ownership

*This section defines the cadence, structure, and ownership model for periodic reviews of personal data holdings across the organization. Describe the triggers, participants, and outputs of each review cycle.*

### 5.1 Overview

[Organization Name] shall conduct periodic reviews of its personal data holdings to identify data that has reached the end of its applicable retention period, confirm the accuracy of data classifications, and initiate disposal or legal hold procedures as appropriate. This review is a mandatory operational control under PDPA s10.

### 5.2 Review Cadence

| Review Type | Frequency | Trigger | Lead Responsibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Full Data Inventory Review** | Annual | Calendar-scheduled (Q[X] of each year) | DPO / Records Manager |
| **Departmental Spot Review** | Semi-annual | Departmental schedule | Data Owner |
| **System Decommission Review** | Event-driven | System retirement or migration | IT / Data Custodian |
| **Incident-Triggered Review** | Event-driven | Data breach or regulatory inquiry | DPO / Legal |
| **Regulatory Change Review** | Event-driven | Change in law or regulatory guidance | DPO / Legal / Compliance |

### 5.3 Review Ownership Model

Each data holding within [Organization Name] shall have a designated **Data Owner** accountable for the review of personal data within their domain. Data Owners shall be assigned at the system and data category level as documented in the [Organization Name] Data Inventory Register.

| Role | Responsibility in Review Cycle |
|---|---|
| **DPO** | Initiates and oversees the annual review cycle; approves disposals; escalates exceptions. |
| **Records Manager** | Coordinates the review schedule; maintains the Retention Schedule and Data Inventory Register; tracks completion. |
| **Data Owner (Head of Department)** | Accountable for review of personal data in their business domain; approves disposal or hold decisions for their data. |
| **Data Custodian (IT)** | Executes technical review of system-level data holdings; provides storage and access reports; implements approved disposals. |
| **Legal / Compliance** | Advises on legal hold requirements; reviews retention obligations arising from regulatory change. |

### 5.4 Review Process Steps

*For each step, describe the specific actions to be taken, the responsible party, and the expected output.*

**Step 1 — Initiation**

1. The DPO or Records Manager issues a formal **Data Review Notice** to all Data Owners at least [X] weeks prior to the scheduled review window.
2. The notice shall specify the review period, scope, and deadline for departmental submissions.
3. The Records Manager extracts a current snapshot of the Data Inventory Register to serve as the review baseline.

**Step 2 — Departmental Review**

1. Each Data Owner, assisted by their Data Custodian, reviews the personal data holdings within their domain against the current Retention Schedule.
2. Data Owners complete the **Data Review Worksheet** (Appendix A) for each data category under their ownership, documenting:
   - Current retention period and end date;
   - Whether data is subject to a legal hold;
   - Whether data is eligible for disposal;
   - Any changes to data classification or ownership; and
   - Any anomalies or exceptions requiring DPO review.
3. Completed worksheets are submitted to the Records Manager by the specified deadline.

**Step 3 — Consolidation and Exception Handling**

1. The Records Manager consolidates all departmental submissions into the **Consolidated Review Report**.
2. Exceptions, disputes, or anomalies are escalated to the DPO for resolution.
3. The DPO, in consultation with Legal and Compliance where necessary, adjudicates on exceptions and issues disposal or hold directives.

**Step 4 — Disposal Initiation**

1. Data confirmed as eligible for disposal (no active legal hold, retention period expired) is listed in the **Disposal Authorisation Register** (Appendix B).
2. The DPO or delegate issues a formal **Disposal Authorisation** for each batch of eligible data.
3. Disposal is executed in accordance with Section 8 of this procedure.

**Step 5 — Review Closure and Reporting**

1. The Records Manager updates the Data Inventory Register to reflect the outcomes of the review.
2. A **Data Review Completion Report** is prepared and submitted to the DPO and relevant Data Owners.
3. The DPO presents a summary to [Senior Management / Risk Committee / Board] as part of the Annual Disposal Audit (Section 12).

### 6.3 End-of-Retention Process

When a data holding reaches the end of its applicable retention period, the following steps shall be taken:

## 7. Legal Hold: Identification, Notification, and Tracking

*This section defines the procedure for placing, managing, and lifting legal holds on personal data. A legal hold suspends the normal disposal of data that may be relevant to litigation, regulatory investigation, audit, or legal proceedings.*

### 7.1 Purpose of Legal Hold

A legal hold is a mandatory preservation directive that overrides the normal retention and disposal cycle. When a legal hold is in effect, the identified data must not be altered, deleted, or otherwise disposed of until the hold is formally lifted by the issuing authority.

### 7.2 Grounds for Issuing a Legal Hold

A legal hold may be issued in any of the following circumstances:

- Commencement of, or reasonable anticipation of, civil or criminal litigation involving [Organization Name];
- Receipt of a regulatory investigation notice, formal inquiry, or audit request from BNM, PDPC, NACSA, PDRM, or any other authority;
- Receipt of a court order, subpoena, or discovery request requiring preservation of documents;
- Internal investigation involving potential misconduct, fraud, or data breach; or
- Any other circumstance in which Legal determines that preservation is prudent.

### 7.3 Legal Hold Identification and Issuance

**Step 1 — Identification**

1. The General Counsel, Head of Legal, or DPO identifies circumstances that give rise to a legal hold obligation.
2. Legal prepares a **Legal Hold Notice** (see Appendix C) specifying:
   - The matter name and case reference;
   - The scope of data to be preserved (data categories, systems, date ranges, custodians);
   - The custodians (individuals whose data holdings are in scope);
   - The date of effect; and
   - The name and contact details of the Legal Hold Coordinator.

**Step 2 — DPO Review**

1. Legal submits the Legal Hold Notice to the DPO for review and concurrence prior to issuance.
2. The DPO confirms that the identified data scope is consistent with [Organization Name]'s Data Inventory Register and flags any data that may have already been disposed of.
3. If data within scope has already been disposed of, Legal is notified immediately.

**Step 3 — Issuance and Notification**

1. The Legal Hold Notice is issued to all identified custodians and their respective Data Owners by the Legal Hold Coordinator.
2. Notification shall be made in writing (email with read receipt or secure internal messaging), and custodians are required to acknowledge receipt within [X] business days.
3. Concurrently, the Legal Hold Coordinator notifies the Records Manager and relevant Data Custodians to suspend disposal of the in-scope data in all relevant systems.

### 7.4 Legal Hold Tracking

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Legal Hold Register** | Maintained by: Records Manager / Legal Hold Coordinator |
| **Location** | [System / SharePoint / Document Management System path] |
| **Update Frequency** | Upon issuance, modification, or lifting of each hold |

The **Legal Hold Register** (see Appendix D) shall record the following for each active hold:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Hold Reference Number | Unique identifier for the legal hold |
| Matter Name | Description of the underlying legal matter |
| Date Issued | Date the legal hold was formally issued |
| Issued By | Name and role of the issuing authority |
| Scope of Data | Data categories, systems, and date ranges in scope |
| Custodians | Names of individuals subject to the hold |
| Acknowledgement Status | Confirmation of receipt by each custodian |
| Review Date | Next scheduled review of the hold's continued necessity |
| Date Lifted | Date the legal hold was formally released (if applicable) |
| Lifted By | Name and role of the releasing authority |
| Disposal Date | Date disposal was executed following lifting of the hold |

### 7.5 Legal Hold Maintenance and Review

1. All active legal holds shall be reviewed by Legal and the DPO on a **[quarterly]** basis to confirm continued necessity.
2. Legal shall provide a written update to the Records Manager confirming the status of each active hold at each review.
3. The Records Manager shall flag to Legal any data subject to an active hold that would otherwise be approaching its retention end date.

### 7.6 Lifting a Legal Hold

1. When the underlying matter is resolved and the legal hold is no longer required, Legal issues a formal **Legal Hold Release Notice** (see Appendix C) to all custodians and the Records Manager.
2. Upon receipt of the Release Notice, the Records Manager:
   - Updates the Legal Hold Register;
   - Initiates a retention status review for the released data; and
   - If the data has exceeded its retention period, initiates the disposal process in accordance with Section 8.
3. No data released from a legal hold shall be disposed of without DPO authorisation.

## 8. Secure Disposal Methods

*This section prescribes the approved methods for securely disposing of personal data across different media types. The method selected must be appropriate to the sensitivity of the data, the media type, and whether the media is to be re-used or retired.*

### 8.1 Selection of Disposal Method

The appropriate disposal method shall be selected based on the following factors:

- **Media type** (magnetic disk, SSD, optical media, paper, mobile device, etc.);
- **Data sensitivity** (standard personal data vs. sensitive personal data as defined under PDPA s4);
- **Intended post-disposal use of media** (re-use within the organization, re-use by a third party, or permanent retirement); and
- **Applicable standard** (NIST SP 800-88, HMG IS5, or equivalent).

| Media Type | Sensitivity | Media Re-used? | Approved Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| HDD (magnetic) | Standard | Yes | Secure overwrite (minimum 3-pass) |
| HDD (magnetic) | Standard | No | Degaussing + Physical destruction |
| HDD (magnetic) | Sensitive | No | Degaussing + Physical destruction |
| SSD / Flash storage | Standard | Yes | Cryptographic erasure + vendor secure erase |
| SSD / Flash storage | Any | No | Physical destruction |
| Magnetic tape | Any | No | Degaussing + Physical destruction |
| USB / Removable flash | Any | No | Physical destruction |
| Optical media (CD/DVD) | Any | No | Physical destruction (shredding) |
| Paper records | Standard | N/A | Cross-cut shredding (DIN 66399 P-4 minimum) |
| Paper records | Sensitive | N/A | Micro-cut shredding (DIN 66399 P-5 minimum) or incineration |
| Mobile devices | Any | No | Factory reset + Physical destruction (if retired) |
| Mobile devices | Sensitive | No | Physical destruction |
| Printers / MFDs | Any | Retiring | Internal storage secure wipe by vendor |
| Cloud-hosted data | Any | N/A | CSP-verified deletion (see Section 11) |

### 8.2 Secure Deletion (Software-Based Overwriting)

*Applicable to magnetic hard disk drives being retained for re-use within the organization.*

1. Secure deletion shall be performed using an approved software tool from the [Organization Name] Approved Software List (Appendix E).
2. The minimum overwriting standard is **[3-pass overwrite]** in accordance with **NIST SP 800-88 Rev.1 (Clear)** or equivalent.
3. Sensitive personal data on magnetic media designated for re-use shall be overwritten using a minimum **[7-pass]** standard.
4. The Data Custodian shall verify the successful completion of each overwrite operation and retain the tool-generated log as evidence.
5. Secure deletion is **not** an approved method for SSDs, flash storage, or any media with wear-levelling characteristics; cryptographic erasure or physical destruction must be used instead.

**Procedure:**

| Step | Action | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Obtain Disposal Authorisation from DPO | Data Owner |
| 2 | Isolate media from production systems | Data Custodian |
| 3 | Boot approved sanitization tool | Data Custodian |
| 4 | Execute overwrite to the approved standard | Data Custodian |
| 5 | Verify successful completion via tool-generated report | Data Custodian |
| 6 | Label media as "Sanitized — Date [DD/MM/YYYY]" | Data Custodian |
| 7 | Record disposal in Disposal Register | Records Manager |

### 8.3 Degaussing

*Applicable to magnetic hard disk drives, magnetic tape, and floppy disks being permanently retired.*

1. Degaussing shall be performed using a degausser that meets or exceeds the coercivity rating of the media being treated.
2. Degaussed media shall be visually verified and physically marked as degaussed.
3. Degaussing renders the media unusable for re-deployment; physical destruction shall follow degaussing for all sensitive personal data media.
4. Degaussing is **not** effective for SSDs, USB drives, optical media, or any solid-state or flash-based storage; physical destruction must be used for these media types.
5. A degaussing log (date, operator, equipment, media serial numbers) shall be retained as evidence.

### 8.4 Physical Destruction

*Applicable to media that cannot be securely sanitized by overwriting or degaussing, or to all media containing sensitive personal data designated for permanent retirement.*

**On-site physical destruction:**

1. Physical destruction of paper records shall be carried out using a cross-cut shredder meeting at minimum **DIN 66399 Security Level P-4** for standard personal data and **P-5** for sensitive personal data.
2. Physical destruction of electronic media (HDDs, SSDs, USB drives) shall be carried out using an approved media shredder or disintegrator producing particles of no more than **[X]mm x [X]mm** in accordance with the approved standard.
3. Destruction shall be witnessed by a minimum of **[two]** authorized personnel, at least one of whom shall be from a function independent of the initiating department.
4. A destruction log shall be completed immediately following each destruction event (see Appendix F).

**Off-site physical destruction (ITAD vendor):**

Refer to Section 9 for procedures governing third-party ITAD vendor engagement.

### 8.5 Cryptographic Erasure

*Applicable as an alternative to physical destruction for encrypted storage media, particularly SSDs and mobile devices, where the data encryption key is destroyed, rendering encrypted data permanently unrecoverable.*

1. Cryptographic erasure is only permissible where:
   - The full contents of the media were encrypted using an approved encryption algorithm (minimum AES-256) **prior to** any personal data being stored on it; and
   - The encryption key was generated and managed in accordance with [Organization Name]'s Key Management Policy (Document ID: [Key Management Policy Document ID]).
2. Disposal by cryptographic erasure is executed by the secure and permanent destruction of all copies of the encryption key.
3. The Data Custodian shall document the key destruction event, including the key identifier, key management system reference, date, and operator.
4. Following cryptographic erasure, if the media is to be permanently retired, physical destruction is also recommended for sensitive personal data.

## 9. Third-Party Media and Hardware Disposal Certification

*This section governs the engagement of external vendors for the disposal of IT assets and media containing personal data, and the certification and documentation requirements that must be obtained from such vendors.*

### 9.1 Vendor Selection and Due Diligence

1. All ITAD vendors engaged by [Organization Name] must be approved by the DPO and IT Security function prior to engagement.
2. Due diligence shall be conducted prior to initial vendor approval and renewed on an **annual** basis, covering:
   - Vendor certification status (e.g., e-Stewards, R2/RIOS, or equivalent);
   - Insurance coverage (public liability, professional indemnity);
   - Data security controls and chain-of-custody procedures;
   - Sub-contractor disclosure (any sub-contractors involved in the destruction process); and
   - References or audit reports from previous engagements.
3. ITAD vendor approval shall be documented in the [Organization Name] Approved Vendor Register.

### 9.2 Contractual Requirements

1. All ITAD vendors must execute a **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** with [Organization Name] prior to handling any media or hardware containing personal data, in accordance with PDPA requirements for data processors.
2. The DPA must include, at minimum:
   - Binding obligations on the vendor to process (dispose of) data only as instructed by [Organization Name];
   - Prohibition on re-sale, re-use, or disclosure of any personal data recovered from the media;
   - Requirements for sub-contractor controls and pass-through obligations;
   - Incident reporting obligations in the event of a disposal incident or data exposure; and
   - Audit rights for [Organization Name] to inspect vendor processes.

### 9.3 Chain of Custody Procedures

| Step | Action | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Prepare a detailed **Asset Manifest** (Appendix G) listing all media/hardware items for disposal (serial numbers, asset tags, model, data classification) | Data Custodian |
| 2 | Perform on-site secure deletion or degaussing where practicable prior to handing over to vendor | Data Custodian |
| 3 | Package and seal media/hardware in tamper-evident containers | Data Custodian |
| 4 | Transfer possession to vendor representative; both parties sign the **Chain of Custody Transfer Form** (Appendix H) | Data Custodian / ITAD Vendor |
| 5 | Vendor transports media to destruction facility | ITAD Vendor |
| 6 | Vendor executes destruction in accordance with agreed method and standard | ITAD Vendor |
| 7 | Vendor provides **Certificate of Destruction** (see Section 10) within [X] business days of destruction | ITAD Vendor |
| 8 | Data Custodian verifies Certificate of Destruction against Asset Manifest | Data Custodian |
| 9 | DPO reviews and countersigns Certificate of Destruction | DPO |
| 10 | Certificate and manifest filed in Disposal Records (see Section 10) | Records Manager |

### 9.4 Witnessed Destruction

1. For disposal events involving **sensitive personal data** or disposal batches exceeding **[X] assets**, [Organization Name] shall where possible arrange for a representative to witness the destruction process, either on-site at the vendor's facility or via verified video evidence.
2. The witnessing representative shall sign the Chain of Custody Transfer Form and Certificate of Destruction as an independent witness.

### 9.5 Rejected or Failed Disposal

1. If a vendor fails to provide a Certificate of Destruction within the required timeframe, or if there are concerns about the adequacy of the disposal, the matter shall be escalated to the DPO and IT Security immediately.
2. The DPO shall determine whether a data breach notification obligation arises under PDPA.
3. The vendor's approval status shall be reviewed and may be suspended pending investigation.

## 11. Cloud Data Deletion Verification Procedures

*This section establishes specific procedures for verifying the deletion of personal data stored in cloud environments, where standard physical disposal methods are inapplicable and deletion assurance must be obtained through contractual means, technical controls, and vendor certification.*

### 11.1 Overview

The deletion of personal data from cloud environments presents unique verification challenges because [Organization Name] does not have direct access to the underlying physical infrastructure. This procedure addresses those challenges through a combination of:

- Contractual obligations on Cloud Service Providers (CSPs);
- Technical deletion controls within the cloud platform; and
- Vendor-provided deletion certification and attestation.

### 11.2 CSP Contractual Requirements

Prior to storing personal data in any cloud environment, the following contractual protections must be in place:

1. The CSP must execute a **Data Processing Agreement** with [Organization Name] that includes explicit data deletion obligations.
2. The DPA must specify:
   - The CSP's obligation to delete all personal data (including backup copies) within a defined period following termination of the service or receipt of a deletion instruction from [Organization Name];
   - The maximum timeframe within which deletion of active data and backup/replica data will be completed;
   - The method by which deletion will be executed (e.g., cryptographic erasure of encryption keys, logical deletion, physical destruction of media);
   - The CSP's obligation to provide written confirmation of deletion upon request; and
   - Audit rights enabling [Organization Name] or its representatives to verify deletion controls.
3. CSP commitments relevant to data deletion from standard agreements (e.g., AWS Data Processing Addendum, Microsoft Online Services DPA, Google Cloud Data Processing Terms) shall be reviewed by Legal and the DPO and documented in the **Cloud Service Register** (Appendix J).

### 11.3 In-Platform Deletion Controls

Prior to requesting CSP-level deletion, [Organization Name] personnel shall:

1. **Delete at the application layer:** Remove data from all active databases, object stores, queues, and caches within the cloud application. This includes:
   - Database record deletion (confirmed by query verification);
   - Object storage deletion (confirmed by API response and absence of object);
   - Cache invalidation and flush; and
   - Search index deletion.
2. **Delete application backups:** Identify and delete all application-managed backups that contain the subject data.
3. **Remove from analytics and logging pipelines:** Identify and delete personal data that has been ingested into cloud-native analytics, logging, or monitoring services.
4. **Document each action:** The Data Custodian records each deletion step in the **Cloud Deletion Verification Checklist** (Appendix K).

### 11.4 CSP-Level Deletion Request

Where personal data is held in CSP-managed backups, disaster recovery replicas, or infrastructure-level storage to which [Organization Name] does not have direct delete access:

1. The Data Custodian submits a formal **Cloud Deletion Request** to the CSP through the CSP's designated channel (support portal, account manager, or API endpoint as specified in the DPA).
2. The request shall reference the DPA, the specific data and storage locations affected, and the required deletion timeframe.
3. The Records Manager tracks the request status and escalates to the DPO if acknowledgement or completion confirmation is not received within the CSP's committed SLA.

### 11.5 Deletion Verification

Upon completion of cloud data deletion, [Organization Name] shall obtain and retain the following verification evidence:

| Evidence Type | Description | Obtained From |
|---|---|---|
| **Application-level deletion confirmation** | Query results, API responses, or system logs confirming data absence | Internal Data Custodian |
| **CSP Deletion Confirmation** | Written confirmation from CSP that all copies (including backups and replicas) have been deleted | CSP (via support case, DPA mechanism, or account portal) |
| **CSP Attestation / Certification** | Third-party audit report or attestation (e.g., SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001 certificate) confirming CSP's deletion controls | CSP / Third-party auditor |
| **Cryptographic key destruction record** | Evidence of destruction of encryption keys (where cryptographic erasure is the deletion mechanism) | CSP key management service logs |

### 11.6 Limitations and Residual Risk

1. Where CSP technical constraints prevent complete deletion within the standard timeframe (e.g., backup retention within CSP infrastructure), the DPO shall document the residual risk and the compensating controls in place.
2. Residual risk resulting from CSP deletion limitations shall be reviewed at each annual disposal audit.
3. Where residual risk is assessed as unacceptable, [Organization Name] shall consider alternative data residency, encryption, or service provider arrangements.

## 12. Annual Disposal Audit and Reporting

*This section defines the requirements for the annual audit of [Organization Name]'s data disposal activities, the reporting obligations arising from that audit, and the escalation pathway for identified deficiencies.*

### 12.1 Purpose of the Annual Disposal Audit

The annual disposal audit provides [Organization Name]'s senior management and governance bodies with independent assurance that:

- Data disposal activities have been carried out in accordance with this procedure and the Retention Schedule;
- Disposal records are complete, accurate, and retained appropriately;
- Legal holds are being managed effectively and released in a timely manner;
- Third-party ITAD vendors and CSPs are meeting their contractual disposal obligations; and
- Control deficiencies identified in the prior year's audit have been remediated.

### 12.2 Audit Scope and Frequency

| Audit Type | Frequency | Scope | Lead |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Annual Disposal Audit** | Annual | Full scope — all disposal activities in the preceding 12 months | DPO / Internal Audit |
| **ITAD Vendor Audit** | Annual | Third-party ITAD vendor controls and certification | IT Security / Procurement |
| **Cloud Deletion Control Review** | Annual | CSP deletion controls, DPA compliance, verification evidence | IT Security / DPO |
| **Legal Hold Review** | Quarterly | Status and necessity of all active legal holds | Legal / DPO |
| **Targeted Review** | Event-driven | In response to incident, complaint, or regulatory inquiry | DPO |

### 12.3 Annual Disposal Audit Process

**Phase 1 — Planning**

1. The DPO prepares an **Annual Disposal Audit Plan** by [Month] of each year, defining the audit scope, sample methodology, and audit team.
2. The Records Manager provides the following inputs:
   - Complete Disposal Authorisation Register for the audit period;
   - All Certificates of Destruction issued during the audit period;
   - Legal Hold Register (current status and all holds closed during the audit period);
   - ITAD vendor contracts, DPAs, and recent certification documents; and
   - Cloud Deletion Verification Checklists for the audit period.

**Phase 2 — Fieldwork**

The audit shall include, at minimum:

| Audit Test | Description |
|---|---|
| **Retention Schedule Compliance** | Sample review of disposed data to confirm disposal occurred after, not before, the applicable retention period end date |
| **Disposal Authorisation Completeness** | Verify all disposal events in the period have a corresponding Disposal Authorisation with DPO approval |
| **Certificate of Destruction Completeness** | Verify all disposal events have a corresponding CoD and that CoD details are consistent with the Disposal Authorisation |
| **Legal Hold Effectiveness** | Confirm no data subject to an active or past legal hold was disposed of during the hold period |
| **ITAD Vendor Certification** | Review vendor CoDs, chain-of-custody records, and current certification status |
| **Cloud Deletion Verification** | Review Cloud Deletion Verification Checklists and CSP deletion confirmations |
| **Unresolved Exceptions** | Identify any disposal events without complete documentation or where disposal method was not as authorised |

**Phase 3 — Reporting**

1. The DPO prepares the **Annual Disposal Audit Report** within **[X]** weeks of completing fieldwork.
2. The report shall include:
   - Executive summary of audit findings;
   - Key metrics (total disposal events, total volume of data disposed, legal holds active/closed, vendor audits completed);
   - Findings and observations rated by risk (Critical / High / Medium / Low / Informational);
   - Recommendations for remediation;
   - Status of prior-year recommendations; and
   - DPO's overall assessment of compliance.

**Phase 4 — Escalation and Governance Reporting**

| Audience | Report Format | Frequency | Submitted By |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Data Owner / Department Head** | Department-level findings extract | Annual | DPO |
| **Chief Risk Officer / CISO** | Full Audit Report | Annual | DPO |
| **Risk Committee / Board Risk Committee** | Executive Summary | Annual | DPO / CRO |
| **Internal Audit** | Full Audit Report | Annual | DPO |
| **[Regulatory Body]** | As required | Upon request | DPO / Legal |

### 12.4 Remediation and Tracking

1. All findings rated **High** or **Critical** must have a remediation owner and target completion date assigned within **[X]** business days of report issuance.
2. The DPO shall track remediation progress and report status updates to the Risk Committee on a **[quarterly]** basis until all critical and high findings are closed.
3. Findings that remain unresolved beyond their target completion date shall be escalated to the [CEO / CRO / Board] for intervention.

## 14. Non-Compliance and Escalation

*This section defines the consequences of non-compliance with this procedure and the escalation pathway for identified violations.*

### 14.1 Non-Compliance Scenarios

The following constitute violations of this procedure:

- Failure to apply the retention schedule, resulting in personal data being retained beyond its authorised retention period;
- Disposal of personal data without a Disposal Authorisation;
- Disposal of personal data subject to an active legal hold;
- Use of an unapproved disposal method;
- Failure to obtain or retain a Certificate of Destruction;
- Engagement of an unapproved ITAD vendor;
- Failure to verify cloud data deletion; and
- Deliberate or negligent circumvention of any control defined in this procedure.

### 14.2 Escalation Pathway

| Severity | Examples | Escalation To | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Disposal during legal hold; data breach arising from disposal failure; unauthorized ITAD engagement | DPO → CEO → Board; PDPC notification assessment | Immediate (same day) |
| **High** | Repeated failure to obtain CoDs; major gaps in disposal records; unapproved disposal method | DPO → CRO / CISO | Within [1] business day |
| **Medium** | Single procedural deviation without data loss; delayed disposal records | DPO → Data Owner | Within [5] business days |
| **Low** | Documentation gaps; minor scheduling delays | Records Manager → Data Owner | Within [10] business days |

### 14.3 Disciplinary Action

Non-compliance with this procedure may result in disciplinary action in accordance with [Organization Name]'s Disciplinary Policy, up to and including termination of employment. Instances involving breach of PDPA obligations may additionally be referred to the Personal Data Protection Commissioner.
