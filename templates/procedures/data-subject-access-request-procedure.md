## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*Describe the intent of this procedure and the regulatory obligation it fulfils. Reference the specific PDPA sections and explain why the procedure is necessary for the organisation.*

This procedure establishes the formal process by which [Organization Name] receives, validates, processes, and responds to Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) submitted by individuals whose personal data is processed by [Organization Name] in its capacity as a data controller. The procedure ensures [Organization Name] meets its statutory obligations under the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**, specifically:

- **Section 30** — Right of access to personal data
- **Section 31** — Right to correct personal data
- **Section 32** — Limitation of right of access
- **Section 33** — Data user may impose fee for compliance

This procedure supports [Organization Name]'s commitment to transparency, accountability, and lawful data processing, and provides a repeatable, auditable process to protect both the rights of data subjects and the legitimate interests of [Organization Name].

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the organisational scope (which entities, departments, and systems are covered), the personal data categories in scope, and any explicit exclusions.*

This procedure applies to:

**Organisational Scope:**
- All departments, business units, and subsidiaries of [Organization Name] that process personal data
- All employees, contractors, agents, and third-party data processors acting on behalf of [Organization Name]
- All systems, databases, and filing systems (physical and electronic) that hold personal data belonging to data subjects

**Data Subject Scope:**
- Any natural person whose personal data is held by [Organization Name], including but not limited to: customers, prospective customers, employees, former employees, guarantors, authorised signatories, and beneficial owners
- Authorised representatives acting on behalf of data subjects (subject to verification requirements in Section 5)

**Exclusions:**

| Exclusion | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Personal data processed solely for personal, family, or household affairs | Outside scope of PDPA per s.3(1) |
| Data held by government authorities acting in official capacity | Subject to separate regulatory frameworks |
| Personal data of deceased individuals | PDPA applies to living individuals only |
| Anonymised or de-identified data | No longer constitutes personal data under PDPA |

## 4. DSAR Receipt, Logging, and Acknowledgement

*This section describes how [Organization Name] receives, registers, and acknowledges DSARs. All staff who may receive a DSAR must be familiar with the intake procedure and immediate escalation requirements.*

### 4.1 Authorised Submission Channels

[Organization Name] accepts DSARs through the following designated channels only. Requests received through unauthorised channels must be redirected to the DPO immediately.

| Channel | Contact Details | Availability |
|---|---|---|
| Dedicated DSAR Email | [dsar@organizationname.com.my] | Business days, 9:00 AM – 5:30 PM |
| Written Post (Registered Mail) | [Organization Name], Attn: Data Protection Officer, [Address] | Received upon delivery |
| In-Person Submission | [Branch / Head Office Address], Attn: Data Protection Officer | By appointment, business hours |
| Online Portal | [URL of DSAR Portal, if applicable] | 24/7 submission; reviewed on business days |

> **Note:** DSARs received verbally (e.g., by telephone or at branch counters) must be converted to a written request using **Appendix A (DSAR Form)** before any processing commences. The receiving staff member must assist the requestor in completing the form and immediately escalate to the DPO.

### 4.2 DSAR Intake Checklist

Upon receipt of any DSAR, the receiving staff member or DPO shall immediately assess completeness using the following checklist:

- [ ] Full name of data subject provided
- [ ] Identity document type and number provided (NRIC / Passport / [Other])
- [ ] Contact details (address, email, phone) provided for correspondence
- [ ] Nature and scope of data requested specified, or sufficient information to identify the data sought
- [ ] Basis for request stated (self or authorised representative)
- [ ] If authorised representative: written authorisation and representative's identity document included
- [ ] Signed declaration by requestor included
- [ ] Applicable fee paid (or fee waiver request submitted) — refer to Section 10

If the request is incomplete, the DPO shall notify the requestor within **three (3) business days** specifying the missing information. The 21-day statutory clock does not commence until a complete and valid request is received.

### 4.3 DSAR Registration and Logging

Upon receipt of a complete DSAR, the DPO shall immediately register the request in the **DSAR Log** (refer to Section 11 and Appendix C). Each DSAR shall be assigned a unique reference number using the following format:

**DSAR-[YYYY]-[MM]-[Sequential Number]**

*Example: DSAR-2025-06-001*

The DSAR Log entry shall capture the following minimum fields at intake:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| DSAR Reference Number | Unique identifier as per format above |
| Date of Receipt | Date complete and valid request received |
| Date Statutory Clock Commences | Same as Date of Receipt for complete requests |
| Statutory Response Deadline | Date of Receipt + 21 calendar days |
| Requestor Name | Full name as per identity document |
| Requestor NRIC / Passport Number | Masked: retain last 4 digits only, e.g., xxxxxx-xx-1234 |
| Requestor Contact Details | Email and/or postal address for correspondence |
| Nature of Request | Summary of data categories / systems specified |
| Submission Channel | Email / Post / In-person / Portal |
| Assigned Handler | Name and designation of DPO or delegated officer |
| Status | Received / In Progress / Pending Verification / Responded / Refused / Extended |
| Fee Status | Paid / Waived / Pending Payment |

### 4.4 Acknowledgement to Requestor

[Organization Name] shall acknowledge receipt of every DSAR — whether complete or incomplete — **within twenty-four (24) hours** of receipt on a business day, or by the next business day if received outside business hours.

**Acknowledgement communication shall include:**

- Confirmation that the DSAR has been received
- The unique DSAR reference number assigned
- The anticipated response date (or, if the request is incomplete, a request for additional information)
- Contact details of the DPO for follow-up queries
- Where applicable, details of the prescribed fee payable and payment instructions

**Template acknowledgement letters are provided in Appendix B.**

> **Internal SLA:** Acknowledgement must be issued within 24 hours. Non-issuance within 24 hours is a Level 2 internal escalation event and must be reported to [Head of Compliance / Chief Risk Officer].

## 5. Identity Verification Requirements and Methods

*This section ensures that [Organization Name] releases personal data only to the legitimate data subject or their duly authorised representative. Robust verification is critical to prevent unauthorised disclosure and potential data breaches.*

### 5.1 Verification Principles

Identity verification under this procedure shall be:

- **Proportionate** — verification requirements shall be commensurate with the sensitivity of the data requested and the channel of submission
- **Non-discriminatory** — verification shall not impose unreasonable burdens on data subjects with limited access to identification documents
- **Documented** — all verification steps and outcomes shall be recorded in the DSAR Log

### 5.2 Verification Requirements for Direct Requestors

A data subject requesting access to their own personal data must provide at least **one (1) primary identity document**:

| Document Type | Acceptable Format |
|---|---|
| Malaysian NRIC (MyKad) | Certified true copy, or original sighted in-person |
| Malaysian Passport | Certified true copy (bio-data page and photo page), or original sighted in-person |
| Foreign Passport | Certified true copy, or original sighted in-person (non-Malaysian residents) |
| [Other Government-Issued ID] | [Specify acceptance criteria] |

Where the DSAR is submitted via email or online portal, the requestor shall provide:
- A scanned copy of the primary identity document (front and back), **and**
- Confirmation of a secondary identifier already held by [Organization Name] (e.g., account number, registered email address, date of birth, or another identifier recorded on the account)

Where identity cannot be confirmed through documentary means, the DPO may arrange a **video verification session** or require the requestor to attend in person.

### 5.3 Verification Requirements for Authorised Representatives

Where a DSAR is submitted by an authorised representative on behalf of a data subject:

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Written Authorisation | Signed letter of authorisation from the data subject, specifying the scope of authority granted |
| Data Subject Identity Document | Certified true copy of the data subject's NRIC or passport |
| Representative Identity Document | Certified true copy of the representative's own NRIC or passport |
| Relationship Declaration | Statement of relationship between representative and data subject (e.g., legal guardian, attorney, next-of-kin) |
| Supporting Legal Documents (where applicable) | Power of Attorney, Court Order, or Letters of Administration as appropriate |

> **Special Case — Deceased Data Subjects:** PDPA applies to living individuals. [Organization Name] shall not process DSARs on behalf of deceased individuals under this procedure. Queries relating to deceased persons' data shall be redirected to [Legal / Estate Administration process].

### 5.4 Verification of Minors

Where the data subject is a minor (below 18 years of age):

- The DSAR must be submitted by a **parent or legal guardian**
- The parent or guardian must provide their own identity document and proof of guardianship (birth certificate, court order, or equivalent)
- The minor's identity document (if any) or birth certificate must be provided

### 5.5 Failed Verification

If [Organization Name] is unable to verify the identity of the requestor to a reasonable standard of confidence:

1. The DPO shall notify the requestor in writing within **five (5) business days** of the verification attempt, specifying what additional evidence is required
2. The statutory 21-day period shall be **suspended** from the date of the verification failure notification until satisfactory evidence is received
3. If the requestor fails to provide satisfactory evidence within **[30] calendar days** of notification, [Organization Name] may treat the DSAR as withdrawn and close the request, with a written notice to the requestor

All verification outcomes (pass/fail/pending) must be recorded in the DSAR Log.

## 6. Response Period Management and Extension Procedures

*This section governs the management of the 21-day statutory response deadline, including tracking, escalation triggers, and the limited circumstances in which an extension may be applied.*

### 6.1 Statutory Response Period

Under **PDPA s.30(3)**, [Organization Name] must respond to a valid DSAR within the period prescribed by the Personal Data Protection Regulations 2013. The prescribed period is **21 calendar days** from the date of receipt of a complete and valid request (the "Response Deadline").

The Response Deadline shall be tracked automatically in the DSAR Log and shall trigger escalation alerts at the following intervals:

| Days from Receipt | Trigger Action |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | DSAR registered; acknowledgement issued; Handler assigned |
| Day 5 | DPO reviews progress; system searches initiated (refer to Section 7) |
| Day 10 | First internal progress check; exemption review commenced (if applicable) |
| Day 15 | Draft response prepared; Legal review initiated if exemptions identified |
| Day 18 | DPO review and approval of response |
| Day 19 | Escalation to [Head of Compliance] if response not yet approved |
| Day 21 | Response must be dispatched; DSAR Log updated to "Responded" or "Refused" |

> **Internal Policy:** [Organization Name] targets response within **[15] business days** as an internal performance standard. The 21-day statutory deadline is the outer limit, not the operational target.

### 6.2 Extension Procedures

*The PDPA Regulations provide for an extension of the response period in prescribed circumstances. Detail the specific grounds recognised by [Organization Name] and the process for invoking an extension.*

An extension of the response period may be applied where:

| Ground for Extension | Description | Maximum Extension |
|---|---|---|
| Complexity of Request | The volume or diversity of data categories requested is substantial and cannot reasonably be processed within 21 days | [As prescribed by Regulations — specify period] |
| Multiple Concurrent DSARs | [Organization Name] has received an unusually high volume of DSARs within the same period | [As prescribed by Regulations — specify period] |
| Third-Party Data Processor Delay | Personal data is held by an authorised data processor who cannot retrieve and supply the data within the standard period | [As prescribed by Regulations — specify period] |
| Ongoing Legal Proceedings | Processing of the DSAR would materially prejudice ongoing or reasonably anticipated legal proceedings | [Seek legal advice; document basis] |

**Extension Process:**

1. The DPO identifies the ground for extension no later than **Day 15** of the statutory period
2. DPO documents the extension basis in the DSAR Log with a written justification
3. Approval from [Head of Compliance / Chief Risk Officer] is obtained
4. Written notice is sent to the requestor **before the expiry of the original 21-day period**, clearly stating:
   - The reason for the extension
   - The revised response date
   - The requestor's right to complain to the Personal Data Protection Commissioner
5. Extension notice template is available at **Appendix B**

> **Important:** An extension does not eliminate [Organization Name]'s obligation to respond. Failure to respond within the extended period may constitute a criminal offence under PDPA s.30 and must be escalated immediately to [Legal Counsel] and [Group Chief Compliance Officer].

### 6.3 Missed Deadline Protocol

If for any reason [Organization Name] cannot issue a response by the Response Deadline:

- [ ] DPO immediately notifies [Head of Compliance] and [Legal Counsel]
- [ ] Incident is recorded in the compliance incident register
- [ ] Root cause analysis is initiated within [5] business days
- [ ] Remediation actions are documented and tracked
- [ ] Assessment of whether a regulatory notification is required is completed by Legal Counsel

## 7. System Search Scope and Methodology

*This section defines how [Organization Name] conducts a thorough and consistent search across all systems to locate personal data responsive to a DSAR. The search must be comprehensive and defensible in the event of audit or regulatory inquiry.*

### 7.1 System Search Inventory

The DPO shall maintain and regularly update a **Data System Inventory** (linked to the organisation's Record of Processing Activities / ROPA) listing all systems that hold personal data. The following systems are in scope for every DSAR search unless the request specifies a narrower scope:

| System / Repository | Data Custodian | Data Categories Held | Search Method | Estimated Retrieval Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Core Banking System] | [IT / Operations] | Account data, transaction history, KYC | [Automated query / IT request] | [X business days] |
| [Customer Relationship Management (CRM)] | [Retail Banking / Business Banking] | Contact details, interaction history | [Automated query] | [X business days] |
| [Loan Origination System] | [Credit / Lending] | Application data, credit assessments | [IT request] | [X business days] |
| [HR / Payroll System] | [Human Resources] | Employee personal data, payroll records | [HR query] | [X business days] |
| [Document Management System] | [Operations] | Scanned documents, contracts | [Document search] | [X business days] |
| [Email Archive] | [IT] | Correspondence containing personal data | [IT eDiscovery] | [X business days] |
| [Physical File Archives] | [Records Management / Branch] | Physical documents | [Manual search] | [X business days] |
| [CCTV / Surveillance] | [Security / Facilities] | Biometric / image data | [Security team retrieval] | [X business days] |
| [Third-Party Processors — list each] | [Relevant business unit] | [Data categories] | [Processor request per SLA] | [Per contract SLA] |
| [Other systems as applicable] | [Custodian] | [Categories] | [Method] | [Time] |

> **Note:** The System Inventory above must be reviewed annually and updated whenever a new system is introduced or decommissioned. The DPO is responsible for maintaining this inventory.

### 7.2 Search Initiation and Coordination

Upon confirmation of requestor identity (Section 5), the DPO shall:

1. Issue a **DSAR Search Notice** to the Data Custodian of each relevant system (template at **Appendix D**), specifying:
   - DSAR reference number and response deadline
   - Name and identity of the data subject
   - Search parameters (date range, account numbers, or other identifiers as provided)
   - Required output format
   - Internal deadline for search results (target: **Day 10**)

2. Receive and collate search results from all Data Custodians

3. Where a third-party data processor is involved, the DPO shall formally request data retrieval under the processor agreement, citing the DSAR reference and deadline

### 7.3 Search Methodology Standards

All searches shall be conducted to the following minimum standard:

- **Accuracy:** Searches must use all available identifiers for the data subject (full name, NRIC, account numbers, email address, phone number, and any aliases known to [Organization Name])
- **Completeness:** Both structured data (databases) and unstructured data (emails, documents, scanned files) must be searched
- **Physical Records:** Physical filing systems must be searched by the designated records custodian using a documented search protocol
- **Date Range:** Unless otherwise specified by the requestor, search shall encompass all personal data held at the time of the request without a default date limitation, subject to the organisation's data retention policy
- **Documentation:** Each Data Custodian must return search results with a signed **Search Completion Certificate** (Appendix D) confirming the systems searched, search methodology used, and confirming whether personal data was or was not located

### 7.4 Data Processor Obligations

[Organization Name] shall include contractual provisions in all data processing agreements requiring data processors to:
- Assist [Organization Name] in responding to DSARs within agreed timeframes
- Return responsive personal data in a structured, machine-readable format where practicable
- Confirm in writing whether they hold responsive personal data, even if the result is nil

## 8. Review for Exemptions

*PDPA s.32 provides specific grounds upon which [Organization Name] may limit or refuse access to personal data. This review must be conducted for every DSAR before a response is issued. All exemption decisions must be documented, legally reviewed, and proportionate.*

### 8.1 Exemption Review Process

The DPO shall conduct a formal exemption review for each DSAR after search results are received. The exemption review shall:

1. Be documented using the **DSAR Exemption Review Checklist** (Appendix E)
2. Involve consultation with [Legal Counsel] where any exemption ground is identified
3. Apply the principle of **proportionality** — exemptions shall only be applied to the extent strictly necessary; partial disclosure must be maximised where possible
4. Result in a written determination documented in the DSAR Log

### 8.2 Exemption Grounds under PDPA s.32

| Exemption Ground | PDPA Reference | Description | Approval Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| **National Security** | s.32(1)(a) | Data access would be prejudicial to the security of Malaysia | [Legal Counsel] + [Chief Risk Officer] |
| **Prevention, Detection, or Investigation of Crime** | s.32(1)(b) | Disclosure would prejudice the prevention, detection, investigation, or prosecution of any offence | [Legal Counsel] + [Compliance Officer] |
| **Assessment or Collection of Tax or Duty** | s.32(1)(c) | Disclosure would prejudice tax or duty assessment or collection | [Legal Counsel] |
| **Estimation of Government Revenue or Expenditure** | s.32(1)(d) | Disclosure would prejudice government financial functions | [Legal Counsel] |
| **Ongoing Negotiations** | s.32(1)(e) | Data consists of records of the data controller's intentions in relation to negotiations being carried on | [Legal Counsel] |
| **Legal Privilege** | s.32(1)(f) | Data consists of information in respect of which legal professional privilege could be maintained in legal proceedings | [Legal Counsel] |
| **Third-Party Rights** | s.32(1)(g) | Disclosure would involve disclosing information relating to another individual who has not consented and it is not reasonable to disclose without consent | [DPO] |
| **Confidential References** | s.32(1)(h) | Data consists of a confidential reference given to the data controller in connection with employment, supply of service, or education | [DPO] + [HR / Relevant Business Unit] |
| **Regulatory Functions** | s.32(1)(i) | Disclosure would be likely to prejudice the discharge of regulatory or supervisory functions | [Legal Counsel] + [Compliance Officer] |

### 8.3 Partial Disclosure

Where an exemption applies to only part of the responsive personal data, [Organization Name] shall:

- Redact or withhold only the specific data to which the exemption applies
- Provide full disclosure of all remaining personal data
- Clearly indicate to the requestor that information has been withheld and the general category of the exemption relied upon (without disclosing information that would itself defeat the exemption)

### 8.4 Notification of Refusal

Where [Organization Name] refuses access to personal data (in whole or in part) on the basis of an exemption, written notice must be provided to the requestor under **PDPA s.32(2)** stating:

- That access is refused
- The general ground for refusal (referencing the applicable exemption category)
- The requestor's right to apply to the Personal Data Protection Commissioner for a review
- Contact details for the Commissioner's office

Template refusal letters are provided at **Appendix B**.

## 9. Format and Structure of Access Response

*This section prescribes the format and content of the access response provided to the data subject upon successful completion of the DSAR process. The response must be clear, complete, and in a format accessible to the requestor.*

### 9.1 Response Content Requirements

Every DSAR response must include the following:

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Cover Letter** | Formal letter referencing DSAR number, confirming processing of the request, and summarising the contents of the response package |
| **Personal Data Summary** | A structured summary of the categories of personal data held |
| **Source of Data** | Where personal data was obtained (e.g., directly from data subject, from credit reference agencies, from third parties) |
| **Purpose(s) of Processing** | The purposes for which personal data is being used, with reference to [Organization Name]'s Privacy Notice |
| **Recipients or Classes of Recipients** | Third parties or categories of third parties to whom personal data has been or may be disclosed |
| **Retention Period** | The period for which personal data is retained, or the criteria used to determine retention |
| **Data Subject Rights Notice** | A summary of the data subject's remaining rights under PDPA (correction, withdrawal of consent, etc.) |
| **Exemption Notice (if applicable)** | Notification of any information withheld and the general category of exemption invoked |

### 9.2 Format of Personal Data Disclosure

Personal data shall be provided in the following format(s), in order of preference:

1. **Secure electronic document** — Password-protected PDF or encrypted electronic file, delivered via [secure email / secure portal]
2. **Printed document** — Sent via registered mail to the requestor's verified address, where electronic delivery is not possible or requested
3. **In-person collection** — Available upon prior arrangement with the DPO, with identity re-verification upon collection

> **Security Requirement:** Personal data shall **never** be transmitted via unencrypted email or unsecured channels. All electronic responses must use [organization's approved encryption standard — e.g., AES-256]. Passwords for encrypted files shall be communicated to the requestor via a separate channel (e.g., SMS to verified mobile number).

The disclosure package shall be structured as follows:

**Section 3: Data Sources**
*[List all sources from which personal data was originally collected]*

**Section 4: Processing Purposes**
*[Cross-reference to [Organization Name]'s Privacy Notice, with specific purposes relevant to the data subject's relationship with [Organization Name]]*

### 9.4 Quality Assurance Before Dispatch

Before dispatching the response, the DPO shall complete the following checks:

- [ ] All data subject identifiers verified and matched (no data belonging to another individual included)
- [ ] All responsive systems searched and results collated
- [ ] Exemption review completed and documented

*This section defines escalation paths for complex, disputed, or time-sensitive DSARs, and the process for handling complaints from data subjects who are dissatisfied with [Organization Name]'s response.*

### 13.1 Internal Escalation Triggers

| Trigger | Escalation Path | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Acknowledgement not issued within 24 hours | DPO → Head of Compliance | Immediate |
| Response Deadline within 3 days and response not ready | DPO → Head of Compliance → CCO | Immediate |
| Suspected fraudulent DSAR | DPO → Legal → [Fraud / Security team] | Within 4 hours |
| DSAR reveals possible data breach | DPO → [CISO / Incident Response team] | Within 2 hours |
| DSAR involves senior executive's personal data | DPO → CCO | Immediate — manage conflict of interest |
| Data subject threatens legal action or regulator complaint | DPO → Legal Counsel | Within 4 business hours |
| Data processor fails to supply data within agreed timeframe | DPO → [Vendor Manager / Legal] | Day 12 at latest |

### 13.2 Handling Complaints from Data Subjects

Where a data subject is dissatisfied with [Organization Name]'s response to their DSAR, [Organization Name] shall:

1. Acknowledge the complaint in writing within **[5] business days**
2. Assign a senior compliance officer (not the original DSAR Handler) to review the complaint
3. Conduct a full internal review within **[15] business days**
4. Provide a written resolution to the data subject
5. Where the complaint is upheld, issue a corrected or supplementary response as soon as practicable
6. Log the complaint in the DSAR Log and the compliance complaints register
7. Conduct a root cause analysis and implement corrective actions

### 13.3 Escalation to Personal Data Protection Commissioner

Data subjects have the right to complain directly to the **Personal Data Protection Commissioner** if they believe [Organization Name] has failed to comply with PDPA. [Organization Name] shall:
- Inform all requestors of this right in all DSAR responses (full disclosure and refusal)
- Cooperate fully and promptly with any inquiry from the Commissioner's office
- Notify [CCO / Legal] immediately upon receipt of any communication from the Commissioner relating to a DSAR
