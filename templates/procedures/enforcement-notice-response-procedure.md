## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This procedure establishes the structured response framework for [Organization Name] upon receipt of an Enforcement Notice issued by the Personal Data Protection Commissioner ("Commissioner") pursuant to Part IX of the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**. It defines the mandatory actions, timelines, governance escalations, legal consultation requirements, compliance remediation steps, appeal considerations, and reporting obligations that [Organization Name] must follow to ensure full and demonstrable compliance with any such notice.

This procedure operates under the authority of:

- **Section 110 PDPA** — Power of the Commissioner to issue enforcement notices requiring a data user to take specified remedial action within a prescribed period.
- **Section 111 PDPA** — Requirements as to enforcement notices, including the mandatory contents of a valid notice and the rights of the data user receiving such a notice.
- **Section 133 PDPA** — Offences and penalties applicable to non-compliance with an enforcement notice, including the risk of criminal liability for officers of a body corporate.

Failure to respond appropriately to an Enforcement Notice may constitute a criminal offence under s133 PDPA and may result in significant reputational, regulatory, and financial consequences for [Organization Name].

### 1.2 Scope

This procedure applies to:

- All Enforcement Notices received from the Commissioner under Part IX of the PDPA, whether issued as a result of a complaint, an audit finding, or the Commissioner's own initiative.
- All personnel of [Organization Name] involved in data protection, legal affairs, compliance, IT, operations, and senior management.
- All personal data processing activities conducted by [Organization Name] and its authorised data processors acting on [Organization Name]'s behalf.

This procedure does not apply to:

- General regulatory correspondence that does not constitute a formal Enforcement Notice under s110 PDPA.
- Information notices issued under s98 PDPA (which are governed by a separate procedure).
- Enforcement actions under other regulatory frameworks (e.g., BNM Guidelines, NACSA requirements) unless those actions also engage PDPA obligations.

### 1.3 Guiding Principles

[Organization Name] is committed to the following principles in responding to Enforcement Notices:

- **Good faith compliance** — [Organization Name] will assess every Enforcement Notice seriously and act in good faith to remediate identified deficiencies within the prescribed timeframe.
- **Legal privilege preservation** — All communications with external legal counsel relating to an Enforcement Notice shall be managed to preserve legal professional privilege where applicable.
- **Board-level accountability** — Receipt of an Enforcement Notice is a material regulatory event and shall be escalated to the Board or its designated committee within [X] business days.
- **Transparency with the Commissioner** — [Organization Name] will maintain open, accurate, and timely communication with the Commissioner throughout the compliance period.

## 6. Assessment of Appeal Grounds

### 6.1 Overview of Appeal Rights

*This section must be completed by legal counsel. Under ss83–100 PDPA, a data user has the right to appeal an Enforcement Notice to the Appeal Tribunal. The decision to appeal must be made carefully, as it does not automatically suspend the obligation to comply with the notice unless a stay is granted.*

The data user has a statutory right to appeal an Enforcement Notice to the **Personal Data Protection Appeal Tribunal** under **Part X of the PDPA**. Legal counsel must advise on:

1. Whether grounds for appeal exist.
2. The deadline for filing an appeal (typically within [X] days of the notice — confirm per current PDPA Rules).
3. Whether to apply for a **stay of the Enforcement Notice** pending the appeal.
4. The risk of non-compliance during any appeal proceedings.

> **Important:** Filing an appeal does **not** automatically suspend the obligation to comply with the Enforcement Notice. [Organization Name] must continue to work toward compliance unless a formal stay is granted by the Tribunal.

*The Evidence Package is [Organization Name]'s formal demonstration to the Commissioner that all requirements of the Enforcement Notice have been fulfilled. ### 10.2 Pre-Filing Preparation

### 10.3 Application for Stay of Enforcement Notice

Where [Organization Name] requires a suspension of the obligations under the Enforcement Notice pending the appeal outcome, legal counsel shall concurrently apply to the Appeal Tribunal for a **stay of the Enforcement Notice**. The application must demonstrate:

- A serious question to be tried (arguable appeal grounds).
- That the balance of convenience favours a stay.
- That damages or compliance costs would not be an adequate remedy in the absence of a stay.

> If a stay is **not** granted, [Organization Name] must continue to pursue compliance in parallel with the appeal proceedings.

### 10.4 Conduct of Appeal Proceedings

| Phase | Description | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Filing | Memorandum of Appeal filed with Tribunal | External Counsel |

| Evidence Exchange | Parties exchange witness statements and documents | External Counsel + DPO |
| Hearing | Oral hearing before Tribunal (if required) | External Counsel |
| Decision | Tribunal issues written decision | Tribunal |

### 10.5 Post-Appeal Actions

| Outcome | Action Required |
|---|---|

| Appeal dismissed — notice upheld | Immediately activate or resume Compliance Roadmap; comply within any revised deadline; notify Board |
