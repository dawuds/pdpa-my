## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This procedure establishes a structured, legally sound, and operationally consistent framework for [Organization Name] to respond to inspections, audits, and investigations conducted by the Department of Personal Data Protection (Jabatan Perlindungan Data Peribadi — **JPDP**) and the Personal Data Protection Commissioner (**the Commissioner**) pursuant to **Part VIII (Enforcement) of the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA 2010)**, specifically under sections 101 through 105.

The procedure ensures that [Organization Name]:

- Responds promptly and lawfully to all JPDP inspection notices and demands;
- Preserves relevant documents, records, and systems to prevent spoliation;
- Coordinates internal stakeholders — including the Data Protection Officer (DPO), senior management, and Legal Counsel — in a manner that is orderly and legally defensible;
- Fulfils its statutory obligations to cooperate with the Commissioner without compromising applicable legal privileges or the rights of its personnel; and
- Implements timely remediation actions following the conclusion of any inspection or investigation.

### 1.2 Scope

This procedure applies to:

- **All departments and business units** of [Organization Name] that process personal data in the course of commercial transactions;
- **All employees, contractors, and third-party data processors** engaged by [Organization Name] who may be subject to, or involved in supporting, a JPDP inspection or investigation;
- **All forms of JPDP enforcement action** under PDPA Part VIII, including but not limited to routine compliance inspections, targeted investigations following a complaint, and Commissioner-directed audits; and
- **All categories of personal data** processed by [Organization Name], whether held in physical records, digital systems, cloud environments, or third-party data processing arrangements.

This procedure does not supersede any obligation imposed directly by the PDPA 2010, regulations made thereunder, or any lawful direction issued by the Commissioner. In the event of conflict between this procedure and a statutory obligation, the statutory obligation prevails.

## 6. Document and System Preservation

*This section describes [Organization Name]'s obligations and internal controls to prevent the destruction, alteration, or concealment of documents and data that may be relevant to a JPDP inspection. A Legal Hold must be treated as a mandatory operational directive. Authors should align this section with the organization's existing records management and information governance policies.*

### 6.1 Legal Hold Activation

Upon notification from the DPO or Legal Counsel, a **Legal Hold** is immediately in force. The Legal Hold supersedes all routine document retention and destruction schedules with respect to materials within its scope.

The Legal Hold Notice must be issued in writing (see **Appendix A — Legal Hold Notice Template**) and must specify:

- The scope of records and data subject to the hold;
- The relevant JPDP inspection or investigation reference;
- The custodians responsible for preserving identified materials;
- The prohibition on destruction, alteration, or transfer of materials;
- The reporting obligation for any inadvertent destruction discovered after the hold is issued.

### 6.2 Scope of Preservation

| Category | Examples | Custodian |
|---|---|---|
| Personal data records | Customer databases, employee records, data subject files | Head of IT / Business Unit Heads |
| Data processing agreements | Contracts with data processors and sub-processors | Head of Legal / Procurement |
| Privacy notices and consent records | Website privacy notices, consent logs, opt-out records | DPO / Marketing / Digital |
| Incident and breach records | Incident logs, breach notifications, JPDP correspondence | DPO / Head of Information Security |
| Internal policies and procedures | Data protection policy, this procedure, DPIA records | DPO |
| IT system logs | Access logs, audit trails, system configuration records | Head of IT / CISO |
| Email and electronic communications | Relevant correspondence relating to data processing | IT / Legal |
| Physical records | Printed customer files, signed consent forms, physical data maps | Relevant Business Unit Heads |
| Training records | Staff data protection training logs and completion records | HR / DPO |

### 6.3 System Preservation Controls

The Head of IT / CISO must, within **[X] hours** of Legal Hold activation:

- [ ] Suspend any scheduled deletion, purging, or archiving jobs affecting in-scope data;
- [ ] Disable automatic log rotation or overwrite functions for relevant audit trail systems;
- [ ] Take forensic snapshots or backup copies of in-scope databases and system states where technically feasible;
- [ ] Document all preservation actions taken, with timestamps and responsible individuals; and
- [ ] Confirm to the DPO in writing that preservation controls are in place.

### 6.4 Prohibition on Spoliation

Any employee who destroys, alters, conceals, or fails to preserve documents or data subject to a Legal Hold — whether intentionally or negligently — may be subject to:

- Disciplinary action up to and including summary dismissal under [Organization Name]'s HR disciplinary policy;
- Personal criminal liability under s105 of the PDPA 2010; and
- Adverse inference in any enforcement proceedings before the Commissioner.

All employees must immediately report to the DPO any knowledge of destruction or alteration of records that may be relevant to the inspection.

### 8.2 Document Production Log

| Field | Description |
|---|---|

### 8.3 Privilege Review Protocol

Legal Counsel must conduct a privilege review of all documents before production. The following categories of documents are presumptively subject to legal professional privilege and must not be produced without explicit Legal Counsel authorisation:

- Communications between [Organization Name] and its Legal Counsel (internal or external) made for the dominant purpose of obtaining or giving legal advice;
- Documents prepared in contemplation of litigation or enforcement proceedings;
- Counsel's notes, memoranda, and working papers prepared in connection with the inspection response.

Where [Organization Name] withholds a document on grounds of privilege, the DPO and Legal Counsel must:

1. Notify JPDP that a document has been withheld and the basis for withholding (without disclosing the privileged content);
2. Log the withheld document in the Document Production Register with status "Withheld — Legal Privilege"; and
3. Prepare, if required, a privilege log for submission to JPDP or the Commissioner.

## 9. Staff Interview Preparation and Legal Rights Briefing

*This section outlines [Organization Name]'s obligations and internal procedures when JPDP officers seek to interview or take statements from employees during an inspection. Employees have legal rights and [Organization Name] must ensure they are informed of those rights without obstructing the inspection. Authors should obtain legal advice on the current state of applicable law before finalising this section.*

### 9.1 Interview Coordination

When JPDP officers request to interview an employee:

1. The Designated Liaison must be notified immediately.
2. Legal Counsel must be consulted before any interview proceeds.
3. [Organization Name] will request that interviews be **scheduled in advance** where operationally feasible, to allow for adequate preparation.
4. No interview should proceed without the employee being given an opportunity to receive a **pre-interview briefing** from Legal Counsel or an HR representative.
5. [Organization Name] may request that Legal Counsel or an HR representative be present during the interview, subject to the agreement of JPDP officers and applicable statutory provisions.

### 9.2 Pre-Interview Briefing for Employees

Legal Counsel or the DPO must conduct a pre-interview briefing for any employee scheduled to be interviewed. The briefing must cover:

| Topic | Key Points |
|---|---|
| **Nature of the inspection** | General context of the JPDP inspection without disclosing legally privileged strategy |
| **Obligation to cooperate** | Employees are generally obliged to answer questions truthfully; obstruction is an offence under s105 |
| **Right to legal representation** | Employees should be informed of their right to seek independent legal advice |
| **Scope limitation** | Employees should only answer questions within their personal knowledge and role — speculate on nothing |
| **Confidentiality of proceedings** | The inspection is confidential; employees must not discuss it externally or on social media |
| **Document requests** | If asked to produce documents, refer the request to the Designated Liaison |
| **Privilege** | Do not disclose communications with Legal Counsel — flag any such questions to Legal Counsel |
| **Post-interview debrief** | Employees must report the substance of the interview to Legal Counsel immediately after |

### 9.3 Post-Interview Documentation

Following each interview:

- Legal Counsel must prepare a **confidential memorandum** recording the substance of the interview, to be treated as privileged attorney work product;
- The Designated Liaison must update the **Inspection Activity Log** with the date, time, employee interviewed, JPDP officer conducting the interview, and duration;
- Any commitments made by the employee during the interview must be reviewed by Legal Counsel for compliance risk.

### 9.4 Employee Welfare

[Organization Name] recognises that regulatory inspections may cause stress for employees involved. The DPO and HR must:

- Communicate clearly to employees that [Organization Name] will provide legal support;
- Ensure no employee faces retaliation, adverse performance consequences, or informal pressure as a result of their good-faith participation in a JPDP inspection; and
- Refer employees to the [Employee Assistance Programme / HR Support Line] if required.

### 10.1 Types of Information Requests

| Request Type | Statutory Basis | Typical Timeframe | Response Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| On-site document production during inspection | s103, s104 | Immediate / as directed by inspector | Physical or electronic copies |
| Written request for information (post-inspection or standalone) | s103 | As specified in the request (typically [X] days) | Written submission |
| Request for records of processing activities | s103 | As specified in the request | Structured data inventory or RoPA extract |

### 10.2 Timeframe Management Process

Upon receipt of any written information request from JPDP:

1. **Log the request** in the **JPDP Request Tracker** (see **Appendix D — JPDP Request Tracker Template**) with the date of receipt and deadline.
2. **Immediately notify** the DPO and Legal Counsel.
3. **Assess the deadline** — if the stated deadline is insufficient for proper compliance, Legal Counsel must contact JPDP promptly to request an extension, with written reasons.
4. **Assign a response coordinator** from the relevant business unit(s) to collect responsive materials.
5. **Conduct privilege review** before any information is submitted (see Section 8.3).
6. **Obtain DPO and Legal Counsel sign-off** on the final response before submission.
7. **Retain a copy** of all submissions in the JPDP inspection file.

### 10.4 Extension Requests

If [Organization Name] requires additional time to respond:

- The extension request must state the reason for the additional time required and the proposed revised deadline;

- [ ] Legal Counsel has reviewed and approved the submission
- [ ] A copy of the submission is retained in the JPDP inspection file
- [ ] Submission is delivered by the required method (e.g., registered post, secure email, hand delivery) and receipt is confirmed

## 11. Post-Inspection Remediation and Follow-Up Reporting

*This section describes [Organization Name]'s obligations and internal process for addressing findings arising from a JPDP inspection, implementing remediation actions, and reporting on outcomes to senior management and regulatory authorities. A structured and timely remediation response is critical to demonstrating good faith compliance to the Commissioner. Authors should align remediation timelines with [Organization Name]'s risk management and audit follow-up frameworks.*

### 11.1 Inspection Outcome Review

Following the conclusion of a JPDP inspection — including receipt of any inspection report, findings letter, or Commissioner's direction — the DPO must convene a **Post-Inspection Review Meeting** within **[X] business days**. Attendees must include:

- [ ] DPO and Deputy DPO
- [ ] Legal Counsel (internal and/or external)
- [ ] Chief Risk Officer / Head of Compliance
- [ ] Relevant Business Unit Heads
- [ ] Head of IT / CISO (where applicable)
- [ ] Head of HR (where applicable)

The meeting must:

1. Review the full inspection report and findings;
2. Categorise each finding by severity (Critical / High / Medium / Low);
3. Assign a remediation owner to each finding;
4. Establish a remediation timeline for each finding; and
5. Confirm the process for reporting progress to senior management and, where required, to the Commissioner.

### 11.2 Remediation Plan

The DPO must prepare a **Remediation Plan** within **[X] business days** of the Post-Inspection Review Meeting. The Remediation Plan must include:

| Finding Ref | JPDP Finding Description | Severity | Root Cause | Corrective Action | Preventive Action | Owner | Target Completion Date | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [F-001] | [Description of finding] | [Critical/High/Medium/Low] | [Root cause analysis] | [Specific corrective action] | [Preventive measure] | [Name / Role] | [DD/MM/YYYY] | [Not Started / In Progress / Complete] |
| [F-002] | [Description of finding] | [Critical/High/Medium/Low] | [Root cause analysis] | [Specific corrective action] | [Preventive measure] | [Name / Role] | [DD/MM/YYYY] | [Not Started / In Progress / Complete] |

### 11.3 Regulatory Submissions

Where the Commissioner issues a formal direction or requires [Organization Name] to submit a remediation plan or progress report:

- Legal Counsel must advise on the specific requirements of the direction;
- The DPO must coordinate the preparation of the required submission;
- All submissions to the Commissioner must be reviewed and approved by the DPO and Legal Counsel before dispatch; and
- Copies of all regulatory submissions must be retained in the JPDP inspection file.

### 11.4 Senior Management Reporting

The DPO must prepare a **Post-Inspection Management Report** for presentation to the Board Risk Committee / Senior Management Committee within **[X] weeks** of the inspection conclusion. The report must include:

- [ ] Executive summary of the inspection and its regulatory context
- [ ] Summary of JPDP findings and overall compliance rating (if provided)
- [ ] Remediation Plan summary with key actions, owners, and timelines
- [ ] Resource requirements for remediation (budget, personnel, technology)
- [ ] Assessment of residual risk and potential enforcement consequences if remediation is not timely
- [ ] Lessons learned and systemic improvements to the data protection compliance programme

### 11.5 Lessons Learned and Policy Update

Following completion of remediation:

- The DPO must conduct a **Lessons Learned Review** to identify systemic weaknesses in [Organization Name]'s data protection programme;
- This procedure, and all related policies, must be reviewed and updated to reflect findings and improvements;
- Updated training materials must be developed and delivered to relevant staff; and
- The DPO must confirm closure of all findings to senior management and, where required, to the Commissioner.
