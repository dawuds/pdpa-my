## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This document serves as the official Training Attendance Record for [Organization Name]'s data protection awareness and training programme. It documents all attendance, completion, and assessment outcomes for training sessions conducted in fulfilment of the organization's obligations under the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**, specifically **Section 9 (General Principle)**, which requires that personal data is processed in a manner consistent with the rights of data subjects, and that staff handling personal data are adequately trained and aware of their obligations.

These records provide auditable evidence that [Organization Name] maintains an active and ongoing staff awareness programme as part of its overall data protection compliance posture.

### 1.2 Scope

This record covers:

- All mandatory data protection training sessions conducted for employees of [Organization Name]
- Classroom-based, e-learning (LMS), and blended training modalities
- Induction training for new hires and refresher training for existing staff
- Role-specific and specialist data protection training
- Third-party and contractor data protection awareness sessions, where applicable

**In Scope:** All employees, contractors, and third parties with access to personal data processed by [Organization Name].

**Out of Scope:** General IT security training not directly related to personal data handling obligations.

### 1.3 Regulatory Basis

| Regulation | Clause / Section | Requirement Summary |
|---|---|---|
| Personal Data Protection Act 2010 | Section 9 — General Principle | Personal data shall be processed in accordance with the rights of data subjects; organisations must demonstrate staff awareness and competency |
| Personal Data Protection Act 2010 | Section 3 — Application | Applies to any person who processes personal data in connection with commercial transactions |
| PDPA Personal Data Protection Standard | Principle 7 — Security | Data processors must take practical steps to protect personal data, including training of personnel |
