## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Cross-Border Personal Data Transfer Register ("the Register") establishes a centralized, auditable record of all transfers of personal data by [Organization Name] to recipients located outside Malaysia. It is maintained to demonstrate ongoing compliance with Section 129A of the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA) (as amended), which imposes obligations on data processors and data users to ensure that personal data transferred internationally is afforded a standard of protection equivalent to that provided under Malaysian law.

The Register enables [Organization Name] to:

- Evidence the lawful basis and safeguards in place for each cross-border transfer;
- Track Transfer Impact Assessments (TIAs) and their outcomes;
- Maintain current contractual and regulatory documentation references;
- Facilitate timely review upon changes to transfer arrangements, recipient jurisdictions, or applicable law;
- Support regulatory examination by the Personal Data Protection Commissioner ("the Commissioner") and internal or external auditors.

### 1.2 Scope

This Register covers **all transfers of personal data** originating from [Organization Name]'s processing activities in Malaysia to any recipient entity, system, or infrastructure located in a foreign jurisdiction. This includes but is not limited to:

- Transfers to third-party processors or sub-processors domiciled abroad;
- Transfers to affiliated entities (subsidiaries, parent companies, joint ventures) outside Malaysia;
- Transfers facilitated through cloud computing services where data is stored or processed in foreign data centres;
- Transfers arising from cross-border financial messaging, correspondent banking arrangements, or regulatory reporting to foreign authorities;
- Transfers made pursuant to outsourcing arrangements governed by BNM outsourcing policy requirements.

*This Register does not apply to the transit of personal data through foreign infrastructure where the data is not accessible to or processed by any party in that jurisdiction. Confirm applicability of transit scenarios with the DPO on a case-by-case basis.*

**Out of Scope:** Transfers of anonymised or aggregated data where no re-identification risk exists, subject to a documented anonymisation assessment.

### Appendix B — Approved Transfer Mechanism Definitions

*Reference definitions for transfer mechanisms referenced in Section 4.5, consistent with PDPA s129A and Commissioner guidance.*

| Mechanism | Definition | Conditions for Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Adequacy Designation** | The Commissioner has determined that the recipient country provides an adequate level of data protection | Confirm current adequacy status on Commissioner's published list prior to reliance |
| **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCC)** | Standardised contractual terms approved or recognised by the Commissioner that impose binding obligations on the recipient | Must incorporate Commissioner-approved modules; TIA required where adequacy not established |
| **Binding Corporate Rules (BCR)** | Legally binding internal rules adopted by a corporate group, approved by the Commissioner or a recognised authority | Available only for intra-group transfers; approval must be current |
| **Explicit Consent** | The data subject has given specific, informed, unambiguous, and freely given consent to the transfer | Consent must meet PDPA Notice and Choice Principle requirements; cannot be default or bundled |
| **Contractual Necessity** | Transfer is necessary for performance of a contract between the data subject and the data user, or for pre-contractual steps at the data subject's request | Necessity must be genuine and documented; cannot be relied upon for data user convenience |
| **Vital Interests** | Transfer necessary to protect the vital interests of the data subject | Narrow exception; document compelling circumstances and inability to obtain consent |
| **Commissioner Approval** | Specific approval granted by the Commissioner for the transfer | Application must be submitted with supporting documentation; approval is transfer-specific |
