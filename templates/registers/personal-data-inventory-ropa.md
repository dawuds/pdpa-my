## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Record of Processing Activities (ROPA) serves as the master compliance register for [Organization Name] documenting all personal data processing activities conducted across the organization. It is maintained pursuant to the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA) and demonstrates [Organization Name]'s accountability in managing personal data in accordance with all seven PDPA personal data protection principles.

This document enables [Organization Name] to:

- Demonstrate compliance with PDPA obligations to the Personal Data Protection Commissioner (PDPC) and relevant regulators
- Identify and manage data protection risks across all processing activities
- Provide a central reference for responding to data subject access requests under s11 PDPA
- Support Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) and incident response activities
- Evidence the application of Privacy by Design across organizational processes

### 1.2 Scope

This ROPA covers **all personal data processing activities** conducted by [Organization Name], including:

- Processing carried out by [Organization Name] as a **data controller** (as defined under s4 PDPA)
- Processing carried out by **third-party data processors** acting on behalf of [Organization Name]
- Processing of personal data belonging to **customers, employees, vendors, contractors, and other data subjects**
- Processing activities across all **business units, departments, and subsidiaries** of [Organization Name]
- **Manual and automated** processing of personal data in any format

This ROPA does **not** cover:

- Personal data processed entirely for personal or household purposes
- Personal data processed by government authorities under separate statutory regimes

### 1.3 Regulatory Basis

*This section identifies the specific legal provisions that mandate and govern the maintenance of this register.*

[Organization Name] is a **data controller** as defined under the PDPA and is registered under the data controller registration regime applicable to [relevant commercial sector]. The maintenance of this inventory is required to demonstrate compliance with the following PDPA principles:

| PDPA Section | Principle | Relevance to ROPA |
|---|---|---|
| s5 | General Principle | Documents lawful bases and conditions for processing |
| s6 | Notice and Choice Principle | Maps notice obligations against each processing activity |
| s7 | Disclosure Principle | Records authorized recipient categories and disclosure controls |
| s8 | Security Principle | Documents security measures applied per processing activity |
| s9 | Retention Principle | Records retention periods and disposal methods |
| s10 | Data Integrity Principle | Tracks data accuracy and update mechanisms |
| s11 | Access Principle | Supports data subject access request fulfilment |
| s12 | Transfer Limitation | Documents cross-border transfer safeguards |

## 6. Maintenance and Review Procedure

### 6.1 Ongoing Maintenance

*This ROPA is a living document and must be updated promptly whenever changes to processing activities occur.*

The following events shall trigger an immediate update to this ROPA:

- Introduction of a **new processing activity** or system that processes personal data
- **Significant change** to the purpose, scope, data categories, or recipients for an existing processing activity
- Engagement of a **new data processor** or termination of an existing processor relationship
- Introduction of **cross-border data transfers** not previously documented
- Change to **retention periods** or disposal methods
- Identification of a **material security vulnerability** or data breach affecting a documented processing activity
- Change in **applicable law or regulatory requirements**
- **Retirement or cessation** of a processing activity

### 6.2 Scheduled Review

| Review Type | Frequency | Trigger | Responsible Party | Review Record |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Full ROPA Review | Quarterly | Calendar — [Month/Year] | DPO + Business Unit Data Owners | Review completion sign-off |
| Processing Activity Spot Check | Monthly | Random selection of 20% of activities | DPO | Spot check log |
| Annual Comprehensive Audit | Annually | [Month each year] | DPO + Internal Audit | Audit report |
| Regulatory Filing Review | As required | Regulatory change or PDPC enquiry | DPO + Legal | Correspondence file |

### 6.3 Change Management

All material changes to this ROPA shall be:

1. Documented in the Version History table in Section 7
2. Reviewed by the DPO prior to finalisation
3. Communicated to relevant Business Unit Data Owners
4. Assessed for privacy notice update requirements under s6 PDPA
5. Assessed for DPIA trigger under Section 4.10 criteria
