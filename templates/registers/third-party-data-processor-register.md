## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This register establishes and maintains a comprehensive inventory of all third parties and data processors engaged by [Organization Name] to process personal data on its behalf. It serves as the authoritative record for demonstrating compliance with the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**, specifically the obligations of a data user when engaging data processors under **Section 9** and the enhanced processor accountability requirements under **Section 129A**.

The register supports [Organization Name]'s obligation to ensure that any processing of personal data by a third party is conducted only pursuant to a written contract, that adequate security safeguards are in place, and that personal data subjects' rights are not undermined through the engagement of processors.

### 1.2 Scope

This register applies to:

- All **data processors** engaged by [Organization Name] that process personal data on its behalf, whether wholly or partly
- All **sub-processors** engaged by primary data processors where [Organization Name] has granted written authorisation
- All **joint data users** where two or more parties jointly determine the purposes and means of processing personal data
- Processing activities carried out within Malaysia and any cross-border transfers of personal data to processors located outside Malaysia
- All personal data categories as defined under the PDPA, including sensitive personal data

This register does **not** apply to:

- Third-party vendors who access [Organization Name]'s premises but do not process personal data
- Regulatory bodies and law enforcement agencies acting under statutory authority
- Intra-group entities covered under a separate Intra-Group Data Sharing Agreement register

### 1.3 Regulatory Basis

| Regulatory Instrument | Provision | Obligation |
|---|---|---|
| Personal Data Protection Act 2010 | Section 9 | Data processors must process personal data only under a written contract; data users remain responsible for processor compliance |
| Personal Data Protection Act 2010 | Section 129A | Enhanced obligations for data users engaging data processors; mandatory contractual requirements and oversight |
| PDPA (Amendment) Act 2023 | Relevant Amendments | Updated processor obligations and accountability requirements |
| Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Policy Document on Outsourcing | As applicable | Outsourcing risk management for licensed financial institutions |

## 2. Definitions

*This section defines key terms used throughout this register. Amend definitions to align with [Organization Name]'s internal data governance glossary where applicable.*

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Data User** | [Organization Name], which processes personal data in the course of its commercial transaction |
| **Data Processor** | Any person who processes personal data on behalf of [Organization Name] and does not process personal data for any of their own purposes |
| **Sub-Processor** | A third party engaged by a data processor to carry out specific processing activities on behalf of the data processor in relation to [Organization Name]'s personal data |
| **Joint Data User** | Any person who, together with [Organization Name], jointly and collectively determines the purposes and means of processing personal data |
| **Personal Data** | Any information in respect of commercial transactions that relates directly or indirectly to a data subject, who is identified or identifiable from that information |
| **Sensitive Personal Data** | Personal data consisting of information as to the physical or mental health or condition of a data subject, political opinions, religious beliefs, and other categories as defined under the PDPA |
| **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** | A written contract between [Organization Name] and a data processor governing the terms of processing personal data, as required under Section 9 and Section 129A of the PDPA |
| **Cross-Border Transfer** | The transfer of personal data outside Malaysia to a place specified under the PDPA (Amendment) Act 2023 or pursuant to an approved transfer mechanism |
| **Due Diligence Assessment** | A structured evaluation of a data processor's technical, organisational, legal, and security capabilities prior to engagement and on an ongoing basis |

1. **Processing Instructions** — The processor shall only process personal data on documented instructions from [Organization Name] and shall immediately inform [Organization Name] if an instruction infringes applicable data protection laws
2. **Confidentiality** — All persons authorised to process the personal data are subject to a duty of confidentiality
3. **Security Measures** — The processor shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk
4. **Sub-Processing** — The processor shall not engage another processor without prior specific or general written authorisation from [Organization Name]
5. **Data Subject Rights Assistance** — The processor shall assist [Organization Name] with fulfilling its obligations to respond to requests from data subjects
6. **Breach Notification Assistance** — The processor shall notify [Organization Name] without undue delay after becoming aware of a personal data breach
7. **Deletion or Return** — Upon termination, the processor shall delete or return all personal data to [Organization Name]
8. **Audit Rights** — The processor shall make available to [Organization Name] all information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow for audits

### Appendix B — Due Diligence Assessment Framework

*This appendix outlines the assessment domains and minimum requirements for due diligence assessments. Full assessment methodology is documented in the Due Diligence Assessment Procedure [Document Reference].*

| Domain | Minimum Assessment Requirements |
|---|---|
| **Technical Security Controls** | Encryption at rest and in transit; access control mechanisms; vulnerability management programme; penetration testing frequency |
| **Organisational Security Controls** | Security awareness training; personnel security screening; incident response procedures; clean desk policy |
| **Legal and Regulatory Compliance** | Applicable licences and registrations; history of regulatory enforcement actions; privacy policy and practices review |
| **Sub-Processor Management** | Register of approved sub-processors; sub-processor contractual obligations; flow-down clauses |
| **Incident and Breach Management** | Breach detection and response capabilities; notification procedures and timelines; breach history |
| **Business Continuity** | BCP and DR plan existence and testing frequency; recovery time and recovery point objectives; redundancy measures |
| **Financial Stability** | Latest audited financial statements; credit rating (if applicable); business continuity implications of financial distress |

**Risk Rating Guidance:**

| Rating | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Low** | All assessment domains satisfactory; no outstanding remediation items; valid security certifications in place |
| **Medium** | Minor deficiencies in one or more domains; remediation plan accepted; security certifications current or minor gaps |
| **High** | Significant deficiencies identified; critical controls absent or ineffective; remediation required before continued engagement |
