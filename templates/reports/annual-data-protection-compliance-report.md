## 2. Purpose and Scope

*Define the purpose of this document, the organisational scope it covers, and any explicit exclusions. Ensure the scope statement aligns with the organisation's registered processing activities and data inventory.*

### 2.1 Purpose

This Annual Data Protection Compliance Report is prepared by the **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** of [Organisation Name] and is presented to the **Board of Directors** and **Senior Management** on an annual basis. The purpose of this report is to:

1. Provide the Board and Senior Management with a comprehensive and structured overview of [Organisation Name]'s compliance status with the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)** of Malaysia and its subsidiary legislation.
2. Document the status of obligations arising from the **seven data protection principles** established under the PDPA, with particular focus on **Section 5 (General Principle)** governing the processing of personal data and **Section 9 (Retention Principle)** governing data retention practices.
3. Record and analyse data breach incidents, data subject rights requests, training outcomes, and audit findings for the reporting period.
4. Identify compliance gaps, risks, and areas requiring improvement, and present a forward-looking improvement plan for the coming year.
5. Fulfil the DPO's reporting obligations to the Board as defined in the organisation's **Data Protection Governance Framework** [Document Reference: [Document ID]].

### 2.2 Regulatory Basis

This report is prepared in accordance with, and addresses obligations arising from, the following regulatory instruments:

| Instrument | Relevance |
|---|---|

| Personal Data Protection Regulations 2013 | Processing standards and security measures |

| Personal Data Protection (Fees) Regulations 2013 | Registration and notification obligations |
| [Other applicable BNM policy documents / PDPA subsidiary legislation] | [Relevance] |

### 2.3 Scope

This report covers all personal data processing activities conducted by **[Organisation Name]** (hereinafter "the Organisation") during the reporting period **[DD Month YYYY] to [DD Month YYYY]**, including but not limited to:

- **Customer personal data** processed in connection with the provision of [banking / insurance / payment / financial] services, including onboarding, account management, credit assessment, and customer communications.

### Appendix H: Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Data User** | An organisation that processes personal data in connection with its business in Malaysia, as defined under PDPA s.4. |
| **Data Subject** | An individual who is the subject of personal data, as defined under PDPA s.4. |
| **Personal Data** | Information in respect of commercial transactions that is processed directly by a data user, as defined under PDPA s.4. |
| **Sensitive Personal Data** | Personal data consisting of information as to the physical or mental health or condition of a data subject; his political opinions; his religious beliefs or other beliefs of a similar nature; the commission or alleged commission of any offence; or any other personal data as the Minister may determine, as defined under PDPA s.40. |
| **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** | A contractual agreement between a data user and a third-party data processor establishing the terms under which personal data may be processed on behalf of the data user. |
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | The appointed officer responsible for overseeing [Organisation Name]'s PDPA compliance programme. |
| **DPIA** | Data Protection Impact Assessment — a systematic process for identifying and mitigating data protection risks associated with new or changed processing activities. |
| **DSR** | Data Subject Request — a request from a data subject to exercise their rights under the PDPA, including the right of access (s.30) and the right of correction (s.34). |
| **JPDP** | Jabatan Perlindungan Data Peribadi — the Department of Personal Data Protection, the regulatory authority responsible for enforcing the PDPA in Malaysia. |
| **RoPA** | Records of Processing Activities — a documented inventory of all personal data processing activities conducted by [Organisation Name], maintained in accordance with PDPA accountability obligations. |
| **RAG Rating** | Red / Amber / Green — a traffic-light rating system used to indicate compliance status or risk level. |
| **SLA** | Service Level Agreement — the internal target timeframe within which the organisation commits to fulfilling a given obligation, such as responding to a DSR. |
