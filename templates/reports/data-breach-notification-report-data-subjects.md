## 7. Protective Actions You Can Take

*Provide clear, actionable steps that data subjects can take to protect themselves. Use simple language and numbered instructions.*

We strongly encourage you to take the following precautionary steps immediately:

1. **Monitor your accounts** — Regularly review your bank statements, credit card transactions, and online accounts for any unauthorised activity.
2. **Change your passwords** — Update passwords for your [Organization Name] account and any other accounts where you use the same or similar credentials. Use a strong, unique password for each account.
3. **Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA)** — Where available, activate MFA on your accounts for an additional layer of security.
4. **Be vigilant against phishing** — Do not click on links or open attachments in unsolicited emails or messages purporting to be from [Organization Name]. We will never ask for your password or PIN via email or phone.
5. **Check your credit report** — Request a copy of your credit report from [e.g., CTOS / Experian Malaysia / RAM Credit Information (RAMCI)] to identify any suspicious credit applications made in your name.
6. **Report suspicious activity** — If you suspect your identity has been misused, file a report with the Royal Malaysia Police (PDRM) and notify your bank immediately.
7. **Use credit monitoring services** — [Organization Name] is offering affected customers [complimentary / subsidised] credit monitoring for a period of **[duration, e.g., 12 months]** through **[Service Provider Name]**. See Section 8 below for details.
