## 3. Assessment Scope and Methodology

*Define the boundaries of this assessment and the methods used to gather evidence. A clearly defined scope ensures the assessment is repeatable, consistent, and defensible during regulatory inquiries.*

### 3.1 Assessment Objectives

This assessment aims to:

1. Evaluate the Processor's compliance with applicable obligations under the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**, specifically regarding data processor accountability under **s.9** and third-party oversight requirements under **s.129A**.
2. Assess the Processor's adherence to contractual data protection obligations as specified in the executed Data Processing Agreement.
3. Review the Processor's information security controls, certifications, and security posture relative to the risk profile of data processed.
4. Identify compliance gaps, assess associated risks, and recommend remedial actions.
5. Document the Processor's management response and remediation commitments.

### 3.2 Assessment Scope

**In Scope:**

- All processing activities performed by the Processor involving personal data sourced from or on behalf of [Organization Name], as described in Section 2.4.
- Information security policies, controls, and certifications maintained by the Processor.
- Contractual compliance with the Data Processing Agreement (DPA) referenced in Section 2.3.
- Sub-processors engaged by the Processor in connection with the in-scope services.
- Cross-border transfers of personal data, if any, initiated by the Processor.

**Out of Scope:**

- Processing activities performed by the Processor for other clients unrelated to the Organization.
